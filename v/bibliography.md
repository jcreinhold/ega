with three additional Séminaire Bourbaki exposés and Mumford's *Lectures on curves on an algebraic surface*, both of
which are heavily cited in §V.5 and §V.6.

## Entries referenced in EGA V

This list extends the EGA IV bibliography (entries `[1]`-`[44]`). New entries `[45]`-`[52]` are introduced by EGA V; the
numbering continues the sequence used through EGA I-IV. Entries already in EGA IV (e.g. Bourbaki's *Algèbre
commutative*, Nagata's *Local Rings*, Mumford-Tate, Serre's *Faisceaux algébriques cohérents*) are not re-listed here;
their numbers in the EGA III/IV bibliographies remain authoritative.

`[45]` D. **Mumford**, _Lectures on curves on an algebraic surface_, Princeton Mathematical Notes **59**, Princeton
University Press, 1966.

`[46]` A. **Grothendieck**, _Techniques de construction en géométrie analytique_, Séminaire H. Cartan, **13e année:
1960/61**, exposés 7-17, École Normale Supérieure, 1962. (Cited as Sém. Bourbaki exposé 232 in some places; see also
`[47]`.)

`[47]` A. **Grothendieck**, _Techniques de construction et théorèmes d'existence en géométrie algébrique_, six Séminaire
Bourbaki exposés (190 — December 1959; 195 — May 1960; 212 — February 1961; 221 — May 1961; 232 — February 1962; 236 —
May 1962), reprinted in _Fondements de la Géométrie Algébrique_ (FGA), Secrétariat Mathématique, Paris, 1962. (Often
cited collectively as the "FGA descent theorems".)

`[48]` A. **Grothendieck**, _Techniques de construction de schémas_ (Sém. Bourbaki, exposé **261**, Mai 1963), reprinted
in FGA.

`[49]` A. **Seidenberg**, _The hyperplane sections of normal varieties_, Trans. Amer. Math. Soc. **69** (1950), pp.
357-386. — Cited in §V.5.5 ("Seidenberg-style theorems").

`[50]` O. **Zariski**, _The theorem of Bertini on the variable singular points of a linear system of varieties_, Trans.
Amer. Math. Soc. **56** (1944), pp. 130-140. — Cited at §V.5.3 (Bertini-Zariski theorem).

`[51]` M. **Artin**, _Elementary morphisms and Henselization_, several Séminaire Bourbaki and Harvard preprints,
1962-1968. — Cited in the §V.5.13 sommaire entry ("Elementary morphisms and a theorem of M. Artin"), though no full
prenote of §V.5.13 survives.

`[52]` J.-P. **Serre**, _Espaces fibrés algébriques_, Séminaire C. Chevalley, **3e année: 1958**, exposé 1, Secrétariat
Mathématique, Paris, 1958. — Cited in §V.6.1 for the Picard-group computation of projective fibrations.

## Translator's note

The EGA V prenotes also reference work by Hironaka, Nagata, Serre (beyond `(FAC)` and `[52]`), Chevalley, and others.
Most of these references appear without precise pagination in the prenotes (Grothendieck's notes-to-self typically
abbreviate to author surname and the result invoked). Where the specific result is cited in the prenote, we trace it to
the EGA III/IV bibliographic entry; where the prenote leaves the reference vague, we preserve that vagueness in the
translated body and add a translator footnote only if the modern equivalent is non-obvious.

The Vaiello unified edition ($papers/books/ega/v/04-vaiello-unified-translation.md$) is the operational source for
§§V.2.15-2.16 and is consulted as a reconciliation reference for §§V.1, V.5, V.6 when the Blass piecemeal source has
ambiguous or illegible passages. The Vaiello edition itself is not bibliographically citable as a separate work; the
underlying translations are by Piotr Blass and Joseph Blass.
