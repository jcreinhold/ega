# В§1. Cohomology of affine schemes

<!-- original page 82 -->

## 1.1. Review of the exterior algebra complex

**1.1.1.**

<!-- label: III.1.1.1 -->

Let `A` be a ring, `рқҗҹ = (f_i)_{1 вүӨ i вүӨ r}` a system of `r` elements of `A`. The *exterior algebra complex* `K_вҖў(рқҗҹ)`
corresponding to `рқҗҹ` is a chain complex `(G, I, 2.2)` defined as follows: the graded `A`-module `K_вҖў(рқҗҹ)` is equal to the
*exterior algebra* `Оӣ(A^r)`, graded in the usual way, and the boundary operator is the *interior multiplication* `i_рқҗҹ`
by `рқҗҹ`, viewed as an element of the dual `(A^r)^вҲЁ`. Recall that `i_рқҗҹ` is an *antiderivation* of degree `вҲ’1` on `Оӣ(A^r)`,
and that if `(рқҗһ_i)_{1 вүӨ i вүӨ r}` is the canonical basis of `A^r` then `i_рқҗҹ(рқҗһ_i) = f_i`; the verification of
`i_рқҗҹ вҲҳ i_рқҗҹ = 0` is immediate.

An equivalent definition is the following. For each `i`, consider the chain complex `K_вҖў(f_i)` defined by
`K_0(f_i) = K_1(f_i) = A`, `K_n(f_i) = 0` for `n вү  0, 1`, and with boundary operator `d_1 : A вҶ’ A` equal to
*multiplication by `f_i`*. Then `K_вҖў(рқҗҹ)` is the *tensor product*

```text
  K_вҖў(рқҗҹ) = K_вҖў(f_1) вҠ— K_вҖў(f_2) вҠ— вӢҜ вҠ— K_вҖў(f_r)                                (G, I, 2.7)
```

equipped with its total grading; the isomorphism between this complex and the one defined above is immediate.

> _Translator's note._ EGA's `complexe de l'algГЁbre extГ©rieure` is what is now universally called the *Koszul complex*.
> We retain EGA's name on first introduction and use it interchangeably below.

**1.1.2.**

<!-- label: III.1.1.2 -->

For every `A`-module `M`, define the *chain complex*

```text
  K_вҖў(рқҗҹ, M) = K_вҖў(рқҗҹ) вҠ—_A M                                                   (1.1.2.1)
```

and the *cochain complex* `(G, I, 2.2)`

```text
  K^вҖў(рқҗҹ, M) = Hom_A(K_вҖў(рқҗҹ), M).                                              (1.1.2.2)
```

If `g` is a `k`-cochain of the latter complex, and if we set

```text
  g(i_1, вҖҰ, i_k) = g(рқҗһ_{i_1} вҲ§ вӢҜ вҲ§ рқҗһ_{i_k}),
```

then `g` identifies with an *alternating* map from `[1, r]^k` to `M`, and the above definitions give

```text
  d^k g(i_1, i_2, вҖҰ, i_{k+1})
        = ОЈ_{h=1}^{k+1} (вҲ’1)^{hвҲ’1} f_{i_h} g(i_1, вҖҰ, Г®_h, вҖҰ, i_{k+1}).        (1.1.2.3)
```

<!-- original page 83 -->

**1.1.3.**

<!-- label: III.1.1.3 -->

From the preceding complexes one deduces, as usual, the *homology* and *cohomology* `A`-modules `(G, I, 2.2)`

```text
  H_вҖў(рқҗҹ, M) = H_вҖў(K_вҖў(рқҗҹ, M)),                                                (1.1.3.1)
  H^вҖў(рқҗҹ, M) = H^вҖў(K^вҖў(рқҗҹ, M)).                                                (1.1.3.2)
```

One defines an `A`-isomorphism `K_вҖў(рқҗҹ, M) вҘІ K^вҖў(рқҗҹ, M)` by associating to each chain
`z = ОЈ (рқҗһ_{i_1} вҲ§ вӢҜ вҲ§ рқҗһ_{i_k}) вҠ— z_{i_1, вҖҰ, i_k}` the cochain `g_z` with

```text
  g_z(j_1, вҖҰ, j_{rвҲ’k}) = Оө В· z_{i_1, вҖҰ, i_k},
```

where `(j_h)_{1 вүӨ h вүӨ rвҲ’k}` is the strictly increasing sequence complementary to the strictly increasing sequence
`(i_h)_{1 вүӨ h вүӨ k}` in `[1, r]` and `Оө = (вҲ’1)^ОҪ`, with `ОҪ` the number of inversions of the permutation
`i_1, вҖҰ, i_k, j_1, вҖҰ, j_{rвҲ’k}` of `[1, r]`. One checks that `g_{dz} = d(g_z)`, yielding an isomorphism

```text
  H^i(рқҗҹ, M) вү… H_{rвҲ’i}(рқҗҹ, M)                  for 0 вүӨ i вүӨ r.                 (1.1.3.3)
```

In this chapter we shall mostly consider the cohomology modules `H^вҖў(рқҗҹ, M)`.

For a given `рқҗҹ`, it is immediate `(G, I, 2.1)` that `M вҶҰ H^вҖў(рқҗҹ, M)` is a *cohomological functor* `(T, II, 2.1)` from the
category of `A`-modules to the category of graded `A`-modules, vanishing in degrees `< 0` and `> r`. In addition

```text
  H^0(рқҗҹ, M) = Hom_A(A/(рқҗҹ), M),                                               (1.1.3.4)
```

writing `(рқҗҹ)` for the ideal of `A` generated by `f_1, вҖҰ, f_r`; this follows immediately from `(1.1.2.3)`, and
`H^0(рқҗҹ, M)` is clearly identified with the submodule of `M` annihilated by `(рқҗҹ)`. Similarly, `(1.1.2.3)` gives

```text
  H^r(рқҗҹ, M) = M / (ОЈ_{i=1}^r f_i M) = (A/(рқҗҹ)) вҠ—_A M.                         (1.1.3.5)
```

We shall use the following well-known result, whose proof we recall for completeness.

**Proposition (1.1.4).**

<!-- label: III.1.1.4 -->

*Let `A` be a ring, `рқҗҹ = (f_i)_{1 вүӨ i вүӨ r}` a finite family of elements of `A`, and `M` an `A`-module. If, for
`1 вүӨ i вүӨ r`, the multiplication `z вҶҰ f_i z` on `M_{iвҲ’1} = M / (f_1 M + вӢҜ + f_{iвҲ’1} M)` is injective, then
`H^p(рқҗҹ, M) = 0` for `p вү  r`.*

**Proof.** It suffices to prove that `H_i(рқҗҹ, M) = 0` for every `i > 0` by virtue of `(1.1.3.3)`. We argue by induction
on `r`, the case `r = 0` being trivial. Set `рқҗҹ' = (f_i)_{1 вүӨ i вүӨ rвҲ’1}`; this family satisfies the hypotheses of the
statement, so on setting `L_вҖў = K_вҖў(рқҗҹ', M)` we have `H_i(L_вҖў) = 0` for `i > 0` by induction and `H_0(L_вҖў) = M_{rвҲ’1}` by
`(1.1.3.3)` and `(1.1.3.5)`. To abbreviate, set `K_вҖў = K_вҖў(f_r) = K_0 вҠ• K_1`, with `K_0 = K_1 = A` and `d_1 : K_1 вҶ’ K_0`
equal to multiplication by `f_r`; by definition (1.1.1) we have `K_вҖў(рқҗҹ, M) = K_вҖў вҠ—_A L_вҖў`. We invoke the following
lemma.

**Lemma (1.1.4.1).**

<!-- label: III.1.1.4.1 -->

*Let `K_вҖў` be a chain complex of free `A`-modules, zero except in dimensions `0` and `1`. For every chain complex `L_вҖў`
of `A`-modules, there is an exact sequence*

```text
  0 вҶ’ H_0(K_вҖў вҠ— H_p(L_вҖў)) вҶ’ H_p(K_вҖў вҠ— L_вҖў) вҶ’ H_1(K_вҖў вҠ— H_{pвҲ’1}(L_вҖў)) вҶ’ 0
```

*for every index `p`.*

<!-- original page 84 -->

This is a particular case of an exact sequence of low-degree terms of the KГјnneth spectral sequence `(M, XVII, 5.2 (a))`
and `(G, I, 5.5.2)`; one can also prove it directly, as follows. Regard `K_0` and `K_1` as chain complexes (zero in
dimensions `вү  0` and `вү  1` respectively); then there is an exact sequence of complexes

```text
  0 вҶ’ K_0 вҠ— L_вҖў вҶ’ K_вҖў вҠ— L_вҖў вҶ’ K_1 вҠ— L_вҖў вҶ’ 0,
```

to which the exact sequence in homology applies:

```text
  вӢҜ вҶ’ H_{p+1}(K_1 вҠ— L_вҖў) вҶ’ H_p(K_0 вҠ— L_вҖў) вҶ’ H_p(K_вҖў вҠ— L_вҖў) вҶ’ H_p(K_1 вҠ— L_вҖў)
                       вҶ’ H_{pвҲ’1}(K_0 вҠ— L_вҖў) вҶ’ вӢҜ.
```

But `H_p(K_0 вҠ— L_вҖў) = K_0 вҠ— H_p(L_вҖў)` and `H_p(K_1 вҠ— L_вҖў) = K_1 вҠ— H_{pвҲ’1}(L_вҖў)` for every `p`; one verifies immediately,
in addition, that the boundary `вҲӮ : K_1 вҠ— H_p(L_вҖў) вҶ’ K_0 вҠ— H_p(L_вҖў)` coincides with `d_1 вҠ— 1`. The lemma follows from
the exact sequence above and from the definitions of `H_0(K_вҖў вҠ— H_p(L_вҖў))` and `H_1(K_вҖў вҠ— H_{pвҲ’1}(L_вҖў))`.

With this lemma in hand, the rest of the proof of (1.1.4) is immediate: the induction hypothesis combined with (1.1.4.1)
gives `H_p(K_вҖў вҠ— L_вҖў) = 0` for `p вүҘ 2`; moreover, if we show that `H_1(K_вҖў, H_0(L_вҖў)) = 0`, then the same lemma also
gives `H_1(K_вҖў вҠ— L_вҖў) = 0`. But by definition `H_1(K_вҖў, H_0(L_вҖў))` is the kernel of the multiplication `z вҶҰ f_r z` on
`M_{rвҲ’1}`, and that kernel is zero by hypothesis. The proof is complete.

**1.1.5.**

<!-- label: III.1.1.5 -->

Let `рқҗ  = (g_i)_{1 вүӨ i вүӨ r}` be a second sequence of `r` elements of `A`, and set `рқҗҹрқҗ  = (f_i g_i)_{1 вүӨ i вүӨ r}`. One can
define a canonical homomorphism of complexes

```text
  ПҶ_рқҗ  : K_вҖў(рқҗҹрқҗ ) вҶ’ K_вҖў(рқҗҹ)                                                     (1.1.5.1)
```

as the canonical extension to the exterior algebra `Оӣ(A^r)` of the `A`-linear map
`(x_1, вҖҰ, x_r) вҶҰ (g_1 x_1, вҖҰ, g_r x_r)` from `A^r` to itself. To see that this is a homomorphism of complexes, note in
general that if `u : E вҶ’ F` is an `A`-linear map, `рқҗұ вҲҲ F^вҲЁ`, and `рқҗІ = бө—u(рқҗұ) вҲҲ E^вҲЁ`, then

```text
  (Оӣ u) вҲҳ i_рқҗІ = i_рқҗұ вҲҳ (Оӣ u);                                                 (1.1.5.2)
```

indeed, both sides are antiderivations of `Оӣ F`, and it suffices to check that they coincide on `F`, which follows
immediately from the definitions.

When one identifies `K_вҖў(рқҗҹ)` with the tensor product of the `K_вҖў(f_i)` (1.1.1), `ПҶ_рқҗ ` is the tensor product of the
`ПҶ_{g_i}`, where `ПҶ_{g_i}` reduces to the identity in degree `0` and to multiplication by `g_i` in degree `1`.

**1.1.6.**

<!-- label: III.1.1.6 -->

In particular, for every pair of integers `m`, `n` with `0 вүӨ n вүӨ m`, we have homomorphisms of complexes

```text
  ПҶ_{рқҗҹ^{mвҲ’n}} : K_вҖў(рқҗҹ^m) вҶ’ K_вҖў(рқҗҹ^n)                                          (1.1.6.1)
```

and hence homomorphisms

```text
  ПҶ_{рқҗҹ^{mвҲ’n}} : K^вҖў(рқҗҹ^n, M) вҶ’ K^вҖў(рқҗҹ^m, M),                                   (1.1.6.2)
  ПҶ_{рқҗҹ^{mвҲ’n}} : H^вҖў(рқҗҹ^n, M) вҶ’ H^вҖў(рқҗҹ^m, M).                                   (1.1.6.3)
```

<!-- original page 85 -->

The latter satisfy the obvious transitivity relation `ПҶ_{рқҗҹ^{mвҲ’p}} = ПҶ_{рқҗҹ^{mвҲ’n}} вҲҳ ПҶ_{рқҗҹ^{nвҲ’p}}` for `p вүӨ n вүӨ m`; they
thus define two inductive systems of `A`-modules. We set

```text
  C^вҖў((рқҗҹ), M) = lim_вҶ’ K^вҖў(рқҗҹ^n, M),                                           (1.1.6.4)
                  n
  H^вҖў((рқҗҹ), M) = H^вҖў(C^вҖў((рқҗҹ), M)) = lim_вҶ’ H^вҖў(рқҗҹ^n, M),                        (1.1.6.5)
                                     n
```

the last equality coming from the fact that the inductive limit commutes with the functor `H^вҖў` `(G, I, 2.1)`. We shall
see later (1.4.3) that `H^вҖў((рқҗҹ), M)` depends only on the *ideal* `(рқҗҹ)` of `A` (in fact only on the `(рқҗҹ)`-preadic
topology on `A`), which justifies the notations.

It is clear that `M вҶҰ C^вҖў((рқҗҹ), M)` is an exact `A`-linear functor and `M вҶҰ H^вҖў((рқҗҹ), M)` a cohomological functor.

**1.1.7.**

<!-- label: III.1.1.7 -->

Let `рқҗҹ = (f_i) вҲҲ A^r` and `рқҗ  = (g_i) вҲҲ A^r`; write `e_рқҗ ` for the left multiplication by the vector `рқҗ  вҲҲ A^r` on the
exterior algebra `Оӣ(A^r)`. We have the *homotopy formula*

```text
  i_рқҗҹ e_рқҗ  + e_рқҗ  i_рқҗҹ = вҹЁрқҗ , рқҗҹвҹ© В· 1                                            (1.1.7.1)
```

on the `A`-module `Оӣ(A^r)` (where `1` denotes the identity automorphism of `Оӣ(A^r)`); this relation says, equivalently,
that *in the complex `K_вҖў(рқҗҹ)`*

```text
  d e_рқҗ  + e_рқҗ  d = вҹЁрқҗ , рқҗҹвҹ© В· 1.                                                (1.1.7.2)
```

If the ideal `(рқҗҹ)` is equal to `A`, then there exists `рқҗ  вҲҲ A^r` such that `вҹЁрқҗ , рқҗҹвҹ© = ОЈ_{i=1}^r g_i f_i = 1`. Therefore
`(G, I, 2.4)`:

**Proposition (1.1.8).**

<!-- label: III.1.1.8 -->

*Suppose the ideal `(рқҗҹ)` generated by the `f_i` is equal to `A`. Then the complex `K_вҖў(рқҗҹ)` is homotopically trivial, and
so are the complexes `K_вҖў(рқҗҹ, M)` and `K^вҖў(рқҗҹ, M)` for every `A`-module `M`.*

**Corollary (1.1.9).**

<!-- label: III.1.1.9 -->

*If `(рқҗҹ) = A`, then `H^вҖў(рқҗҹ, M) = 0` and `H^вҖў((рқҗҹ), M) = 0` for every `A`-module `M`.*

**Proof.** We then have `(рқҗҹ^n) = A` for every `n`.

**Remark (1.1.10).**

<!-- label: III.1.1.10 -->

With the notation above, let `X = Spec(A)`, and let `Y` be the closed subscheme of `X` defined by the ideal `(рқҗҹ)`. We
shall prove in В§9 that `H^вҖў((рқҗҹ), M)` is isomorphic to the cohomology `H_Y^вҖў(X, MМғ)` of `X` with support in the filter of
closed subsets of `Y` `(T, 3.2)`. We shall also show that Proposition (1.2.3), applied to `X` and to `в„ұ = MМғ`, is a
particular case of an exact cohomology sequence

```text
  вӢҜ вҶ’ H_Y^p(X, в„ұ) вҶ’ H^p(X, в„ұ) вҶ’ H^p(X вҲ’ Y, в„ұ) вҶ’ H_Y^{p+1}(X, в„ұ) вҶ’ вӢҜ.
```

## 1.2. ДҢech cohomology of an open cover

**1.2.1. Notations.**

<!-- label: III.1.2.1 -->

Throughout this article we shall write:

- `X` for a prescheme;
- `в„ұ` for a quasi-coherent `рқ’Ә_X`-module;

<!-- original page 86 -->

- `A = О“(X, рқ’Ә_X)`, `M = О“(X, в„ұ)`;
- `рқҗҹ = (f_i)_{1 вүӨ i вүӨ r}` for a finite system of elements of `A`;
- `U_i = X_{f_i}` for the open set `(0_I, 5.5.2)` of `x вҲҲ X` with `f_i(x) вү  0`;
- `U = вӢғ U_i`;
- `рқ”ҳ` for the open cover `(U_i)_{1 вүӨ i вүӨ r}` of `U`.

**1.2.2.**

<!-- label: III.1.2.2 -->

Suppose `X` is either a prescheme whose underlying space is Noetherian, or a scheme whose underlying space is
quasi-compact. Then `(I, 9.3.3)` gives `О“(U_i, в„ұ) = M_{f_i}`. Set

```text
  U_{i_0, вҖҰ, i_p} = вӢӮ_{k=0}^p U_{i_k} = X_{f_{i_0} вӢҜ f_{i_p}}                (0_I, 5.5.3);
```

we then likewise have

```text
  О“(U_{i_0, вҖҰ, i_p}, в„ұ) = M_{f_{i_0} вӢҜ f_{i_p}}.                             (1.2.2.1)
```

But `(0_I, 1.6.1)` identifies `M_{f_{i_0} вӢҜ f_{i_p}}` with the inductive limit `lim_вҶ’_n M_{i_0, вҖҰ, i_p}^{(n)}`, where
`M_{i_0, вҖҰ, i_p}^{(n)} = M` and the transition map `ПҶ_{n,m} : M_{i_0, вҖҰ, i_p}^{(m)} вҶ’ M_{i_0, вҖҰ, i_p}^{(n)}` is
multiplication by `(f_{i_0} вӢҜ f_{i_p})^{nвҲ’m}` for `m вүӨ n`. Denote by `C^p_n(M)` the set of alternating maps from
`[1, r]^{p+1}` to `M` (for every `n`); these `A`-modules likewise form an inductive system under the `ПҶ_{n,m}`. If
`C^p(рқ”ҳ, в„ұ)` is the group of alternating ДҢech `p`-cochains of `в„ұ` relative to the cover `рқ”ҳ` `(G, II, 5.1)`, the foregoing
gives

```text
  C^p(рқ”ҳ, в„ұ) = lim_вҶ’ C^p_n(M).                                                 (1.2.2.2)
                n
```

With the notation of (1.1.2), `C^p_n(M)` is identified with `K^{p+1}(рқҗҹ^n, M)`, and the map `ПҶ_{n,m}` with the map
`ПҶ_{рқҗҹ^{nвҲ’m}}` defined in (1.1.6). Thus, for every `p вүҘ 0`, there is a canonical functorial-in-`в„ұ` isomorphism

```text
  C^p(рқ”ҳ, в„ұ) вү… C^p((рқҗҹ), M).                                                   (1.2.2.3)
```

Moreover, the formula `(1.1.2.3)` and the definition of the cohomology of a cover `(G, II, 5.1)` show that the
isomorphisms `(1.2.2.3)` are compatible with the coboundary operators.

**Proposition (1.2.3).**

<!-- label: III.1.2.3 -->

*If `X` is a prescheme whose underlying space is Noetherian, or a scheme whose underlying space is quasi-compact, then
there is a canonical functorial-in-`в„ұ` isomorphism*

```text
  H^p(рқ”ҳ, в„ұ) вү… H^{p+1}((рқҗҹ), M)              for every p вүҘ 1.                  (1.2.3.1)
```

*In addition, there is a functorial-in-`в„ұ` exact sequence*

```text
  0 вҶ’ H^0((рқҗҹ), M) вҶ’ M вҶ’ H^0(рқ”ҳ, в„ұ) вҶ’ H^1((рқҗҹ), M) вҶ’ 0.                         (1.2.3.2)
```

<!-- original page 87 -->

**Proof.** The relations `(1.2.3.1)` are an immediate consequence of (1.2.2). On the other hand,
`C^0(рқ”ҳ, в„ұ) = C^1((рқҗҹ), M)`, so `H^0(рқ”ҳ, в„ұ)` identifies with the subgroup of `1`-cocycles of `C^вҖў((рқҗҹ), M)`; since
`M = C^0((рқҗҹ), M)`, the exact sequence `(1.2.3.2)` is the one that comes from the definition of the cohomology groups
`H^0((рқҗҹ), M)` and `H^1((рқҗҹ), M)`.

**Corollary (1.2.4).**

<!-- label: III.1.2.4 -->

*Suppose the `X_{f_i}` are quasi-compact and that there exist `g_i вҲҲ О“(U, в„ұ)` such that `ОЈ_i g_i (f_i вҲЈ U) = 1_U`. Then,
for every quasi-coherent `(рқ’Ә_X вҲЈ U)`-module `в„ұ`, we have `H^p(рқ”ҳ, в„ұ) = 0` for `p > 0`; if in addition `U = X`, the
canonical homomorphism `(1.2.3.2)` `M вҶ’ H^0(рқ”ҳ, в„ұ)` is bijective.*

**Proof.** Since by hypothesis the `U_i = X_{f_i}` are quasi-compact, so is `U`, so we may reduce to the case `U = X`;
the hypothesis then implies `H^p((рқҗҹ), M) = 0` for every `p вүҘ 0` (1.1.9). The corollary follows from `(1.2.3.1)` and
`(1.2.3.2)`.

Note that since `H^0(рқ”ҳ, в„ұ) = H^0(U, в„ұ)` `(G, II, 5.2.2)`, the result `(I, 1.3.7)` is recovered here as a special case.

**Remark (1.2.5).**

<!-- label: III.1.2.5 -->

Suppose `X` is an affine scheme; then the `U_i = X_{f_i} = D(f_i)` are affine open sets, as are the `U_{i_0, вҖҰ, i_p}`
(though `U` is not necessarily affine). In this case the functors `О“(X, В·)` and `О“(U_{i_0, вҖҰ, i_p}, В·)` are exact in `в„ұ`
`(I, 1.3.11)`. Given an exact sequence `0 вҶ’ в„ұ' вҶ’ в„ұ вҶ’ в„ұ'' вҶ’ 0` of quasi-coherent `рқ’Ә_X`-modules, the sequence of complexes


is exact, and yields a long exact sequence in cohomology

```text
  вӢҜ вҶ’ H^p(рқ”ҳ, в„ұ') вҶ’ H^p(рқ”ҳ, в„ұ) вҶ’ H^p(рқ”ҳ, в„ұ'') вҶ’ H^{p+1}(рқ”ҳ, в„ұ') вҶ’ вӢҜ.
```

On the other hand, setting `M' = О“(X, в„ұ')`, `M'' = О“(X, в„ұ'')`, the sequence `0 вҶ’ M' вҶ’ M вҶ’ M'' вҶ’ 0` is exact; as
`C^вҖў((рқҗҹ), M)` is an exact functor of `M`, we also obtain the exact sequence in cohomology

```text
  вӢҜ вҶ’ H^p((рқҗҹ), M') вҶ’ H^p((рқҗҹ), M) вҶ’ H^p((рқҗҹ), M'') вҶ’ H^{p+1}((рқҗҹ), M') вҶ’ вӢҜ.
```

Since the diagram

```text
  0 вҶ’ C^вҖў(рқ”ҳ, в„ұ')     вҶ’ C^вҖў(рқ”ҳ, в„ұ)     вҶ’ C^вҖў(рқ”ҳ, в„ұ'')     вҶ’ 0
        вҶ“                 вҶ“                 вҶ“
  0 вҶ’ C^вҖў((рқҗҹ), M')   вҶ’ C^вҖў((рқҗҹ), M)   вҶ’ C^вҖў((рқҗҹ), M'')   вҶ’
```

<!-- original page 88 -->

is commutative, the diagrams

```text
              H^p(рқ”ҳ, в„ұ'')   вҶ’   H^{p+1}(рқ”ҳ, в„ұ')
                  вҶ“                  вҶ“                                       (1.2.5.1)
              H^{p+1}((рқҗҹ), M'') вҶ’ H^{p+1}((рқҗҹ), M')
```

are commutative for every `p` `(G, I, 2.1.1)`.

## 1.3. Cohomology of an affine scheme

**Theorem (1.3.1).**

<!-- label: III.1.3.1 -->

*Let `X` be an affine scheme. For every quasi-coherent `рқ’Ә_X`-module `в„ұ`, we have `H^p(X, в„ұ) = 0` for every `p > 0`.*

**Proof.** Let `рқ”ҳ` be a finite cover of `X` by affine open sets `X_{f_i} = D(f_i)` (`1 вүӨ i вүӨ r`); the ideal of
`A = О“(X, рқ’Ә_X)` generated by the `f_i` is then `A`. By `(1.2.4)` we have `Иһ^p(рқ”ҳ, в„ұ) = 0` for `p > 0`. Since there are
arbitrarily fine finite affine open covers of `X` `(I, 1.1.10)`, the definition of ДҢech cohomology `(G, II, 5.8)` shows
that `Иһ^p(X, в„ұ) = 0` for `p > 0`. The same argument applies to every `X_f` for `f вҲҲ A` `(I, 1.3.6)`, so
`Иһ^p(X_f, в„ұ) = 0` for `p > 0`. As `X_f вҲ© X_g = X_{fg}`, we conclude that `H^p(X, в„ұ) = 0` for every `p > 0` by virtue of
`(G, II, 5.9.2)`.

**Corollary (1.3.2).**

<!-- label: III.1.3.2 -->

*Let `Y` be a prescheme and `f : X вҶ’ Y` an affine morphism `(II, 1.6.1)`. For every quasi-coherent `рқ’Ә_X`-module `в„ұ`, we
have `R^q f_*(в„ұ) = 0` for `q > 0`.*

**Proof.** By definition, `R^q f_*(в„ұ)` is the `рқ’Ә_Y`-module associated to the presheaf `U вҶҰ H^q(f^{-1}(U), в„ұ)`, where `U`
ranges over the open sets of `Y`. The affine open sets form a base of `Y`, and for such a `U` the preimage `f^{-1}(U)`
is affine `(II, 1.3.2)`, hence `H^q(f^{-1}(U), в„ұ) = 0` by `(1.3.1)`, proving the corollary.

**Corollary (1.3.3).**

<!-- label: III.1.3.3 -->

*Let `Y` be a prescheme and `f : X вҶ’ Y` an affine morphism. For every quasi-coherent `рқ’Ә_X`-module `в„ұ`, the canonical
homomorphism*

```text
  H^p(Y, f_*(в„ұ)) вҶ’ H^p(X, в„ұ)                                   (0, 12.1.3.1)
```

*is bijective for every `p`.*

**Proof.** By `(0, 12.1.7)` it suffices to show that the edge homomorphisms `вҖіE_2^{p, 0} = H^p(Y, f_*(в„ұ)) вҶ’ H^p(X, в„ұ)`
of the second spectral sequence of the composite functor `О“ f_*` are bijective. The `E_2` term of this sequence is
`вҖіE_2^{p, q} = H^p(Y, R^q f_*(в„ұ))` `(G, II, 4.17.1)`, and (1.3.2) gives `вҖіE_2^{p, q} = 0` for `q > 0`; the spectral
sequence therefore degenerates. The assertion follows from `(0, 11.1.6)`.

**Corollary (1.3.4).**

<!-- label: III.1.3.4 -->

*Let `f : X вҶ’ Y` be an affine morphism and `g : Y вҶ’ Z` a morphism.*

<!-- original page 89 -->

*For every quasi-coherent `рқ’Ә_X`-module `в„ұ`, the canonical homomorphism `R^p g_*(f_*(в„ұ)) вҶ’ R^p(g вҲҳ f)_*(в„ұ)`
`(0, 12.2.5.1)` is bijective for every `p`.*

**Proof.** Note that, by (1.3.3), for every affine open `W` of `Z`, the canonical homomorphism
`H^p(g^{-1}(W), f_*(в„ұ)) вҶ’ H^p(f^{-1}(g^{-1}(W)), в„ұ)` is bijective; this shows that the presheaf homomorphism defining
the canonical homomorphism `R^p g_*(f_*(в„ұ)) вҶ’ R^p(g вҲҳ f)_*(в„ұ)` is bijective `(0, 12.2.5)`.

## 1.4. Application to the cohomology of arbitrary preschemes

**Proposition (1.4.1).**

<!-- label: III.1.4.1 -->

**Proof.** Since `X` is a scheme, every finite intersection `V` of open sets of the cover `рқ”ҳ` is affine `(I, 5.5.6)`, so
`H^q(V, в„ұ) = 0` for `q вүҘ 1` by `(1.3.1)`. The proposition follows from Leray's theorem `(G, II, 5.4.1)`.

**Remark (1.4.2).**

**Proposition (1.4.5).**

<!-- label: III.1.4.5 -->

*Let `X` be a quasi-compact scheme, `в„’` an invertible `рқ’Ә_X`-module, and consider the graded ring `A_вҖў = О“_*(в„’)`
`(II, 5.4.6)`. Then `H^вҖў(X, в„ұ) = вҠ•_n H^вҖў(X, в„ұ вҠ— в„’^{вҠ—n})` is a graded `A_вҖў`-module, and for every `f вҲҲ A_d` there is a

```text
  H^вҖў(X_f, в„ұ) вү… (H^вҖў(X, в„ұ))_{(f)}                                            (1.4.5.1)
```

*of `(A_вҖў)_{(f)}`-modules.*

**Proof.** Since `X` is a quasi-compact scheme, the cohomology of all the `рқ’Ә_X`-modules `в„ұ вҠ— в„’^{вҠ—n}` can be computed
with one and the same finite cover `рқ”ҳ = (U_i)` by affine open sets such that the restriction `в„’ вҲЈ U_i` is isomorphic to
`рқ’Ә_X вҲЈ U_i` for each `i` (1.4.1). It is then immediate that the `U_i вҲ© X_f` are affine open sets `(I, 1.3.6)`, so the
cohomology `H^вҖў(X_f, в„ұ вҠ— в„’^{вҠ—n})` can also be computed with the cover `рқ”ҳ вҲЈ X_f = (U_i вҲ© X_f)` (1.4.1). It is immediate
that for every `f вҲҲ A_d` multiplication by `f` defines a homomorphism `C^вҖў(рқ”ҳ, в„ұ вҠ— в„’^{вҠ—n}) вҶ’ C^вҖў(рқ”ҳ, в„ұ вҠ— в„’^{вҠ—(n+d)})`,

```text

```

taking `(I, 1.3.9, (ii))` into account. Passing to the cohomology of both complexes, one obtains the isomorphism
`(1.4.5.1)`, using that the functor `M вҶҰ M_{(f)}` is exact on the category of graded `A_вҖў`-modules.

**Corollary (1.4.7).**

<!-- label: III.1.4.7 -->

*Let `X` be a quasi-compact scheme and `f вҲҲ О“(X, рқ’Ә_X)`.*

*(i) Suppose the open set `X_f` is affine. Then, for every quasi-coherent `рқ’Ә_X`-module `в„ұ`, every `i > 0`, and every
`Оҫ вҲҲ H^i(X, в„ұ)`, there exists an integer `n > 0` such that `f^n Оҫ = 0`.*

*(ii) Conversely, suppose `X_f` is quasi-compact and that for every quasi-coherent sheaf of ideals `рқ’Ҙ` of `рқ’Ә_X` and
every `Оҫ вҲҲ H^1(X, рқ’Ҙ)` there exists `n > 0` such that `f^n Оҫ = 0`. Then `X_f` is affine.*

**Proof.** (i) If `X_f` is affine, then `H^i(X_f, в„ұ) = 0` for every `i > 0` (1.3.1), so the assertion follows from

(ii) By Serre's criterion `(II, 5.2.1)`, it suffices to show that for every quasi-coherent sheaf of ideals `рқ’Ҙ'` of
`рқ’Ә_{X_f}` we have `H^1(X_f, рқ’Ҙ') = 0`. Since `X_f` is a quasi-compact open set in the quasi-compact scheme `X`, there is
a quasi-coherent sheaf of ideals `рқ’Ҙ` of `рқ’Ә_X` such that `рқ’Ҙ' = рқ’Ҙ вҲЈ X_f` `(I, 9.4.2)`. By (1.4.6),
`H^1(X_f, рқ’Ҙ') = (H^1(X, рқ’Ҙ))_f`, and the hypothesis forces the right side to be zero, whence the conclusion.

**Lemma (1.4.9).**

<!-- label: III.1.4.9 -->

*Let `X` be a quasi-compact scheme, `рқ”ҳ = (U_i)_{1 вүӨ i вүӨ n}` a finite cover of `X` by affine open sets, and `в„ұ` a
quasi-coherent `рқ’Ә_X`-module. The complex of sheaves `рқ’һ^вҖў(рқ”ҳ, в„ұ)` defined by the cover `рқ”ҳ` `(G, II, 5.2)` is then a
quasi-coherent `рқ’Ә_X`-module.*

**Proof.** By the definitions `(G, II, 5.2)`, `рқ’һ^вҖў(рқ”ҳ, в„ұ)` is a direct sum of sheaves obtained as direct images of the
`в„ұ вҲЈ U_{i_0, вҖҰ, i_p}` under the canonical injections `U_{i_0, вҖҰ, i_p} вҶ’ X`. The hypothesis that `X` is a scheme implies
that these injections are affine morphisms `(I, 5.5.6)`, so the `рқ’һ^вҖў(рқ”ҳ, в„ұ)` are quasi-coherent `(II, 1.2.6)`.

**Proposition (1.4.10).**

<!-- label: III.1.4.10 -->

*Let `u : X вҶ’ Y` be a separated and quasi-compact morphism. For every quasi-coherent `рқ’Ә_X`-module `в„ұ`, the `R^q u_*(в„ұ)`
are quasi-coherent `рқ’Ә_Y`-modules.*

**Proof.** The question being local on `Y`, we may suppose `Y` affine. Then `X` is a finite union of affine open sets
`U_i` (`1 вүӨ i вүӨ n`); let `рқ”ҳ = (U_i)`. Since `Y` is a scheme, `(I, 5.5.10)` shows that for every affine open `V вҠҶ Y` the
canonical injection `u^{-1}(V) вҶ’ X` is an affine morphism; therefore (by (1.4.1) and `(G, II, 5.2)`) there is a
canonical isomorphism

```text
  H^вҖў(u^{-1}(V), в„ұ) вү… H^вҖў(О“(V, рқ’ў)),                                          (1.4.10.1)
```

where `рқ’ў = u_*(рқ’һ^вҖў(рқ”ҳ, в„ұ))`. By (1.4.9) and `(I, 9.2.2)`, `рқ’ў` is a quasi-coherent `рқ’Ә_Y`-module; moreover, since
`рқ’һ^вҖў(рқ”ҳ, в„ұ)` is a complex of sheaves, so is `рқ’ў`. The definition of the cohomology `рқ“—^вҖў(рқ’ў)` `(G, II, 4.1)` then shows that

exact in `рқ’ў` on the category of quasi-coherent `рқ’Ә_Y`-modules, `(G, II, 4.1)` gives


is commutative. We conclude from the foregoing that the isomorphisms `(1.4.10.1)` define an isomorphism of `рқ’Ә_Y`-modules

```text
  R^вҖўu_*(в„ұ) вү… рқ“—^вҖў(рқ’ў),                                                        (1.4.10.3)
```

<!-- original page 92 -->

and hence `R^q u_*(в„ұ)` is quasi-coherent. The relations `(1.4.10.3)`, `(1.4.10.2)`, `(1.4.10.1)` also yield:

**Proposition (1.4.15).**

**Proof.** The canonical homomorphism `ПҒ : в„ұ вҶ’ v'_*(v'^*(в„ұ))` `(0_I, 4.4.3.2)` defines by functoriality a homomorphism

Composing `(1.4.15.3)` and `(1.4.15.2)` gives a homomorphism

```text
  ПҲ : R^q u_*(в„ұ) вҶ’ v_*(R^q u'_*(в„ұ'))
```

and hence the canonical homomorphism (whose definition uses no hypothesis on `v`)

```text
  ОҰ : v^*(R^q u_*(в„ұ)) вҶ’ R^q u'_*(в„ұ'),                                        (1.4.15.4)
```

which one must show is an isomorphism when `v` is flat. The question is local on `Y` and `Y'`, so we may suppose
`Y = Spec(A)` and `Y' = Spec(B)`. We use the following lemma.

**Lemma (1.4.15.5).**

<!-- label: III.1.4.15.5 -->

*Let `ПҶ : A вҶ’ B` be a ring homomorphism, `Y = Spec(A)`, `X = Spec(B)`, `f : X вҶ’ Y` the morphism corresponding to `ПҶ`,
and `M` a `B`-module. The `рқ’Ә_X`-module `MМғ` is `f`-flat `(0_I, 6.7.1)` if and only if `M` is a flat `A`-module. In
particular, the morphism `f` is flat if and only if `B` is a flat `A`-module.*

**Proof.** This follows from the definition `(0_I, 6.7.1)` and from `(0_I, 6.3.3)`, taking `(I, 1.3.4)` into account.

That being so, by `(1.4.11.1)` and the definitions of the homomorphisms `(1.4.15.3)` (cf. `0, 12.2.5`), `ОҰ` corresponds
to the composite morphism

```text
  H^q(X, в„ұ) вҶ’^ПҒ H^q(X, v'_*(в„ұ')) вҶ’^Пғ H^q(X', v'^*(v'_*(в„ұ'))) вҶ’^Оё H^q(X', в„ұ'),
```

where `ПҒ` and `Пғ` are the homomorphisms corresponding in cohomology to the canonical morphisms `ПҒ` and
`Пғ : v'^*(v'_*(в„ұ')) вҶ’ в„ұ'`, and `Оё` is the `ПҶ`-morphism `(0, 12.1.3.1)` relative to the `рқ’Ә_X`-module `v'_*(v'^*(в„ұ))`. By
the functoriality of `Оё`, one has the commutative diagram

```text
   H^q(X, в„ұ)              вҶ’^{ПҒ_ПҶ}              H^q(X, v'_*(v'^*(в„ұ)))
        вҶ“                                          вҶ“
   H^q(X', v'^*(в„ұ))                  H^q(X', v'^*(v'_*(v'^*(в„ұ))))
```

<!-- original page 94 -->

and since by definition `(0_I, 4.4.3)` `v'^*(ПҒ)` is the inverse of `Пғ`, the composite morphism considered above is none
other than `Оё_ПҶ`; thus `ОҰ` is the `B`-homomorphism associated to `H^q(X, в„ұ) вҠ—_A B вҶ’ H^q(X', в„ұ')`. Since `u` is of finite
type, `X` is a finite union of affine open sets `U_i` (`1 вүӨ i вүӨ r`); let `рқ”ҳ = (U_i)`. Moreover, `v` is an affine
morphism, hence so is `v'` `(II, 1.6.2, (iii))`, and the `U'_i = v'^{-1}(U_i)` form a finite affine open cover `рқ”ҳ'` of
`X'`. We know `(0, 12.1.4.2)` that the diagram

```text
   H^вҖў(рқ”ҳ, в„ұ)    вҶ’    H^вҖў(рқ”ҳ', в„ұ')
        вҶ“                  вҶ“
   H^вҖў(X, в„ұ)    вҶ’    H^вҖў(X', в„ұ')
```

is commutative, and the vertical arrows are isomorphisms, since `X` and `X'` are schemes (1.4.1). It thus suffices to
show that the canonical `ПҶ`-morphism `Оё : H^вҖў(рқ”ҳ, в„ұ) вҶ’ H^вҖў(рқ”ҳ', в„ұ')` is such that the associated `B`-homomorphism
`H^вҖў(рқ”ҳ, в„ұ) вҠ—_A B вҶ’ H^вҖў(рқ”ҳ', в„ұ')` is an isomorphism. For every sequence `s = (i_h)_{0 вүӨ h вүӨ p}` of `p + 1` indices in
`[1, r]`, set `U_s = вӢӮ_h U_{i_h}`, `U'_s = вӢӮ_h U'_{i_h} = v'^{-1}(U_s)`, `M_s = О“(U_s, в„ұ)`, `M'_s = О“(U'_s, в„ұ')`. The
canonical map `M_s вҠ—_A B вҶ’ M'_s` is an isomorphism `(I, 1.6.5)`, so the canonical map `C^вҖў(рқ”ҳ, в„ұ) вҠ—_A B вҶ’ C^вҖў(рқ”ҳ', в„ұ')` is
an isomorphism, under which `d вҠ— 1` is identified with the coboundary operator `C^вҖў(рқ”ҳ', в„ұ') вҶ’ C^{вҖў+1}(рқ”ҳ', в„ұ')`. Since
`B` is a flat `A`-module, the definition of the cohomology modules immediately gives that the canonical map
`H^вҖў(рқ”ҳ, в„ұ) вҠ—_A B вҶ’ H^вҖў(рқ”ҳ', в„ұ')` is an isomorphism `(0_I, 6.1.1)`. This result will be generalized later (В§6).

**Corollary (1.4.16).**

<!-- label: III.1.4.16 -->

*Let `A` be a ring, `X` an `A`-scheme of finite type, `B` an `A`-algebra faithfully flat over `A`. Then `X` is affine if
and only if `X вҠ—_A B` is affine.*

**Proof.** The condition is evidently necessary `(I, 3.2.2)`; we show it is sufficient. Since `X` is separated over `A`
and the morphism `Spec(B) вҶ’ Spec(A)` is flat, (1.4.15) gives

```text
  H^i(X вҠ—_A B, в„ұ вҠ—_A B) = H^i(X, в„ұ) вҠ—_A B                                    (1.4.16.1)
```

for every `i вүҘ 0` and every quasi-coherent `рқ’Ә_X`-module `в„ұ`. If `X вҠ—_A B` is affine, the left side of `(1.4.16.1)` is
zero for `i = 1`, hence so is `H^1(X, в„ұ)`, since `B` is faithfully flat over `A`. As `X` is a quasi-compact scheme, we
conclude by Serre's criterion `(II, 5.2.1)`.

**Proposition (1.4.17).**

<!-- label: III.1.4.17 -->

*Let `X` be a prescheme and `0 вҶ’ в„ұ' вҶ’ в„ұ вҶ’ в„ұ'' вҶ’ 0` an exact sequence of `рқ’Ә_X`-modules. If `в„ұ'` and `в„ұ''` are
quasi-coherent, so is `в„ұ`.*

<!-- original page 95 -->

**Proof.** The question is local on `X`, so we may suppose `X = Spec(A)` affine; it then suffices to show that `в„ұ`
satisfies the conditions `(d_1)` and `(d_2)` of `(I, 1.4.1)` (with `V = X`). The verification of `(d_1)` is immediate:
if `t вҲҲ О“(X, в„ұ)` has zero restriction to `D(f)`, then so does its image `v(t) вҲҲ О“(X, в„ұ'')`; thus there exists `m > 0`
such that `f^m v(t) = v(f^m t) = 0` `(I, 1.4.1)`. Since `в„ұ'` is exact at left, `f^m t = u(s)` for some `s вҲҲ О“(X, в„ұ')`;
since `u` is injective, the restriction of `s` to `D(f)` is zero, hence by `(I, 1.4.1)` there is `n > 0` with
`f^n s = 0`, and finally `f^{m+n} t = u(f^n s) = 0`.

Now verify `(d_2)`. Let `t' вҲҲ О“(D(f), в„ұ)`. Since `в„ұ''` is quasi-coherent, there exists an integer `m` such that
`f^m v(t') = v(f^m t')` extends to a section `t'' вҲҲ О“(X, в„ұ'')` `(I, 1.4.1)`. By (1.3.1) (or `(I, 5.1.9.2)`) applied to
the quasi-coherent `рқ’Ә_X`-module `в„ұ''`, the sequence `О“(X, в„ұ) вҶ’ О“(X, в„ұ'') вҶ’ 0` is exact, so there exists `П„ вҲҲ О“(X, в„ұ)`
with `v(П„) = t''`. We see that `v(f^m t' вҲ’ П„ вҲЈ D(f)) = 0`; writing `t'''` for the restriction of `П„` to `D(f)`, we have
`f^m t' вҲ’ t''' = u(s')` for some `s' вҲҲ О“(D(f), в„ұ')`. Since `в„ұ'` is quasi-coherent, there exists `n > 0` such that
`f^n s'` extends to a section `s вҲҲ О“(X, в„ұ')`; then `f^{m+n} t' вҲ’ f^n t''' = u(f^n s')`, so `f^{m+n} t'` is the
restriction to `D(f)` of the section `f^n П„ + u(f^n s) вҲҲ О“(X, в„ұ)`, completing the proof.
