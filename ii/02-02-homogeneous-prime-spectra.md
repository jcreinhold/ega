# В§2. Homogeneous prime spectra

## 2.1. Generalities on graded rings and modules

**Notation.**

<!-- label: II.2.1.1 -->

Given a _positively graded_ ring `S`, we denote by `S_n` the subset of `S` consisting
of homogeneous elements of degree `n` (`n вүҘ 0`), and by `S_+` the (direct) sum of the
`S_n` for `n > 0`. We have `1 вҲҲ S_0`, `S_0` is a subring of `S`, `S_+` is a graded
ideal of `S`, and `S` is the direct sum of `S_0` and `S_+`. If `M` is a _graded_
module over `S` (with positive or negative degrees), we similarly denote by `M_n` the
`S_0`-module formed by the homogeneous elements of degree `n` of `M` (with `n вҲҲ в„Ө`).

For every integer `d > 0`, we denote by `S^{(d)}` the direct sum of the `S_{nd}`;
by considering the elements of `S_{nd}` as homogeneous of degree `n`, the `S_{nd}`
define on `S^{(d)}` a graded ring structure.

For every integer `k` such that `0 вүӨ k вүӨ d вҲ’ 1`, we denote by `M^{(d, k)}` the direct
sum

<!-- original page 20 -->

of the `M_{nd + k}` (`n вҲҲ в„Ө`); this is a graded `S^{(d)}`-module when we consider
the elements of `M_{nd + k}` as homogeneous of degree `n`. We write `M^{(d)}` in
place of `M^{(d, 0)}`.

With the above notation, for every integer `n` (positive or negative), we denote by
`M(n)` the graded `S`-module defined by `(M(n))_k = M_{n + k}` for every `k вҲҲ в„Ө`. In
particular, `S(n)` will be a graded `S`-module such that `(S(n))_k = S_{n + k}`, with
the convention `S_n = 0` for `n < 0`. We say that a graded `S`-module `M` is _free_
if it is isomorphic, _as a graded module_, to a direct sum of modules of the form
`S(n)`; since `S(n)` is a monogeneous `S`-module, generated by the element `1` of `S`
considered as an element of degree `вҲ’n`, this is equivalent to saying that `M` admits
a _basis_ over `S` consisting of _homogeneous_ elements.

We say that a graded `S`-module `M` _admits a finite presentation_ if there exists an
exact sequence `P вҶ’ Q вҶ’ M вҶ’ 0`, where `P` and `Q` are finite direct sums of modules
of the form `S(n)` and the homomorphisms are of degree `0` (cf. (2.1.2)).

**(2.1.2)**

<!-- label: II.2.1.2 -->

Let `M`, `N` be two graded `S`-modules; we define on `M вҠ—_S N` a _graded_
`S`-module structure as follows. On the tensor product `M вҠ—_в„Ө N`, we can define a
graded `в„Ө`-module structure (where `в„Ө` is graded by `в„Ө_0 = в„Ө`, `в„Ө_n = 0` for `n вү  0`)
by setting `(M вҠ—_в„Ө N)_q = вҠ•_{m + n = q} M_m вҠ—_в„Ө N_n` (since `M` and `N` are
respectively direct sums of the `M_m` and the `N_n`, we know that `M вҠ—_в„Ө N` can be
canonically identified with the direct sum of all the `M_m вҠ—_в„Ө N_n`). This being so,
we have `M вҠ—_S N = (M вҠ—_в„Ө N)/P`, where `P` is the `в„Ө`-submodule of `M вҠ—_в„Ө N`
generated by the elements `(xs) вҠ— y вҲ’ x вҠ— (sy)` for `x вҲҲ M`, `y вҲҲ N`, `s вҲҲ S`; it is
clear that `P` is a _graded_ `в„Ө`-submodule of `M вҠ—_в„Ө N`, and we see immediately that
passing to the quotient yields a graded `S`-module structure on `M вҠ—_S N`.

For two graded `S`-modules `M`, `N`, recall that a homomorphism `u : M вҶ’ N` of
`S`-modules is said to be _of degree `k`_ if `u(M_j) вҠӮ N_{j + k}` for every `j вҲҲ в„Ө`.
If `H_n` denotes the set of all homomorphisms of degree `n` from `M` to `N`, we
denote by `Hom_S(M, N)` the (direct) _sum_ of the `H_n` (`n вҲҲ в„Ө`) inside the
`S`-module `H` of all (`S`-module) homomorphisms from `M` to `N`; in general
`Hom_S(M, N)` is not equal to the latter. However, we do have `H = Hom_S(M, N)` when
`M` is _of finite type_; for we may then suppose `M` generated by a finite number of
homogeneous elements `x_i` (`1 вүӨ i вүӨ n`), and every homomorphism `u вҲҲ H` can be
written in a unique way as `вҲ‘_{k вҲҲ в„Ө} u_k`, where for each `k`, `u_k(x_i)` is equal
to the homogeneous component of degree `k + deg(x_i)` of `u(x_i)` (`1 вүӨ i вүӨ n`),
which forces `u_k = 0` except for finitely many indices; by definition `u_k вҲҲ H_k`,
whence the conclusion.

We say that the elements of degree `0` of `Hom_S(M, N)` are the _homomorphisms of
graded `S`-modules_. It is clear that `S_m H_n вҠӮ H_{m + n}`, so the `H_n` define on
`Hom_S(M, N)` a graded `S`-module structure.

It follows immediately from these definitions that we have

```text
  M(m) вҠ—_S N(n) = (M вҠ—_S N)(m + n),                                       (2.1.2.1)
```

```text
  Hom_S(M(m), N(n)) = (Hom_S(M, N))(n вҲ’ m),                               (2.1.2.2)
```

for two graded `S`-modules `M`, `N`.

Let `S`, `S'` be two graded rings; a _homomorphism of graded rings_ `ПҶ : S вҶ’ S'` is a
ring homomorphism such that `ПҶ(S_n) вҠӮ S'_n` for every `n вҲҲ в„Ө` (in other words, `ПҶ`
must be a homomorphism _of degree `0`_ of graded `в„Ө`-modules). The data of such a
homomorphism endows `S'` with a _graded_ `S`-module structure; equipped with this
structure and with its graded ring structure, we say that `S'` is a _graded
`S`-algebra_.

If `M` is a graded `S`-module, the tensor product `M вҠ—_S S'` of _graded_
`S`-modules is in a natural way endowed with a _graded_ `S'`-module structure, the
grading being defined as above.

**Lemma.**

<!-- label: II.2.1.3 -->

Let `S` be a positively graded ring. For a subset `E` of `S_+` consisting of
homogeneous elements to generate `S_+` as an `S`-module, it is necessary and
sufficient that `E` generate `S` as an `S_0`-algebra.

**Proof.** The condition is evidently sufficient; we show that it is necessary. Let
`E_n` (resp. `E^n`) be the set of elements of `E` of degree equal to `n` (resp.
`вүӨ n`); it suffices to show, by induction on `n > 0`, that `S_n` is the
`S_0`-module generated by the elements of degree `n` that are products of elements
of `E^n`. This is evident for `n = 1` by virtue of the hypothesis; the same
hypothesis also shows that `S_n = вҲ‘_{p = 0}^{n вҲ’ 1} S_p E_{n вҲ’ p}`, and the
induction is then immediate.

**Corollary.**

<!-- label: II.2.1.4 -->

For `S_+` to be an ideal of finite type, it is necessary and sufficient that `S` be
an `S_0`-algebra of finite type.

**Proof.** We may always assume that a finite system of generators of the
`S_0`-algebra `S` (resp. of the `S`-ideal `S_+`) consists of homogeneous elements,
by replacing each generator considered by its homogeneous components.

**Corollary.**

<!-- label: II.2.1.5 -->

For `S` to be Noetherian, it is necessary and sufficient that `S_0` be Noetherian
and that `S` be an `S_0`-algebra of finite type.

**Proof.** The condition is evidently sufficient; it is necessary, since `S_0` is
isomorphic to `S/S_+` and `S_+` must be an ideal of finite type (2.1.4).

**Lemma.**

<!-- label: II.2.1.6 -->

Let `S` be a positively graded ring that is an `S_0`-algebra of finite type. Let `M`
be a graded `S`-module of finite type. Then:

(i) The `M_n` are `S_0`-modules of finite type, and there exists an integer `n_0`
such that `M_n = 0` for `n вүӨ n_0`.

(ii) There exists an integer `n_1` and an integer `h > 0` such that, for every
integer `n вүҘ n_1`, `M_{n + h} = S_h M_n`.

(iii) For every pair of integers `(d, k)` with `d > 0`, `0 вүӨ k вүӨ d вҲ’ 1`,
`M^{(d, k)}` is an `S^{(d)}`-module of finite type.

(iv) For every integer `d > 0`, `S^{(d)}` is an `S_0`-algebra of finite type.

(v) There exists an integer `h > 0` such that `S_{mh} = (S_h)^m` for every `m > 0`.

(vi) For every integer `n > 0`, there exists an integer `m_0` such that
`S_m вҠӮ S_+^n` for every `m вүҘ m_0`.

**Proof.** We may assume `S` generated (as an `S_0`-algebra) by homogeneous elements
`f_i` of degree `h_i` (`1 вүӨ i вүӨ r`), and `M` generated (as an `S`-module) by
homogeneous elements `x_j` of degree `k_j` (`1 вүӨ j вүӨ s`). It is clear that `M_n`
consists of linear combinations,

<!-- original page 22 -->

with coefficients in `S_0`, of the elements `f_1^{Оұ_1} вӢҜ f_r^{Оұ_r} x_j` such that the
`Оұ_i` are integers `вүҘ 0` satisfying `k_j + вҲ‘_i Оұ_i h_i = n`; for each `j`, there are
only finitely many systems `(Оұ_i)` satisfying this equation, since the `h_i` are
`> 0`, whence the first assertion of (i); the second is evident. On the other hand,
let `h` be the l.c.m. of the `h_i` and set `g_i = f_i^{h/h_i}` (`1 вүӨ i вүӨ r`), so that
all the `g_i` are of degree `h`; let `z_Ој` be the elements of `M` of the form
`f_1^{Оұ_1} вӢҜ f_r^{Оұ_r} x_j` with `0 вүӨ Оұ_i < h/h_i` for `1 вүӨ i вүӨ r`; these elements
are finite in number, so let `n_1` be the largest of their degrees. It is clear that
for `n вүҘ n_1`, every element of `M_{n + h}` is a linear combination of the `z_Ој`
whose coefficients are monomials of degree `> 0` with respect to the `g_i`, whence
`M_{n + h} = S_h M_n`, which establishes (ii). In the same way, we see (for every
`d > 0`) that an element of `M^{(d, k)}` is a linear combination, with coefficients
in `S_0`, of elements of the form `g^d f_1^{Оұ_1} вӢҜ f_r^{Оұ_r} x_j` with
`0 вүӨ Оұ_i < d`, `g` being a homogeneous element of `S`; whence (iii); (iv) then
follows from (iii) and from Lemma (2.1.3), by taking `M = S_+`, since
`(S_+)^{(d)} = (S^{(d)})_+`. The assertion of (v) is deduced from (ii) by taking
`M = S`. Finally, for a given `n`, there are only finitely many systems `(Оұ_i)` such
that `Оұ_i вүҘ 0` and `вҲ‘_i Оұ_i < n`, so if `m_0` is the largest value of the sum
`вҲ‘_i Оұ_i h_i` for these systems, we have `S_m вҠӮ S_+^n` for `m > m_0`, which
proves (vi).

**Corollary.**

<!-- label: II.2.1.7 -->

If `S` is Noetherian, so is `S^{(d)}` for every integer `d > 0`.

**Proof.** This follows from (2.1.5) and (2.1.6, (iv)).

**(2.1.8)**

Let `рқ”ӯ` be a _graded_ prime ideal of the graded ring `S`; `рқ”ӯ` is then a direct sum
of the subgroups `рқ”ӯ_n = рқ”ӯ вҲ© S_n`. Suppose that `рқ”ӯ does not contain `S_+``. Then if
`f вҲҲ S_+` is not in `рқ”ӯ`, the relation `f^n x вҲҲ рқ”ӯ` is equivalent to `x вҲҲ рқ”ӯ`; in
particular, if `f вҲҲ S_d` (`d > 0`), for every `x вҲҲ S_{m вҲ’ nd}`, the relation
`f^n x вҲҲ рқ”ӯ_m` is equivalent to `x вҲҲ рқ”ӯ_{m вҲ’ nd}`.

**Proposition.**

<!-- label: II.2.1.9 -->

Let `n_0` be an integer `> 0`; for every `n вүҘ n_0`, let `рқ”ӯ_n` be a subgroup of
`S_n`. For there to exist a graded prime ideal `рқ”ӯ` of `S` not containing `S_+` and
such that `рқ”ӯ вҲ© S_n = рқ”ӯ_n` for every `n вүҘ n_0`, it is necessary and sufficient that
the following conditions be satisfied:

1В° `S_m рқ”ӯ_n вҠӮ рқ”ӯ_{m + n}` for every `m вүҘ 0` and every `n вүҘ n_0`.

2В° For `m вүҘ n_0`, `n вүҘ n_0`, `f вҲҲ S_m`, `g вҲҲ S_n`, the relation `fg вҲҲ рқ”ӯ_{m + n}`
implies `f вҲҲ рқ”ӯ_m` or `g вҲҲ рқ”ӯ_n`.

3В° `рқ”ӯ_n вү  S_n` for at least one `n вүҘ n_0`.

Moreover, _the graded prime ideal `рқ”ӯ` is then unique_.

**Proof.** It is evident that conditions 1В° and 2В° are necessary. Moreover, if
`рқ”ӯ вҠ… S_+`, there exists at least one `k > 0` such that `рқ”ӯ вҲ© S_k вү  S_k`; if
`f вҲҲ S_k` is not in `рқ”ӯ`, the relation `рқ”ӯ вҲ© S_n = S_n` implies
`рқ”ӯ вҲ© S_{n вҲ’ mk} = S_{n вҲ’ mk}` by (2.1.8); hence, if `рқ”ӯ вҲ© S_n = S_n` from some value
of `n` onwards, we would have `рқ”ӯ вҠғ S_+` contrary to the hypothesis, which proves
that 3В° is necessary. Conversely, suppose conditions 1В°, 2В°, and 3В° are satisfied.
Note that if for an integer `d вүҘ n_0`, `f вҲҲ S_d` is not in `рқ”ӯ_d`, then, if `рқ”ӯ`
exists, `рқ”ӯ_m`, for `m < n_0`, is necessarily equal to the set of `x вҲҲ S_m` such
that `f^r x вҲҲ рқ”ӯ_{m + rd}`, except for finitely many values of `r`. This already
proves that if `рқ”ӯ` exists, it is unique. It remains to show

<!-- original page 23 -->

that if we define the `рқ”ӯ_m` for `m < n_0` by the previous condition, then
`рқ”ӯ = вҲ‘_{n = 0}^вҲһ рқ”ӯ_n` is a prime ideal. Note first that by virtue of 2В°, for
`m вүҘ n_0`, `рқ”ӯ_m` is also defined as the set of `x вҲҲ S_m` such that
`f^r x вҲҲ рқ”ӯ_{m + rd}` except for finitely many values of `r`. This being so, if
`g вҲҲ S_m`, `x вҲҲ рқ”ӯ_n`, we have `f^r gx вҲҲ рқ”ӯ_{m + n + rd}` except for finitely many
values of `r`, so `gx вҲҲ рқ”ӯ_{m + n}`, which proves that `рқ”ӯ` is an ideal of `S`. To
establish that this ideal is prime, in other words that the ring `S/рқ”ӯ`, graded by
the subgroups `S_n/рқ”ӯ_n`, is an integral domain, it suffices (by considering the
components of highest degree of two elements of `S/рқ”ӯ`) to prove that if `x вҲҲ S_m`,
`y вҲҲ S_n` are such that `x вҲү рқ”ӯ_m`, `y вҲү рқ”ӯ_n`, then `xy вҲү рқ”ӯ_{m + n}`. Otherwise,
for `r` large enough, we would have `f^{2r} xy вҲҲ рқ”ӯ_{m + n + 2rd}`; but
`f^r y вҲү рқ”ӯ_{n + rd}` for every `r > 0`; it follows from 2В° that, except for
finitely many values of `r`, we have `f^r x вҲҲ рқ”ӯ_{m + rd}`, and we conclude that
`x вҲҲ рқ”ӯ_m` contrary to the hypothesis.

**(2.1.10)**

<!-- label: II.2.1.10 -->

We say that a subset `рқ”Қ` of `S_+` is an _ideal of `S_+`_ if it is an ideal of `S`,
and that `рқ”Қ` is a _graded prime ideal of `S_+`_ if it is the intersection of `S_+`
and a graded prime ideal of `S` _not containing `S_+`_ (this prime ideal is also
unique by (2.1.9)). If `рқ”Қ` is an ideal of `S_+`, the _radical of `рқ”Қ` in `S_+`_ is
the set of elements of `S_+` having a power in `рқ”Қ`, in other words the set
`рқ”Ҝ_+(рқ”Қ) = рқ”Ҝ(рқ”Қ) вҲ© S_+`; in particular, the radical of `0` in `S_+` is called the
_nilradical_ of `S_+` and denoted `рқ”‘_+`: it is the set of nilpotent elements of
`S_+`. If `рқ”Қ` is a _graded_ ideal of `S_+`, its radical `рқ”Ҝ_+(рқ”Қ)` is a _graded_
ideal: passing to the quotient ring `S/рқ”Қ`, we reduce to the case `рқ”Қ = 0`, and it
remains to see that if `x = x_h + x_{h + 1} + вӢҜ + x_k` is nilpotent, then so are the
`x_i вҲҲ S_i` (`h вүӨ i вүӨ k`); we may assume `x_k вү  0`, and the component of highest
degree of `x^n` is then `x_k^n`, hence `x_k` is nilpotent, and we argue by
induction on `k`. We say that the graded ring `S` is _essentially reduced_ if
`рқ”‘_+ = 0`, in other words if `S_+` contains no nilpotent elements `вү  0`.

**(2.1.11)**

<!-- label: II.2.1.11 -->

We note that if, in the graded ring `S`, an element `x` is a zero-divisor, so is its
component of highest degree. We say that a ring `S` is _essentially integral_ if the
ring `S_+` (_without the unit element_) contains no zero-divisor and is `вү  0`; it
suffices for this that a homogeneous element `вү  0` in `S_+` not be a zero-divisor in
that ring. It is clear that if `рқ”ӯ` is a graded prime ideal of `S_+`, then `S/рқ”ӯ` is
essentially integral.

Let `S` be an essentially integral graded ring, and let `x_0 вҲҲ S_0`: if there exists
_a_ homogeneous element `f вү  0` of `S_+` such that `x_0 f = 0`, then `x_0 S_+ = 0`,
since `(x_0 g) f = (x_0 f) g = 0` for every `g вҲҲ S_+`, and the hypothesis thus
forces `x_0 g = 0`. For `S` to be integral, it is therefore necessary and sufficient
that `S_0` be integral and that the annihilator of `S_+` in `S_0` be reduced to `0`.

## 2.2. Rings of fractions of a graded ring

**(2.2.1)**

<!-- label: II.2.2.1 -->

Let `S` be a positively graded ring, `f` a _homogeneous_ element of `S` of degree
`d > 0`; then the ring of fractions `S' = S_f` is graded, taking for `S'_n` the set
of the `x/f^k`, where `x вҲҲ S_{n + kd}` with `k вүҘ 0` (here `n` may take arbitrary
negative values); we denote by `S_{(f)}` the subring `S'_0 = (S_f)_0` of `S'`
consisting of elements _of degree `0`_.

If `f вҲҲ S_d`, then the monomials `(f/1)^h` in `S_f` (`h` a positive or negative
integer) form a _free system_ over the ring `S_{(f)}`, and the set of their linear
combinations is none other than

<!-- original page 24 -->

the ring `(S^{(d)})_f`, which is therefore _isomorphic to
`S_{(f)}[T, TвҒ»В№] = S_{(f)} вҠ—_в„Ө в„Ө[T, TвҒ»В№]`_ (where `T` is an indeterminate). Indeed,
if we have a relation `вҲ‘_{h = вҲ’a}^b z_h (f/1)^h = 0` with `z_h = x_h/f^m`, where the
`x_h` are in `S_{md}`, then this relation is by definition equivalent to the
existence of a `k > вҲ’a` such that `вҲ‘_{h = вҲ’a}^b f^{h + k} x_h = 0`, and since the
degrees of the terms of this sum are distinct, we have `f^{h + k} x_h = 0` for
every `h`, whence `z_h = 0` for every `h`.

If `M` is a graded `S`-module, then `M' = M_f` is a graded `S_f`-module, `M'_n`
being the set of `z/f^k` with `z вҲҲ M_{n + kd}` (`k вүҘ 0`); we denote by `M_{(f)}` the
set of homogeneous elements of degree `0` of `M'`; it is immediate that `M_{(f)}` is
an `S_{(f)}`-module and that we have
`(M^{(d)})_f = M_{(f)} вҠ—_{S_{(f)}} (S^{(d)})_f`.

**Lemma.**

<!-- label: II.2.2.2 -->

Let `d`, `e` be integers `> 0`, `f вҲҲ S_d`, `g вҲҲ S_e`. There exists a canonical ring
isomorphism

```text
  S_{(fg)} вү… (S_{(f)})_{g^d/f^e};
```

if we canonically identify these two rings, there exists a canonical module
isomorphism

```text
  M_{(fg)} вү… (M_{(f)})_{g^d/f^e}.
```

**Proof.** Indeed, `fg` divides `f^e g^d`, and this latter element divides
`(fg)^{de}`, so the graded rings `S_{fg}` and `S_{f^e g^d}` are canonically
identified; on the other hand, `S_{f^e g^d}` also identifies with `(S_{f^e})_{g^d/1}`
`(0, 1.4.6)`, and since `f^e/1` is invertible in `S_{f^e}`, `S_{f^e g^d}` also
identifies with `(S_{f^e})_{g^d/f^e}`. Now the element `g^d/f^e` is of degree `0` in
`S_{f^e}`; we conclude immediately that the subring of `(S_{f^e})_{g^d/f^e}`
consisting of elements of degree `0` is `(S_{(f^e)})_{g^d/f^e}`, and since we
evidently have `S_{(f^e)} = S_{(f)}`, this proves the first part of the
proposition; the second is established in the same way.

**(2.2.3)**

<!-- label: II.2.2.3 -->

Under the hypotheses of (2.2.2), it is clear that the canonical homomorphism
`S_f вҶ’ S_{fg}` `(0, 1.4.1)`, which sends `x/f^k` to `g^k x/(fg)^k`, is of degree `0`,
and so by restriction yields a _canonical homomorphism `S_{(f)} вҶ’ S_{(fg)}`_, such
that the diagram

```text
            S_{(f)}
           /       \
          вҶ“         вҶ“
   S_{(fg)} в”Җв”ҖвҶ’ (S_{(f)})_{g^d/f^e}
```

commutes. We similarly define a canonical homomorphism `M_{(f)} вҶ’ M_{(fg)}`.

**Lemma.**

<!-- label: II.2.2.4 -->

If `f`, `g` are two homogeneous elements of `S_+`, the ring `S_{(fg)}` is generated
by the union of the canonical images of `S_{(f)}` and `S_{(g)}`.

**Proof.** By virtue of (2.2.2), it suffices to see that
`1/(g^d/f^e) = f^{d + e}/(fg)^d` belongs to the canonical image of `S_{(g)}` in
`S_{(fg)}`, which is evident by definition.

**Proposition.**

<!-- label: II.2.2.5 -->

Let `d` be an integer `> 0` and let `f вҲҲ S_d`. Then there exists a canonical ring
isomorphism `S_{(f)} вү… S^{(d)}/(f вҲ’ 1) S^{(d)}`; if we identify these two rings by
this isomorphism, there exists a canonical module isomorphism
`M_{(f)} вү… M^{(d)}/(f вҲ’ 1) M^{(d)}`.

**Proof.** The first of these isomorphisms is defined by sending `x/f^n`, where
`x вҲҲ S_{nd}`, to the element `xМ„`, the class of `x mod (f вҲ’ 1) S^{(d)}`; this map is
well-defined, since we have the congruence `f^h x вүЎ x (mod (f вҲ’ 1) S^{(d)})` for
every `x вҲҲ S^{(d)}`, so if `f^h x = 0` for some `h > 0`,

<!-- original page 25 -->

then we have `xМ„ = 0`. On the other hand, if `x вҲҲ S_{nd}` is such that `x = (f вҲ’ 1)y`
with `y = y_{hd} + y_{(h + 1)d} + вӢҜ + y_{kd}` with `y_{jd} вҲҲ S_{jd}` and
`y_{hd} вү  0`, then we necessarily have `h = n` and `x = вҲ’y_{hd}`, along with the
relations `y_{(j + 1)d} = f y_{jd}` for `h вүӨ j вүӨ k вҲ’ 1`, `f y_{kd} = 0`, which
finally gives `f^{k вҲ’ n} x = 0`; to every class `xМ„ mod (f вҲ’ 1) S^{(d)}` of an element
`x вҲҲ S_{nd}`, we can therefore associate the element `x/f^n` of `S_{(f)}`, since the
remark above shows that this map is well-defined. It is immediate that the two maps
so defined are ring homomorphisms, each the inverse of the other. We proceed in
exactly the same way for `M`.

**Corollary.**

<!-- label: II.2.2.6 -->

If `S` is Noetherian, so is `S_{(f)}` for every `f` homogeneous of degree `> 0`.

**Proof.** This follows immediately from (2.1.7) and (2.2.5).

**(2.2.7)**

<!-- label: II.2.2.7 -->

Let `T` be a multiplicative subset of `S_+` consisting of _homogeneous_ elements;
`T_0 = T вҲӘ {1}` is then a multiplicative subset of `S`; since the elements of `T_0`
are homogeneous, the ring `T_0вҒ»В№ S` is still graded in the evident way; we denote by
`S_{(T)}` the subring of `T_0вҒ»В№ S` consisting of elements of order `0`, that is, of
elements of the form `x/h`, where `h вҲҲ T` and `x` is homogeneous of degree equal to
that of `h`. We know `(0, 1.4.5)` that `T_0вҒ»В№ S` is canonically identified with the
inductive limit of the rings `S_f`, where `f` runs over `T` (for the canonical
homomorphisms `S_f вҶ’ S_{fg}`); since this identification respects degrees, it
identifies `S_{(T)}` with the _inductive limit_ of the `S_{(f)}` for `f вҲҲ T`. For
every graded `S`-module `M`, we similarly define the module `M_{(T)}` (over the ring
`S_{(T)}`) consisting of the elements of degree `0` of `T_0вҒ»В№ M`, and we see that
this module is the inductive limit of the `M_{(f)}` for `f вҲҲ T`.

If `рқ”ӯ` is a graded prime ideal of `S_+`, we denote by `S_{(рқ”ӯ)}` and `M_{(рқ”ӯ)}` the
ring `S_{(T)}` and the module `M_{(T)}` respectively, where `T` is the set of
_homogeneous_ elements of `S_+` not belonging to `рқ”ӯ`.

## 2.3. Homogeneous prime spectrum of a graded ring

**(2.3.1)**

<!-- label: II.2.3.1 -->

Given a positively graded ring `S`, we call the _homogeneous prime spectrum_ of `S`,
and denote by `Proj(S)`, the set of graded prime ideals of `S_+` (2.1.10), or
equivalently the set of graded prime ideals of `S` _not containing `S_+`_; we will
define a _scheme_ structure having `Proj(S)` as underlying set.

**(2.3.2)**

<!-- label: II.2.3.2 -->

For every subset `E` of `S`, let `V_+(E)` be the set of graded prime ideals of `S`
containing `E` and not containing `S_+`; this is thus the subset
`V(E) вҲ© Proj(S)` of `Spec(S)`. From `(I, 1.1.2)` we deduce:

```text
  V_+(0) = Proj(S), V_+(S) = V_+(S_+) = вҲ…                                 (2.3.2.1)
```

```text
  V_+(вӢғ_О» E_О») = вӢӮ_О» V_+(E_О»)                                             (2.3.2.2)
```

```text
  V_+(EE') = V_+(E) вҲӘ V_+(E')                                             (2.3.2.3)
```

We do not change `V_+(E)` by replacing `E` with the graded ideal generated by `E`;
moreover, if `рқ”Қ` is a graded ideal of `S`, we have

```text
  V_+(рқ”Қ) = V_+(вӢғ_{q вүҘ n} (рқ”Қ вҲ© S_q))                                       (2.3.2.4)
```

<!-- original page 26 -->

for every `n > 0`: indeed, if `рқ”ӯ вҲҲ Proj(S)` contains the homogeneous elements of `рқ”Қ`
of degree `вүҘ n`, then since by hypothesis there exists a homogeneous element
`f вҲҲ S_d` not contained in `рқ”ӯ`, for every `m вүҘ 0` and every `x вҲҲ S_m вҲ© рқ”Қ`, we have
`f^r x вҲҲ рқ”Қ вҲ© S_{m + rd}` for all but finitely many values of `r`, so
`f^r x вҲҲ рқ”ӯ вҲ© S_{m + rd}`, which implies `x вҲҲ рқ”ӯ вҲ© S_m` (2.1.9).

Finally, we have, for every graded ideal `рқ”Қ` of `S`,

```text
  V_+(рқ”Қ) = V_+(рқ”Ҝ_+(рқ”Қ)).                                                   (2.3.2.5)
```

**(2.3.3)**

<!-- label: II.2.3.3 -->

By definition, the `V_+(E)` are the closed subsets of `X = Proj(S)` for the topology
induced by the spectral topology of `Spec(S)`, which we will also call the _spectral
topology_ on `X`. For every `f вҲҲ S`, we set

```text
  D_+(f) = D(f) вҲ© Proj(S) = Proj(S) вҲ– V_+(f)                              (2.3.3.1)
```

and consequently, for two elements `f`, `g` of `S` `(I, 1.1.9.1)`,

```text
  D_+(fg) = D_+(f) вҲ© D_+(g).                                              (2.3.3.2)
```

**Proposition.**

<!-- label: II.2.3.4 -->

As `f` runs over the set of homogeneous elements of `S_+`, the `D_+(f)` form a base
for the topology of `X = Proj(S)`.

**Proof.** It follows from (2.3.2.2) and (2.3.2.4) that every closed subset of `X`
is the intersection of sets of the form `V_+(f)`, where `f` is homogeneous of
degree `> 0`.

**(2.3.5)**

<!-- label: II.2.3.5 -->

Let `f` be a _homogeneous_ element of `S_+`, of degree `d > 0`; for every graded
prime ideal `рқ”ӯ` of `S` not containing `f`, we know that the set of `x/f^n`, where
`x вҲҲ рқ”ӯ` and `n вүҘ 0`, is a prime ideal of the ring of fractions `S_f` `(0, 1.2.6)`;
its intersection with `S_{(f)}` is therefore a prime ideal of that ring, which we
denote by `ПҲ_f(рқ”ӯ)`: it is the set of `x/f^n` for `n вүҘ 0`, `x вҲҲ рқ”ӯ вҲ© S_{nd}`. We have
thus defined a map

```text
  ПҲ_f : D_+(f) вҶ’ Spec(S_{(f)});
```

moreover, if `g вҲҲ S_e` is another homogeneous element of `S_+`, we have a
commutative diagram

```text
   D_+(f)  в”Җв”ҖПҲ_fв”Җв”ҖвҶ’  Spec(S_{(f)})                                        (2.3.5.1)
     вҶ‘                    вҶ‘
   D_+(fg) в”Җв”ҖПҲ_{fg}в”Җв”ҖвҶ’ Spec(S_{(fg)})
```

where the left vertical arrow is the inclusion and the right one is the map
`бөғПү_{fg, f}` deduced from the canonical homomorphism
`Пү = Пү_{fg, f} : S_{(f)} вҶ’ S_{(fg)}` `(I, 1.2.1)`. Indeed, if
`x/f^n вҲҲ ПүвҒ»В№(ПҲ_{fg}(рқ”ӯ))`, where `fg вҲү рқ”ӯ`, then by definition
`g^n x/(fg)^n вҲҲ ПҲ_{fg}(рқ”ӯ)`, so `g^n x вҲҲ рқ”ӯ`, and hence `x вҲҲ рқ”ӯ`; the converse is
evident.

**Proposition.**

<!-- label: II.2.3.6 -->

The map `ПҲ_f` is a homeomorphism from `D_+(f)` to `Spec(S_{(f)})`.

**Proof.** First, `ПҲ_f` is continuous; for if `h вҲҲ S_{nd}` is such that
`h/f^n вҲҲ ПҲ_f(рқ”ӯ)`, then by definition `h вҲҲ рқ”ӯ` and conversely, so
`ПҲ_fвҒ»В№(D(h/f^n)) = D_+(hf)`, and our claim follows from (2.3.3.2). Furthermore, the
`D_+(hf)`, where `h` runs over the sets `S_{nd}`, form a base for the topology of
`D_+(f)` by (2.3.4) and (2.3.3.2); the

<!-- original page 27 -->

above thus proves, taking into account the (TвӮҖ) axiom valid in `D_+(f)` and in
`Spec(S_{(f)})`, that `ПҲ_f` is injective and that the inverse map
`ПҲ_f(D_+(f)) вҶ’ D_+(f)` is continuous. Finally, to see that `ПҲ_f` is surjective, we
remark that if `рқ”®_0` is a prime ideal of `S_{(f)}`, and if, for every `n > 0`, we
denote by `рқ”ӯ_n` the set of `x вҲҲ S_n` such that `x^d/f^n вҲҲ рқ”®_0`, then the `рқ”ӯ_n`
satisfy the conditions of (2.1.9): indeed, if `x вҲҲ S_n`, `y вҲҲ S_n` are such that
`x^d/f^n вҲҲ рқ”®_0` and `y^d/f^n вҲҲ рқ”®_0`, then `(x + y)^{2d}/f^{2n} вҲҲ рқ”®_0`, whence
`(x + y)^d/f^n вҲҲ рқ”®_0` since `рқ”®_0` is prime; this proves that the `рқ”ӯ_n` are
subgroups of the `S_n`, and the verification of the other conditions of (2.1.9) is
immediate, taking into account that `рқ”®_0` is prime. If `рқ”ӯ` is the graded prime ideal
of `S` thus defined, then indeed `ПҲ_f(рқ”ӯ) = рқ”®_0`, since for `x вҲҲ S_{nd}`, the
relations `x/f^n вҲҲ рқ”®_0` and `x^d/f^{nd} вҲҲ рқ”®_0` are equivalent, `рқ”®_0` being prime.

**Corollary.**

<!-- label: II.2.3.7 -->

For `D_+(f) = вҲ…`, it is necessary and sufficient that `f` be nilpotent.

**Proof.** For `Spec(S_{(f)}) = вҲ…`, it is necessary and sufficient that
`S_{(f)} = 0`, or again that `1 = 0` in `S_f`, which means by definition that `f` is
nilpotent.

**Corollary.**

<!-- label: II.2.3.8 -->

Let `E` be a subset of `S_+`. The following conditions are equivalent:

(a) `V_+(E) = X = Proj(S)`.

(b) Every element of `E` is nilpotent.

(c) The homogeneous components of every element of `E` are nilpotent.

**Proof.** It is clear that (c) implies (b) and that (b) implies (a). If `рқ”Қ` is the
graded ideal of `S` generated by `E`, condition (a) is equivalent to `V_+(рқ”Қ) = X`;
_a fortiori_, (a) implies that every homogeneous element `f вҲҲ рқ”Қ` is such that
`V_+(f) = X`, hence `f` is nilpotent by (2.3.7).

**Corollary.**

<!-- label: II.2.3.9 -->

If `рқ”Қ` is a graded ideal of `S_+`, `рқ”Ҝ_+(рқ”Қ)` is the intersection of the graded prime
ideals of `S_+` containing `рқ”Қ`.

**Proof.** Considering the graded ring `S/рқ”Қ`, we reduce to the case `рқ”Қ = 0`. We must
prove that if `f вҲҲ S_+` is not nilpotent, there is a graded prime ideal of `S` not
containing `f`; but at least one of the homogeneous components of `f` is not
nilpotent, so we may suppose `f` homogeneous; the assertion then follows from
(2.3.7).

**(2.3.10)**

<!-- label: II.2.3.10 -->

For every subset `Y` of `X = Proj(S)`, let `рқ”§_+(Y)` be the set of `f вҲҲ S_+` such
that `Y вҠӮ V_+(f)`; equivalently, `рқ”§_+(Y) = рқ”§(Y) вҲ© S_+`; then `рқ”§_+(Y)` is an ideal of
`S_+`, equal to its radical in `S_+`.

**Proposition.**

<!-- label: II.2.3.11 -->

(i) For every subset `E` of `S_+`, `рқ”§_+(V_+(E))` is the radical in `S_+` of the
graded ideal of `S_+` generated by `E`.

(ii) For every subset `Y` of `X`, `V_+(рқ”§_+(Y)) = ИІ`, the closure of `Y` in `X`.

**Proof.**

(i) If `рқ”Қ` is the graded ideal of `S_+` generated by `E`, then
`V_+(E) = V_+(рқ”Қ)`, and the assertion follows from (2.3.9).

(ii) Since `V_+(рқ”Қ) = вӢӮ_{f вҲҲ рқ”Қ} V_+(f)`, the relation `Y вҠӮ V_+(рқ”Қ)` implies
`Y вҠӮ V_+(f)` for every `f вҲҲ рқ”Қ`, and hence `рқ”§_+(Y) вҠғ рқ”Қ`, whence
`V_+(рқ”§_+(Y)) вҠӮ V_+(рқ”Қ)`, which proves (ii) by definition of the closed subsets.

**Corollary.**

<!-- label: II.2.3.12 -->

The closed subsets `Y` of `X = Proj(S)` and the graded ideals of `S_+` equal to
their radical in `S_+` correspond bijectively via the inclusion-reversing maps
`Y вҶҰ рқ”§_+(Y)`, `рқ”Қ вҶҰ V_+(рқ”Қ)`; to the union `Y_1 вҲӘ Y_2` of two closed subsets of `X`
corresponds

<!-- original page 28 -->

`рқ”§_+(Y_1) вҲ© рқ”§_+(Y_2)`, and to the intersection of an arbitrary family `(Y_О»)` of
closed subsets corresponds the radical in `S_+` of the sum of the `рқ”§_+(Y_О»)`.

**Corollary.**

<!-- label: II.2.3.13 -->

Let `рқ”Қ` be a graded ideal of `S_+`; for `V_+(рқ”Қ) = вҲ…`, it is necessary and sufficient
that every element of `S_+` have a power in `рқ”Қ`.

This last corollary can also be expressed in one of the equivalent forms:

**Corollary.**

<!-- label: II.2.3.14 -->

Let `(f_Оұ)` be a family of homogeneous elements of `S_+`. For the `D_+(f_Оұ)` to form
a cover of `X = Proj(S)`, it is necessary and sufficient that every element of `S_+`
have a power in the ideal generated by the `f_Оұ`.

**Corollary.**

<!-- label: II.2.3.15 -->

Let `(f_Оұ)` be a family of homogeneous elements of `S_+` and `f` an element of
`S_+`. The following are equivalent: (a) `D_+(f) вҠӮ вӢғ_Оұ D_+(f_Оұ)`;
(b) `V_+(f) вҠғ вӢӮ_Оұ V_+(f_Оұ)`; (c) a power of `f` belongs to the ideal generated by
the `f_Оұ`.

**Corollary.**

<!-- label: II.2.3.16 -->

For `X = Proj(S)` to be empty, it is necessary and sufficient that every element of
`S_+` be nilpotent.

**Corollary.**

<!-- label: II.2.3.17 -->

In the bijective correspondence described in (2.3.12), the _irreducible_ closed
subsets of `X` correspond to the graded _prime_ ideals of `S_+`.

**Proof.** Indeed, if `Y = Y_1 вҲӘ Y_2`, where `Y_1` and `Y_2` are closed and distinct
from `Y`, then

```text
  рқ”§_+(Y) = рқ”§_+(Y_1) вҲ© рқ”§_+(Y_2)
```

with the ideals `рқ”§_+(Y_1)` and `рқ”§_+(Y_2)` distinct from `рқ”§_+(Y)`, so `рқ”§_+(Y)` is not
prime. Conversely, if `рқ”Қ` is a graded ideal of `S_+` that is not prime, there exist
two elements `f`, `g` of `S_+` such that `f вҲү рқ”Қ`, `g вҲү рқ”Қ`, `fg вҲҲ рқ”Қ`; then
`V_+(f) вҠғ V_+(рқ”Қ)`, `V_+(g) вҠғ V_+(рқ”Қ)`, but `V_+(рқ”Қ) вҠӮ V_+(f) вҲӘ V_+(g)` by (2.3.2.3);
we conclude that `V_+(рқ”Қ)` is the union of the closed subsets `V_+(f) вҲ© V_+(рқ”Қ)` and
`V_+(g) вҲ© V_+(рқ”Қ)`, which are distinct from `V_+(рқ”Қ)`.

## 2.4. The scheme structure on `Proj(S)`

**(2.4.1)**

<!-- label: II.2.4.1 -->

Let `f`, `g` be two homogeneous elements of `S_+`; consider the affine schemes
`Y_f = Spec(S_{(f)})`, `Y_g = Spec(S_{(g)})`, and `Y_{fg} = Spec(S_{(fg)})`. By
(2.2.2), the morphism `w_{fg, f} = (бөғПү_{fg, f}, ПүМғ_{fg, f})` from `Y_{fg}` to `Y_f`,
corresponding to the canonical homomorphism `Пү_{fg, f} : S_{(f)} вҶ’ S_{(fg)}`, is an
_open immersion_ `(I, 1.3.6)`. By means of the inverse homeomorphism of
`ПҲ_f : D_+(f) вҶ’ Y_f` (2.3.6), we can transport the affine scheme structure of `Y_f`
to `D_+(f)`; by the commutativity of diagram (2.3.5.1), the affine scheme
`D_+(fg)` is thus identified with the scheme induced on the open subset `D_+(fg)` of
the underlying space of the affine scheme `D_+(f)`. It is then clear (taking (2.3.4)
into account) that `X = Proj(S)` is endowed with a unique _prescheme_ structure
whose restriction to each `D_+(f)` is the affine scheme just defined. Moreover:

**Proposition.**

<!-- label: II.2.4.2 -->

The prescheme `Proj(S)` is a scheme.

**Proof.** It suffices `(I, 5.5.6)` to show that, for any homogeneous `f`, `g` in
`S_+`, `D_+(f) вҲ© D_+(g) = D_+(fg)` is affine and that its ring is generated by the
canonical images of the rings of `D_+(f)` and `D_+(g)`; the first point is evident
by definition, and the second follows from (2.2.4).

<!-- original page 29 -->

Whenever we speak of the homogeneous prime spectrum `Proj(S)` as a _scheme_, it will
always be with respect to the structure just defined.

**Example.**

<!-- label: II.2.4.3 -->

Let `S = K[T_1, T_2]`, where `K` is a field and `T_1`, `T_2` are indeterminates, with
`S` graded by total degree. It follows from (2.3.14) that `Proj(S)` is the union of
`D_+(T_1)` and `D_+(T_2)`; we see immediately that these affine schemes are
canonically isomorphic to `K[T]`, and that `Proj(S)` is obtained by the gluing of
these two affine schemes described in `(I, 2.3.2)` (cf. (7.4.14)).

**Proposition.**

<!-- label: II.2.4.4 -->

Let `S` be a positively graded ring, and let `X` be the scheme `Proj(S)`.

(i) If `рқ”‘_+` is the nilradical of `S_+` (2.1.10), the scheme `X_red` is canonically
isomorphic to `Proj(S/рқ”‘_+)`; in particular, if `S` is essentially reduced, `Proj(S)`
is reduced.

(ii) Suppose `S` essentially reduced. Then for `X` to be integral, it is necessary
and sufficient that `S` be essentially integral.

**Proof.**

(i) Let `SМ„` be the graded ring `S/рқ”‘_+`, and denote by `x вҶҰ xМ„` the canonical
homomorphism `S вҶ’ SМ„`, of degree `0`. For every `f вҲҲ S_d` (`d > 0`), the canonical
homomorphism `S_f вҶ’ SМ„_{fМ„}` `(0, 1.5.1)` is surjective and of degree `0`, so by
restriction yields a surjective homomorphism `S_{(f)} вҶ’ SМ„_{(fМ„)}`; supposing
`f вҲү рқ”‘_+`, we verify immediately that `SМ„_{(fМ„)}` is reduced and that the kernel of
the previous homomorphism is the nilradical of `S_{(f)}`, in other words
`SМ„_{(fМ„)} = (S_{(f)})_red`. To this homomorphism therefore corresponds a closed
immersion `D_+(fМ„) вҶ’ D_+(f)` that identifies `D_+(fМ„)` with `(D_+(f))_red`
`(I, 5.1.2)`, and which is in particular a homeomorphism of the underlying spaces of
these two affine schemes. Furthermore, if `g вҲү рқ”‘_+` is another homogeneous element
of `S_+`, the diagram

```text
   D_+(fМ„)  в”Җв”ҖвҶ’  D_+(f)
     вҶ‘           вҶ‘
   D_+(fМ„бёЎ) в”Җв”ҖвҶ’  D_+(fg)
```

commutes; since moreover the `D_+(f)`, for `f` homogeneous of degree `> 0` and
`f вҲү рқ”‘_+`, form a cover of `X = Proj(S)` (2.3.7), we see that the morphisms
`D_+(fМ„) вҶ’ D_+(f)` are the restrictions of a closed immersion `Proj(SМ„) вҶ’ Proj(S)`
which is a homeomorphism of the underlying spaces; whence the conclusion
`(I, 5.1.2)`.

(ii) Suppose `S` essentially integral, in other words `(0)` is a graded prime
ideal of `S_+` distinct from `S_+`; then `X` is reduced by (i) and irreducible by
(2.3.17). Conversely, suppose `S` essentially reduced and `X` integral; then for
`f вү  0` homogeneous in `S_+`, we have `D_+(f) вү  вҲ…` (2.3.7); the hypothesis that `X`
is irreducible implies `D_+(f) вҲ© D_+(g) вү  вҲ…` for `f`, `g` homogeneous and `вү  0` in
`S_+`; hence `fg вү  0` by (2.3.3.2), and we conclude that `S_+` has no zero-divisors,
whence the first assertion.

**(2.4.5)**

<!-- label: II.2.4.5 -->

Given a commutative ring `A`, recall that a graded ring `S` is called a _graded
`A`-algebra_ if it is endowed with an `A`-algebra structure such that each of the
subgroups `S_n` is an `A`-module; it suffices for this that `S_0` be

<!-- original page 30 -->

an `A`-algebra, in other words we define on `S` a graded `A`-algebra structure by
defining an `A`-algebra structure on `S_0` and setting, for `Оұ вҲҲ A`, `x вҲҲ S_n`,
`Оұ В· x = (Оұ В· 1) x`.

**Proposition.**

<!-- label: II.2.4.6 -->

Suppose `S` is a graded `A`-algebra. Then on `X = Proj(S)`, the structure sheaf
`рқ’Ә_X` is an `A`-algebra (where `A` is considered as a simple sheaf on `X`); in other
words, `X` is a scheme over `Spec(A)`.

**Proof.** It suffices to note that for every `f` homogeneous in `S_+`, `S_{(f)}` is
an algebra over `A`, and that the diagram

```text
   S_{(f)} в”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”ҖвҶ’ S_{(fg)}
        вҶ–        вҶ—
           A
```

commutes, for `f`, `g` homogeneous in `S_+`.

**Proposition.**

<!-- label: II.2.4.7 -->

Let `S` be a positively graded ring.

(i) For every integer `d > 0`, there exists a canonical isomorphism of the scheme
`Proj(S)` onto the scheme `Proj(S^{(d)})`.

(ii) Let `S'` be the graded ring such that `S'_0 = в„Ө`, `S'_n = S_n` (considered as a
`в„Ө`-module) for `n > 0`. There exists a canonical isomorphism of the scheme
`Proj(S)` onto the scheme `Proj(S')`.

**Proof.**

(i) We first show that the map `рқ”ӯ вҶҰ рқ”ӯ вҲ© S^{(d)}` is a bijection from the set
`Proj(S)` to `Proj(S^{(d)})`. Indeed, suppose given a graded prime ideal
`рқ”ӯ' вҲҲ Proj(S^{(d)})`, and set `рқ”ӯ_{nd} = рқ”ӯ' вҲ© S_{nd}` (`n вүҘ 0`). For every `n > 0`
not a multiple of `d`, define `рқ”ӯ_n` as the set of `x вҲҲ S_n` such that
`x^d вҲҲ рқ”ӯ_{nd}`; if `x вҲҲ рқ”ӯ_n`, `y вҲҲ рқ”ӯ_n`, then `(x + y)^{2d} вҲҲ рқ”ӯ_{2nd}`, so
`(x + y)^d вҲҲ рқ”ӯ_{nd}` since `рқ”ӯ'` is prime; it is immediate that the `рқ”ӯ_n` so defined
for every `n вүҘ 0` satisfy the conditions of (2.1.9), hence there exists a unique
prime ideal `рқ”ӯ вҲҲ Proj(S)` such that `рқ”ӯ вҲ© S^{(d)} = рқ”ӯ'`. Since for every `f`
homogeneous in `S_+`, `V_+(f) = V_+(f^d)` (2.3.2.3), we see that the previous
bijection is a homeomorphism of topological spaces. Finally, with the same notation,
`S_{(f)}` and `S_{(f^d)}` are canonically identified (2.2.2), hence `Proj(S)` and
`Proj(S^{(d)})` are canonically identified as _schemes_.

(ii) If, to every `рқ”ӯ вҲҲ Proj(S)`, we associate the unique prime ideal
`рқ”ӯ' вҲҲ Proj(S')` such that `рқ”ӯ' вҲ© S_n = рқ”ӯ вҲ© S_n` for every `n > 0` (2.1.9), it is
clear that we define a canonical homeomorphism `Proj(S) вү… Proj(S')` of the
underlying spaces, since `V_+(f)` is the same set for `S` and for `S'` when `f` is
a homogeneous element of `S_+`. Since moreover `S_{(f)} = S'_{(f)}`, `Proj(S)` and
`Proj(S')` identify as _schemes_.

**Corollary.**

<!-- label: II.2.4.8 -->

If `S` is a graded `A`-algebra, `S'_A` the graded `A`-algebra such that
`(S'_A)_0 = A`, `(S'_A)_n = S_n` for `n > 0`, then there exists a canonical
isomorphism of `Proj(S)` onto `Proj(S'_A)`.

**Proof.** Indeed, these two schemes are both canonically isomorphic to `Proj(S')`,
with the notation of (2.4.7, (ii)).

## 2.5. The sheaf associated to a graded module

**(2.5.1)**

**Proposition.**

fortiori_ it is satisfied for every `f` homogeneous in `S_+` when `S_1` generates

the proposition.

## 2.7. Finiteness conditions

**Proposition.**

<!-- label: II.2.7.1 -->

(i) If `S` is a graded Noetherian ring, `X = Proj(S)` is a Noetherian scheme.

(ii) If `S` is a graded `A`-algebra of finite type, `X = Proj(S)` is a scheme of
finite type over `Y = Spec(A)`.

<!-- original page 39 -->

**Proof.**

(i) If `S` is Noetherian, the ideal `S_+` admits a finite system of homogeneous
generators `f_i` (`1 вүӨ i вүӨ p`), hence by (2.3.14) the underlying space `X` is the
union of the `D_+(f_i) = Spec(S_{(f_i)})`, and the matter reduces to showing that
each `S_{(f_i)}` is Noetherian, which follows from (2.2.6).

(ii) The hypothesis implies that `S_0` is an `A`-algebra of finite type and `S` an
`S_0`-algebra of finite type, hence `S_+` is an ideal of finite type (2.1.4). We
are thus reduced, as in (i), to proving that for `f вҲҲ S_d`, `S_{(f)}` is an
`A`-algebra of finite type. By virtue of (2.2.5), it suffices to show that
`S^{(d)}` is an `A`-algebra of finite type, which follows from (2.1.6).

**(2.7.2)**

<!-- label: II.2.7.2 -->

In what follows we consider the following finiteness conditions for a graded
`S`-module `M`:

(TF) There exists an integer `n` such that the submodule `вҠ•_{k вүҘ n} M_k` is an
`S`-module of finite type.

(TN) There exists an integer `n` such that `M_k = 0` for `k вүҘ n`.

If `M` satisfies (TN), then `M_{(f)} = 0` for every `f` homogeneous in `S_+`,
hence `MМғ = 0`.

Let `M`, `N` be two graded `S`-modules; we say that a homomorphism `u : M вҶ’ N` of
degree `0` is _(TN)-injective_ (resp. _(TN)-surjective_, _(TN)-bijective_) if there
exists an integer `n` such that `u_k : M_k вҶ’ N_k` is injective (resp. surjective,
bijective) for `k вүҘ n`. To say that `u` is (TN)-injective (resp. (TN)-surjective)
thus amounts to saying that `Ker u` (resp. `Coker u`) satisfies (TN). By virtue of
(2.5.4), if `u` is (TN)-injective (resp. (TN)-surjective, (TN)-bijective), then `Е©`
is injective (resp. surjective, bijective); when `u` is (TN)-bijective, we also say
that `u` is a _(TN)-isomorphism_.

**Proposition.**

<!-- label: II.2.7.3 -->

Let `S` be a graded ring such that the ideal `S_+` is of finite type, and let `M`
be a graded `S`-module.

(i) If `M` satisfies condition (TF), the `рқ’Ә_X`-module `MМғ` is of finite type.

(ii) Suppose `M` satisfies (TF); for `MМғ = 0`, it is necessary and sufficient that
`M` satisfy (TN).

**Proof.** We have just seen that condition (TN) implies `MМғ = 0`. If `M` satisfies
(TF), the graded submodule `M' = вҠ•_{k вүҘ n} M_k`, which is by hypothesis of finite
type, is such that `M/M'` satisfies (TN); we have therefore `(M/M')Мғ = 0`, and the
exactness of the functor `MМғ` (2.5.4) implies `MМғ = MМғ'`; to prove that `MМғ` is of
finite type, we are thus reduced to the case where `M` is _of finite type_. Since
the question is local, it suffices to prove that `M_{(f)}` is an `S_{(f)}`-module
of finite type `(I, 1.3.9)`; but `M^{(d)}` is an `S^{(d)}`-module of finite type
(2.1.6, (iii)), and our assertion then follows from (2.2.5).

Now suppose `M` satisfies (TF) and `MМғ = 0`; then with the same notation as above,
`MМғ' = 0`, and condition (TN) for `M'` is equivalent to condition (TN) for `M`, so
to prove that `MМғ = 0` implies `M` satisfies (TN), we may again reduce to the case
where `M` is generated by a finite number of homogeneous elements `x_i`
(`1 вүӨ i вүӨ p`); let `(f_j)_{1 вүӨ j вүӨ q}` be a system of homogeneous generators of the
ideal `S_+`. By hypothesis, `M_{(f_j)} = 0` for every `j`, hence there exists an
integer `n` such that `f_j^n x_i = 0` for any `i`, `j`. Let `n_j` be the degree of
`f_j`, and let `m` be the largest value of `вҲ‘_j r_j n_j` over the finite set of
systems of integers `(r_j)` such that `вҲ‘_j r_j вүӨ nq`; it is then clear

<!-- original page 40 -->

that if `k > m`, then `S_k x_i = 0` for every `i`; if `h` is the largest of the
degrees of the `x_i`, we conclude that `M_k = 0` for `k > h + m`, which finishes
the proof.

**Corollary.**

`N` and by (2.5.4), the restriction of the isomorphism `ОұМғ` to `Г‘` is an
isomorphism from `Г‘` onto `QМғ`, whence the conclusion by (2.6.5.1).

## 2.8. Functorial behaviour

**(2.8.1)**

<!-- label: II.2.8.1 -->

Let `S`, `S'` be two positively graded rings, and `ПҶ : S' вҶ’ S` a homomorphism of
graded rings. We denote by `G(ПҶ)` the open subset of `X = Proj(S)` complementary to
`V_+(ПҶ(S'_+))`, or, equivalently, the union of the `D_+(ПҶ(f'))` where `f'` runs
over the set of homogeneous elements of `S'_+`. The restriction to `G(ПҶ)` of the
continuous map `бөғПҶ` from `Spec(S)` to `Spec(S')` `(I, 1.2.1)` is therefore a
continuous map from `G(ПҶ)` to `Proj(S')`, which we again denote, by an abuse of
language, by `бөғПҶ`. If `f' вҲҲ S'_+` is homogeneous, we have

```text
  бөғПҶвҒ»В№(D_+(f')) = D_+(ПҶ(f'))                                             (2.8.1.1)
```

taking into account the fact that `бөғПҶ` sends `G(ПҶ)` to `Proj(S')`, and `(I, 1.2.2.2)`.
On the other hand, the homomorphism `ПҶ` canonically defines (with the same
notation) a homomorphism of graded rings `S'_{f'} вҶ’ S_f`, whence, by restriction to
the elements of degree `0`,

<!-- original page 42 -->

a homomorphism `S'_{(f')} вҶ’ S_{(f)}` which we denote by `ПҶ_{(f)}`; to it
corresponds `(I, 1.6.1)` a morphism of affine schemes
`(бөғПҶ_{(f)}, ПҶМғ_{(f)}) : Spec(S_{(f)}) вҶ’ Spec(S'_{(f')})`. If we canonically
identify `Spec(S_{(f)})` with the scheme induced by `Proj(S)` on `D_+(f)` (2.3.6),
we have defined a morphism `ОҰ_f : D_+(f) вҶ’ D_+(f')`, and `бөғПҶ_{(f)}` identifies with
the restriction of `бөғПҶ` to `D_+(f)`. It is also immediate that, if `g'` is another
homogeneous element of `S'_+` and `g = ПҶ(g')`, then the diagram

```text
   D_+(f)  в”Җв”ҖОҰ_fв”Җв”ҖвҶ’  D_+(f')
     вҶ‘                 вҶ‘
   D_+(fg) в”Җв”ҖОҰ_{fg}в”Җв”ҖвҶ’ D_+(f'g')
```

commutes, by the commutativity of the diagram

```text
   S'_{(f')}    в”Җв”ҖПҶ_{(f)}в”Җв”ҖвҶ’  S_{(f)}
       вҶ“ Пү_{f'g', f'}             вҶ“ Пү_{fg, f}
   S'_{(f'g')} в”Җв”ҖПҶ_{(fg)}в”Җв”ҖвҶ’ S_{(fg)}.
```

Taking into account the definition of `G(ПҶ)` and (2.3.3.2), we therefore see that:

**Proposition.**

<!-- label: II.2.8.2 -->

Given a homomorphism of graded rings `ПҶ : S' вҶ’ S`, there exists one and only one
morphism `(бөғПҶ, ПҶМғ)` from the induced prescheme `G(ПҶ)` to `Proj(S')` (said to be
_associated to `ПҶ`_, and denoted by `Proj(ПҶ)`) such that, for every homogeneous
element `f' вҲҲ S'_+`, the restriction of this morphism to `D_+(ПҶ(f'))` agrees with
the morphism associated to the homomorphism `S'_{(f')} вҶ’ S_{(ПҶ(f'))}` corresponding
to `ПҶ`.

**Proof.** With the above notation, if `f' вҲҲ S'_d`, the diagram

```text
   S'_{(f')}                  в”Җв”ҖПҶ_{(f)}в”Җв”ҖвҶ’  S_{(f)}                       (2.8.2.1)
       вҶ“ вү…                                    вҶ“ вү…
   S'^{(d)}/(f' вҲ’ 1) S'^{(d)} в”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”ҖвҶ’  S^{(d)}/(f вҲ’ 1) S^{(d)}
```

commutes (the vertical arrows being the isomorphisms (2.2.5)).

**Corollary.**

<!-- label: II.2.8.3 -->

(i) The morphism `Proj(ПҶ)` is affine.

(ii) If `Ker(ПҶ)` is nilpotent (and in particular if `ПҶ` is injective), the morphism
`Proj(ПҶ)` is dominant.

**Proof.** Claim (i) is an immediate consequence of (2.8.2) and (2.8.1.1). On the
other hand, if `Ker(ПҶ)` is nilpotent, for every `f'` homogeneous in `S'_+`, we
verify at once that `Ker(ПҶ_f)` (with `f = ПҶ(f')`) is nilpotent, hence so too is
`Ker(ПҶ_{(f)})`; the conclusion follows from (2.8.2) and from `(I, 1.2.7)`.

<!-- original page 43 -->

We note that there exist in general morphisms from `Proj(S)` to `Proj(S')` that are
not affine, and which therefore do not come from homomorphisms of graded rings
`S' вҶ’ S`; an example is the structure morphism `Proj(S) вҶ’ Spec(A)` when `A` is a
field (`Spec(A)` then being identified with `Proj(A[T])`, cf. (3.1.7)); this indeed
follows from `(I, 2.3.2)`.

**(2.8.4)**

<!-- label: II.2.8.4 -->

Let `S''` be a third positively graded ring, `ПҶ' : S'' вҶ’ S'` a homomorphism of
graded rings, and set `ПҶ'' = ПҶ вҲҳ ПҶ'`. By virtue of (2.8.1.1) and the formula
`бөғПҶ'' = (бөғПҶ') вҲҳ (бөғПҶ)`, we verify at once that `G(ПҶ'') вҠӮ G(ПҶ)` and that, if `ОҰ`,
`ОҰ'`, `ОҰ''` are the morphisms associated to `ПҶ`, `ПҶ'`, `ПҶ''` respectively, then
`ОҰ'' = ОҰ' вҲҳ (ОҰ | G(ПҶ''))`.

**(2.8.5)**

<!-- label: II.2.8.5 -->

Suppose `S` (resp. `S'`) is a graded `A`-algebra (resp. a graded `A'`-algebra), and
let `ПҲ : A' вҶ’ A` be a ring homomorphism such that the diagram

```text
   A' в”Җв”ҖПҲв”Җв”ҖвҶ’ A                                                            (2.8.5.1)
   вҶ“         вҶ“
   S' в”Җв”ҖПҶв”Җв”ҖвҶ’ S
```

commutes. We can then consider `G(ПҶ)` (resp. `Proj(S')`) as a scheme over
`Spec(A)` (resp. `Spec(A')`); if `ОҰ` (resp. `ОЁ`) is the morphism associated to `ПҶ`
(resp. `ПҲ`), the diagram

```text
   G(ПҶ)     в”Җв”ҖОҰв”Җв”ҖвҶ’  Proj(S')
     вҶ“                 вҶ“
   Spec(A) в”Җв”ҖОЁв”Җв”ҖвҶ’ Spec(A')
```

commutes: it suffices to prove this for the restriction of `ОҰ` to `D_+(f)`, where
`f = ПҶ(f')`, `f'` homogeneous in `S'_+`; this then follows from the commutativity
of the diagram

```text
   A'         в”Җв”ҖПҲв”Җв”ҖвҶ’  A
   вҶ“                   вҶ“
   S'_{(f')} в”Җв”ҖПҶ_{(f)}в”Җв”ҖвҶ’ S_{(f)}.
```

**(2.8.6)**

<!-- label: II.2.8.6 -->

Now let `M` be a graded `S`-module, and consider the `S'`-module `M_{[ПҶ]}`, which is
evidently graded. Let `f'` be homogeneous in `S'_+`, and set `f = ПҶ(f')`; we know
`(0, 1.5.2)` that there is a canonical isomorphism
`(M_{[ПҶ]})_{f'} вү… (M_f)_{[ПҶ_f]}`, and it is immediate that this isomorphism
preserves degrees, whence a canonical isomorphism
`(M_{[ПҶ]})_{(f')} вү… (M_{(f)})_{[ПҶ_{(f)}]}`. To this isomorphism there canonically
corresponds an isomorphism of sheaves
`(M_{[ПҶ]})Мғ | D_+(f') вү… (ОҰ_f)_*(MМғ | D_+(f))` ((2.5.2) and `(I, 1.6.3)`).
Moreover,

<!-- original page 44 -->

if `g'` is another homogeneous element of `S'_+` and `g = ПҶ(g')`, the diagram

```text
   (M_{[ПҶ]})_{(f')}   в”Җв”Җвү…в”Җв”ҖвҶ’  (M_{(f)})_{[ПҶ_{(f)}]}
       вҶ“                              вҶ“
```

commutes, whence we immediately conclude that the isomorphism
`(M_{[ПҶ]})Мғ | D_+(f'g') вү… (ОҰ_{fg})_*(MМғ | D_+(fg))` is the restriction to
`D_+(f'g')` of the isomorphism
`(M_{[ПҶ]})Мғ | D_+(f') вү… (ОҰ_f)_*(MМғ | D_+(f))`. Since `ОҰ_f` is the restriction to
`D_+(f)` of the morphism `ОҰ`, we see, taking (2.8.1.1) into account, and setting
`X' = Proj(S')`:

**Proposition.**

<!-- label: II.2.8.7 -->

There exists a canonical functorial isomorphism from the `рқ’Ә_{X'}`-module `(M_{[ПҶ]})Мғ`
onto the `рқ’Ә_{X'}`-module `ОҰ_*(MМғ | G(ПҶ))`.

We deduce immediately a canonical functorial map from the set of `ПҶ`-morphisms
`M' вҶ’ M` from a graded `S'`-module `M'` to the graded `S`-module `M`, to the set of
`ОҰ`-morphisms `MМғ' вҶ’ MМғ | G(ПҶ)`. With the notation of (2.8.4), if `M''` is a graded
`S''`-module, then to the composition of a `ПҶ`-morphism `M' вҶ’ M` and a
`ПҶ'`-morphism `M'' вҶ’ M'` canonically corresponds the composition of
`MМғ' | G(ПҶ'') вҶ’ MМғ | G(ПҶ'')` and `MМғ'' вҶ’ MМғ' | G(ПҶ')`.

**Proposition.**

<!-- label: II.2.8.8 -->

Under the hypotheses of (2.8.1), let `M'` be a graded `S'`-module. There exists a
canonical functorial homomorphism `ОҪ` from the `(рқ’Ә_X | G(ПҶ))`-module `ОҰ*(MМғ')` to
the `(рқ’Ә_X | G(ПҶ))`-module `(M' вҠ—_{S'} S)Мғ | G(ПҶ)`. If the ideal `S'_+` is generated
by `S'_1`, then `ОҪ` is an isomorphism.

**Proof.** Indeed, for `f' вҲҲ S'_d` (`d > 0`), we define a canonical functorial
homomorphism of `S_{(f)}`-modules (where `f = ПҶ(f')`)

```text
  ОҪ_f : M'_{(f')} вҠ—_{S'_{(f')}} S_{(f)} вҶ’ (M' вҠ—_{S'} S)_{(f)}             (2.8.8.1)
```

by composing the homomorphism
`M'_{(f')} вҠ—_{S'_{(f')}} S_{(f)} вҶ’ M'_{f'} вҠ—_{S'_{f'}} S_f` and the canonical
isomorphism `M'_{f'} вҠ—_{S'_{f'}} S_f вү… (M' вҠ—_{S'} S)_f` `(0, 1.5.4)`, and noting
that the latter preserves degrees. We verify at once the compatibility of the
`ОҪ_f` with the restriction operators from `D_+(f)` to `D_+(fg)` (for another
`g' вҲҲ S'_+` and `g = ПҶ(g')`), whence the definition of the homomorphism

```text
  ОҪ : ОҰ*(MМғ') вҶ’ (M' вҠ—_{S'} S)Мғ | G(ПҶ)
```

taking `(I, 1.6.5)` into account. To prove the second assertion, it suffices to
show that `ОҪ_f` is an isomorphism for every `f' вҲҲ S'_1`, since `G(ПҶ)` is then a
union of the `D_+(ПҶ(f'))`. We first define a `в„Ө`-bilinear map
`M'_m Г— S_n вҶ’ M'_{(f')} вҠ—_{S'_{(f')}} S_{(f)}` by sending `(x', s)` to the element
`(x'/f'^m) вҠ— (s/f^n)` (with the convention that `x'/f'^m` means `f'^{вҲ’m} x'/1`

<!-- original page 45 -->

when `m < 0`). One observes, as in the proof of (2.5.13), that this map gives rise
to a di-homomorphism of modules

```text
  О·_f : M' вҠ—_{S'} S вҶ’ M'_{(f')} вҠ—_{S'_{(f')}} S_{(f)}.
```

Moreover, if for some `r > 0` we have `f'^r вҲ‘_i (x'_i вҠ— s_i) = 0`, this can also be
written `вҲ‘_i (f'^r x'_i вҠ— s_i) = 0`, whence by `(0, 1.5.4)`,
`вҲ‘_i (f'^r x'_i/f'^{m_i + r}) вҠ— (s_i/f^{n_i}) = 0`, i.e.
`О·_f(вҲ‘_i x'_i вҠ— s_i) = 0`, which proves that `О·_f` factors as
`M' вҠ—_{S'} S вҶ’ (M' вҠ—_{S'} S)_f вҶ’ M'_{(f')} вҠ—_{S'_{(f')}} S_{(f)}`; one finally
verifies that `О·'_f` and `ОҪ_f` are inverse isomorphisms of one another.

In particular, it follows from (2.1.2.1) that we have a canonical homomorphism


for every `n вҲҲ в„Ө`.

**(2.8.9)**

<!-- label: II.2.8.9 -->

Let `A`, `A'` be two rings, `ПҲ : A' вҶ’ A` a ring homomorphism, defining a morphism
`ОЁ : Spec(A) вҶ’ Spec(A')`. Let `S'` be a positively graded `A'`-algebra, and set

making the diagram (2.8.5.1) commute. Since here `S_+` is the `A`-module generated
by `ПҶ(S'_+)`, we have `G(ПҶ) = Proj(S) = X`; whence, setting `X' = Proj(S')`, a
commutative diagram

```text
   X  в”Җв”ҖОҰв”Җв”ҖвҶ’  X'                                                          (2.8.9.1)
   вҶ“p         вҶ“
   Y  в”Җв”ҖОЁв”Җв”ҖвҶ’  Y'
```

Now let `M'` be a graded `S'`-module, and set `M = M' вҠ—_{A'} A = M' вҠ—_{S'} S`.
Under these conditions:

**Proposition.**

<!-- label: II.2.8.10 -->

The diagram (2.8.9.1) identifies the scheme `X` with the product `X' Г—_{Y'} Y`;
moreover, the canonical homomorphism `ОҪ : ОҰ*(MМғ') вҶ’ MМғ` (2.8.8) is an isomorphism.

in other words, it suffices `(I, 3.2.2)` to prove that `S_{(f)}` identifies
canonically with `S'_{(f')} вҠ—_{A'} A`, which is immediate by virtue of the
existence of the canonical isomorphism `S_f вү… S'_{f'} вҠ—_{A'} A` that preserves
degrees `(0, 1.5.4)`. The second assertion follows from the fact that
`M'_{(f')} вҠ—_{S'_{(f')}} S_{(f)}` identifies by what precedes with
`M'_{(f')} вҠ—_{A'} A`, and this latter identifies with `M_{(f)}`, since `M_f`
identifies canonically with `M'_{f'} вҠ—_{A'} A` by an isomorphism preserving
degrees.

**Corollary.**

<!-- label: II.2.8.11 -->

For every integer `n вҲҲ в„Ө`, `MМғ(n)` identifies with
`ОҰ*(MМғ'(n)) = MМғ'(n) вҠ—_{Y'} рқ’Ә_Y`; in particular,
`рқ’Ә_X(n) = ОҰ*(рқ’Ә_{X'}(n)) = рқ’Ә_{X'}(n) вҠ—_{Y'} рқ’Ә_Y`.

**Proof.** This follows from (2.8.10) and (2.5.15).

**(2.8.12)**

<!-- label: II.2.8.12 -->

Under the hypotheses of (2.8.9), for `f' вҲҲ S'_d` (`d > 0`) and `f = ПҶ(f')`, the

## 2.9. Closed subschemes of a scheme `Proj(S)`

**(2.9.1)**

<!-- label: II.2.9.1 -->

If `ПҶ : S вҶ’ S'` is a homomorphism of graded rings, we say that `ПҶ` is
_(TN)-surjective_ (resp. _(TN)-injective_, _(TN)-bijective_) if there exists an
integer `n` such that, for `k вүҘ n`, `ПҶ_k : S_k вҶ’ S'_k` is _surjective_ (resp.
_injective_, _bijective_). Then it follows from (2.8.15) that the study of `ОҰ`
reduces to the case where `ПҶ` is surjective (resp. injective, bijective). Instead
of saying that `ПҶ` is (TN)-bijective, we then also say that it is a
_(TN)-isomorphism_.

**Proposition.**

<!-- label: II.2.9.2 -->

Let `S` be a positively graded ring, and let `X = Proj(S)`.

(i) If `ПҶ : S вҶ’ S'` is a (TN)-surjective homomorphism of graded rings, the
corresponding morphism `ОҰ` (2.8.1) is defined on the whole of `Proj(S')` and is a
closed immersion of `Proj(S')` into `X`. If `рқ”Қ` is the kernel of `ПҶ`, the closed
subscheme of `X` associated to `ОҰ` is defined by the quasi-coherent sheaf of ideals
`рқ”ҚМғ` of `рқ’Ә_X`.

(ii) Suppose moreover the ideal `S_+` is generated by a finite number of
homogeneous elements of degree `1`. Let `X'` be a closed subscheme of `X` defined
by a quasi-coherent sheaf of ideals `рқ’Ҙ` of `рқ’Ә_X`. Let `рқ”Қ` be the graded ideal of
`S` given by the inverse image of `О“_вҖў(рқ’Ҙ)` under the canonical homomorphism
`Оұ : S вҶ’ О“_вҖў(рқ’Ә_X)` (2.6.2), and set `S' = S/рқ”Қ`. Then `X'` is the subscheme
associated to the closed immersion `Proj(S') вҶ’ X` corresponding to the canonical
homomorphism of graded rings `S вҶ’ S'`.

**Proof.**

(i) We may suppose `ПҶ` surjective (2.9.1). Since by hypothesis `ПҶ(S_+)` generates
`S'_+`, we have `G(ПҶ) = Proj(S')`. On the other hand, the second assertion is
verified locally on `X`; so let `f` be a homogeneous element of `S_+`, and set
`f' = ПҶ(f)`. Since `ПҶ` is a surjective homomorphism of graded rings, we verify at
once that `ПҶ_{(f)} : S_{(f)} вҶ’ S'_{(f')}` is surjective and that its kernel is
`рқ”Қ_{(f)}`, which finishes the proof of (i) `(I, 4.2.3)`.

(ii) By virtue of (i), we are reduced to verifying that the homomorphism
`jМғ : рқ”ҚМғ вҶ’ рқ’Ә_X` deduced from the canonical injection `j : рқ”Қ вҶ’ S` is an isomorphism
from `рқ”ҚМғ` onto `рқ’Ҙ`, which follows from (2.7.11).

We note that `рқ”Қ` is the _largest_ of the graded ideals `рқ”Қ'` of `S` such that
`jМғ(рқ”ҚМғ') = рқ’Ҙ`, since we verify immediately by going back to the definitions (2.6.2)
that this relation implies `Оұ(рқ”Қ') вҠӮ О“_вҖў(рқ’Ҙ)`.

**Corollary.**

<!-- label: II.2.9.3 -->

Suppose the hypotheses of (2.9.2, (i)) are satisfied, and moreover that the ideal
`S_+` is generated by `S_1`; then `ОҰ*((S(n))Мғ)` is canonically isomorphic to
`(S'(n))Мғ` for every `n вҲҲ в„Ө`, and consequently `ОҰ*(в„ұ(n))` is canonically isomorphic
to `ОҰ*(в„ұ)(n)` for every `рқ’Ә_X`-module `в„ұ`.

**Proof.** This is a particular case of (2.8.8), taking (2.5.10.2) into account.

**Corollary.**

<!-- label: II.2.9.4 -->

Suppose the hypotheses of (2.9.2, (ii)) are satisfied. For the closed
sub-prescheme `X'` of `X` to be integral, it is necessary and sufficient that the
graded ideal `рқ”Қ` be prime in `S`.

<!-- original page 49 -->

**Proof.** Since `X'` is isomorphic to `Proj(S/рқ”Қ)`, the condition is sufficient by
virtue of (2.4.4). To see that it is necessary, consider the exact sequence
`0 вҶ’ рқ’Ҙ вҶ’ рқ’Ә_X вҶ’ рқ’Ә_X/рқ’Ҙ`, which gives the exact sequence

```text
  0 вҶ’ О“_вҖў(рқ’Ҙ) вҶ’ О“_вҖў(рқ’Ә_X) вҶ’ О“_вҖў(рқ’Ә_X/рқ’Ҙ).
```

It suffices to prove that if `f вҲҲ S_m`, `g вҲҲ S_n` are such that the image in
`О“_вҖў(рқ’Ә_X/рқ’Ҙ)` of `Оұ_{n + m}(fg)` is zero, then one of the images of `Оұ_m(f)`,
`Оұ_n(g)` is zero. But by definition, these images are sections of invertible
`(рқ’Ә_X/рқ’Ҙ)`-modules `в„’ = (рқ’Ә_X/рқ’Ҙ)(m)` and `в„’' = (рқ’Ә_X/рқ’Ҙ)(n)` over the integral scheme
`X'`; the hypothesis implies that the product of these two sections is zero in
`в„’ вҠ— в„’'` ((2.9.3) and (2.5.14.1)), so one of them is zero by virtue of
`(I, 7.4.4)`.

**Corollary.**

<!-- label: II.2.9.5 -->

Let `A` be a ring, `M` an `A`-module, `S` a graded `A`-algebra generated by the set
`S_1` of homogeneous elements of degree `1`, `u : M вҶ’ S_1` a surjective
homomorphism of `A`-modules, and `Е« : рқ•Ҡ(M) вҶ’ S` the homomorphism (of `A`-algebras)
from the symmetric algebra `рқ•Ҡ(M)` of `M` to `S` that extends `u`. Then the morphism
corresponding to `Е«` is a closed immersion of `Proj(S)` into `Proj(рқ•Ҡ(M))`.

**Proof.** Indeed, `Е«` is surjective by hypothesis, and it suffices to apply
(2.9.2).
