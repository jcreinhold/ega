# В§8. Blow-up preschemes; projecting cones; projective closure

## 8.1. Blow-up preschemes

**(8.1.1)**

<!-- label: II.8.1.1 -->

Let `Y` be a prescheme, and, for every integer `n вүҘ 0`, let `в„җвӮҷ` be a quasi-coherent
sheaf of ideals of `рқ’Ә_Y`; suppose that the following conditions are satisfied:

```text
  в„җвӮҖ = рқ’Ә_Y,    в„җвӮҷ вҠӮ в„җвӮҳ    for m вүӨ n,                                       (8.1.1.1)
```

```text
  в„җвӮҳ В· в„җвӮҷ вҠӮ в„җ_{m+n}     for any m, n.                                       (8.1.1.2)
```

<!-- original page 153 -->

Note that these hypotheses imply

```text
  в„җвӮҒвҒҝ вҠӮ в„җвӮҷ.                                                                 (8.1.1.3)
```

Set

```text
  рқ’® = вҠ•_{nвүҘ0} в„җвӮҷ.                                                            (8.1.1.4)
```

It follows from (8.1.1.1) and (8.1.1.2) that `рқ’®` is a quasi-coherent graded
`рқ’Ә_Y`-algebra, and therefore defines a `Y`-scheme `X = Proj(рқ’®)`. If `рқ’Ҙ` is an
_invertible_ sheaf of ideals of `рқ’Ә_Y`, then `в„җвӮҷ вҠ—_{рқ’Ә_Y} рқ’Ҙ^{вҠ— n}` is canonically
identified with `в„җвӮҷ В· рқ’ҘвҒҝ`. If we then replace the `в„җвӮҷ` by the `в„җвӮҷ В· рқ’ҘвҒҝ`, and in doing
so replace `рқ’®` by a quasi-coherent `рқ’Ә_Y`-algebra `рқ’®_{(рқ’Ҙ)}`, then
`X_{(рқ’Ҙ)} = Proj(рқ’®_{(рқ’Ҙ)})` is canonically isomorphic to `X` (3.1.8).

**(8.1.2)**

<!-- label: II.8.1.2 -->

Suppose `Y` is _locally integral_, so that the sheaf `в„ӣ(Y)` of rational functions is
a quasi-coherent `рқ’Ә_Y`-algebra `(I, 7.3.7)`. We say that an `рқ’Ә_Y`-submodule `в„җ` of
`в„ӣ(Y)` is a _fractional ideal_ of `в„ӣ(Y)` if it is _of finite type_ `(0, 5.2.1)`.
Suppose given, for every `n вүҘ 0`, a quasi-coherent fractional ideal `в„җвӮҷ` of `в„ӣ(Y)`
such that `в„җвӮҖ = рқ’Ә_Y` and such that condition (8.1.1.2) is satisfied (but not
necessarily the second condition (8.1.1.1)); we can then again define, by formula
(8.1.1.4), a quasi-coherent graded `рқ’Ә_Y`-algebra and the corresponding `Y`-scheme
`X = Proj(рқ’®)`. We again have a canonical isomorphism from `X` to `X_{(рқ’Ҙ)}` for every
_invertible_ fractional ideal `рқ’Ҙ` of `в„ӣ(Y)`.

**Definition.**

<!-- label: II.8.1.3 -->

Let `Y` be a prescheme (resp. a locally integral prescheme), and let `в„җ` be a
quasi-coherent sheaf of ideals of `рқ’Ә_Y` (resp. a quasi-coherent fractional ideal
of `в„ӣ(Y)`). We say that the `Y`-scheme `X = Proj(вҠ•_{nвүҘ0} в„җвҒҝ)` is _obtained by
blowing up the ideal `в„җ`_, or is the _blow-up prescheme of `Y` relative to `в„җ`_.
When `в„җ` is a quasi-coherent sheaf of ideals of `рқ’Ә_Y` and `YвҖІ` the closed
subprescheme of `Y` defined by `в„җ`, we also say that `X` is the `Y`-scheme obtained
by _blowing up `YвҖІ`_.

By definition, `рқ’® = вҠ•_{nвүҘ0} в„җвҒҝ` is then generated by `рқ’®вӮҒ = в„җ`; if `в„җ` is an
`рқ’Ә_Y`-module of _finite type_, then `X` is _projective_ over `Y` (5.5.2). Without any
hypothesis on `в„җ`, the `рқ’Ә_X`-module `рқ’Ә_X(1)` is _invertible_ (3.2.5) and
_very ample_, by (4.4.3) applied to the structure morphism `X вҶ’ Y`.

We note that, if `f : X вҶ’ Y` is the structure morphism, then the restriction of `f`
to `fвҒ»В№(Y вҲ– YвҖІ)` is an _isomorphism_ onto `Y вҲ– YвҖІ` whenever `в„җ` is an ideal of `рқ’Ә_Y`
and `YвҖІ` is the closed subprescheme it defines: indeed, since the question is local
on `Y`, it suffices to assume that `в„җ = рқ’Ә_Y`, and our claim then follows from
(3.1.7).

If we replace `в„җ` by `в„җ^d` (`d > 0`), the blow-up `Y`-scheme `X` is replaced by a
canonically isomorphic `Y`-scheme `XвҖІ` (8.1.1); similarly, for every _invertible_
ideal (resp. _invertible_ fractional ideal) `рқ’Ҙ`, the blow-up prescheme `X_{(рқ’Ҙ)}`
relative to the ideal `в„җрқ’Ҙ` is canonically isomorphic to `X` (8.1.1).

In particular, whenever `в„җ` is an _invertible_ ideal (resp. _invertible_ fractional
ideal), the `Y`-scheme obtained by blowing up `в„җ` is _isomorphic to `Y`_ (3.1.7).

**Proposition.**

<!-- label: II.8.1.4 -->

Let `Y` be an integral prescheme.

1. For every sequence `(в„җвӮҷ)` of quasi-coherent fractional ideals of `в„ӣ(Y)` satisfying
    (8.1.1.2)

    <!-- original page 154 -->

    and such that `в„җвӮҖ = рқ’Ә_Y`, the `Y`-scheme `X = Proj(вҠ•_{nвүҘ0} в„җвӮҷ)` is integral and
    the structure morphism `f : X вҶ’ Y` is dominant.
1. Let `в„җ` be a quasi-coherent fractional ideal of `в„ӣ(Y)`, and let `X` be the
    `Y`-scheme given by the blow-up of `Y` relative to `в„җ`. If `в„җ вү  0`, then the
    structure morphism `f : X вҶ’ Y` is birational and surjective.

**Proof.**

(i) This follows from the fact that `рқ’® = вҠ•_{nвүҘ0} в„җвӮҷ` is an _integral_
`рқ’Ә_Y`-algebra (3.1.12 and 3.1.14), since for every `y вҲҲ Y`, `рқ’Ә_y` is an integral
ring `(I, 5.1.4)`.

(ii) By (i), `X` is integral; if, furthermore, `x` and `y` are the generic points of
`X` and `Y` (respectively), then `f(x) = y`, and it remains to show that `Оә(x)` is
of rank 1 over `Оә(y)`. But `x` is also the generic point of the fibre `fвҒ»В№(y)`; if
`ПҲ` is the canonical morphism `Z вҶ’ Y`, where `Z = Spec(Оә(y))`, then the prescheme
`fвҒ»В№(y)` is identified with `Proj(рқ’®вҖІ)`, where `рқ’®вҖІ = ПҲ*(рқ’®)` (3.5.3). It is clear that
`рқ’®вҖІ = вҠ•_{nвүҘ0} (в„җ_y)вҒҝ`, and since `в„җ` is a quasi-coherent fractional ideal of `в„ӣ(Y)`
that is not zero, `в„җ_y вү  0` `(I, 7.3.6)`, whence `в„җ_y = Оә(y)`; then `Proj(рқ’®вҖІ)` is
identified with `Spec(Оә(y))` (3.1.7), whence the conclusion.

We will prove a _converse_ of (8.1.4) in `(III, 2.3.8)`.

**Proposition.**

<!-- label: II.8.1.7 -->

Let `Y` be a prescheme, `в„җ` a quasi-coherent sheaf of ideals of `рқ’Ә_Y`, and
`X = Proj(вҠ•_{nвүҘ0} в„җвҒҝ)` the `Y`-scheme obtained by blowing up `в„җ`. Then, for every
`n > 0`, there is a canonical isomorphism

```text
  рқ’Ә_X(n) вҘІ в„җвҒҝ В· рқ’Ә_X = в„җ_{n,X}                                                (8.1.7.1)
```

(cf. `(0, 4.3.5)`), and consequently `в„җвҒҝ В· рқ’Ә_X` is a very ample invertible
`рқ’Ә_X`-module if `n > 0`.

**Proof.** The last assertion is immediate, since `рқ’Ә_X(1)` is invertible (3.2.5)
and very ample for `Y` by definition (4.4.3 and 4.4.9). On the other hand, by
definition, the image of `v_n` is precisely `в„җвҒҝрқ’®`, and (8.1.7.1) thus follows from
the exactness of the functor `MМғ` (3.2.4) and from formula (3.2.4.1).

**Corollary.**

<!-- label: II.8.1.8 -->

Under the hypotheses of (8.1.7), if `f : X вҶ’ Y` is the structure morphism and `YвҖІ`
is the closed subprescheme of `Y` defined by `в„җ`, then the closed subprescheme
`XвҖІ = fвҒ»В№(YвҖІ)` of `X` is defined by `в„җ В· рқ’Ә_X` (which is canonically isomorphic to
`рқ’Ә_X(1)`), whence a canonical short exact sequence

```text
  0 вҶ’ рқ’Ә_X(1) вҶ’ рқ’Ә_X вҶ’ рқ’Ә_{XвҖІ} вҶ’ 0.                                            (8.1.8.1)
```

**Proof.** This follows from (8.1.7.1) and from `(I, 4.4.5)`.

```text
  Е©_{вҲ’1} : рқ’Ә_X вҶ’ рқ’Ә_X(вҲ’1)
```

canonically corresponds to a section `s` of `рқ’Ә_X(вҲ’1)` over `X`, which we call the

**Corollary.**

With the notation of (8.1.8), the underlying space of `XвҖІ` is the set of `x вҲҲ X`
such that `s(x) = 0`, where `s` denotes the canonical section of `рқ’Ә_X(вҲ’1)`.

**Proof.** Indeed, if `c_x` is a generator of the fibre `(рқ’Ә_X(1))_x` at a point

<!-- label: II.8.1.11 -->

and `X` the `Y`-scheme obtained by blowing up `в„җ`. Then `в„җ В· рқ’Ә_X` is an invertible

## 8.2. Preliminary results on localisation in graded rings

**(8.2.1)**

<!-- label: II.8.2.1 -->

Let `S` be a graded ring, where for the moment we do not assume the degrees to be
positive. We set

```text
  S^вүҘ = вҠ•_{nвүҘ0} S_n,    S^вүӨ = вҠ•_{nвүӨ0} S_n                                   (8.2.1.1)
```

which are graded subrings of `S`, with degrees respectively all positive and all
negative. If `f` is a homogeneous element of degree `d` (positive or negative) of
`S`, then the ring of fractions `S_f = SвҖІ` is again endowed with the structure of a
graded ring, by taking for `SвҖІ_n` (`n вҲҲ в„Ө`) the set of `x/f^k` with `x вҲҲ S_{n+kd}`
(`k вүҘ 0`); we set `S_{(f)} = SвҖІ_0`, and we will write `S_f^вүҘ` and `S_f^вүӨ` for
`SвҖІ^вүҘ` and `SвҖІ^вүӨ` respectively. If `d > 0`, then

```text
  (S^вүҘ)_f = S_f                                                              (8.2.1.2)
```

since, if `x вҲҲ S_{n+kd}` with `n + kd < 0`, we can write `x/f^k = xf^h/f^{h+k}` and
`n + (h+k)d > 0` for `h` sufficiently large and `> 0`. We conclude by definition
that

```text
  (S^вүҘ)_{(f)} = (S_f^вүҘ)_0 = S_{(f)}.                                         (8.2.1.3)
```

If `M` is a graded `S`-module, we similarly set

```text
  M^вүҘ = вҠ•_{nвүҘ0} M_n,    M^вүӨ = вҠ•_{nвүӨ0} M_n                                   (8.2.1.4)
```

which are respectively a graded `S^вүҘ`-module and a graded `S^вүӨ`-module, and whose
intersection is the `S_0`-module `M_0`. If `f вҲҲ S_d`, we again define `M_f` as the
graded `S_f`-module whose elements of degree `n` are the `z/f^k` for `z вҲҲ M_{n+kd}`
(`k вүҘ 0`); we denote by `M_{(f)}` the set of elements of degree zero of `M_f`,
which is an `S_{(f)}`-module, and we will write `M_f^вүҘ` and `M_f^вүӨ` for `(M_f)^вүҘ`
and `(M_f)^вүӨ` respectively. If `d > 0`, we see as above that

```text
  (M^вүҘ)_f = M_f                                                              (8.2.1.5)
```

and

```text
  (M^вүҘ)_{(f)} = (M_f^вүҘ)_0 = M_{(f)}.                                         (8.2.1.6)
```

**(8.2.2)**

<!-- label: II.8.2.2 -->


**Lemma (8.2.3).**

<!-- label: II.8.2.3 -->

1. We have canonical isomorphisms of (non-graded) rings

    ```text
      Ең_{(рқҗі)} вҘІ Ең/(рқҗі вҲ’ 1)Ең вҘІ S.                                            (8.2.3.1)
    ```

    ```text
      Ең_{(f)} вҘІ S_f^вүӨ                                                       (8.2.3.2)
    ```

    for every `f вҲҲ S_d`, with `d > 0`.

**Proof.** The first of the isomorphisms in (8.2.3.1) was defined in (2.2.5) and
the second is trivial; the isomorphism `Ең_{(рқҗі)} вҘІ S` thus defined makes correspond
to `xрқҗівҒҝ/рқҗі^{n+k}` (where `deg(x) = k`, `k вүҘ вҲ’n`) the element `x`. The homomorphism
(8.2.3.2) makes correspond to `xрқҗівҒҝ/f^k` (where `deg(x) = kd вҲ’ n`) the element

**Lemma (8.2.10).**

<!-- label: II.8.2.10 -->

Let `S` be a positively-graded ring.

1. For `S^вҷ®` to be an `S_0^вҷ®`-algebra of finite type (resp. a Noetherian
    `S_0^вҷ®`-algebra), it is necessary and sufficient that `S` be an `S_0^вҷ®`-algebra
    of finite type (resp. a Noetherian `S_0^вҷ®`-algebra).
1. For `S_{n+1}^вҷ® = S_1^вҷ® S_n^вҷ®` (`n вүҘ n_0`), it is necessary and sufficient that
    `S_{n+1} = S_1 S_n` (`n вүҘ n_0`).
1. For `S_n^вҷ® = (S_1^вҷ®)вҒҝ` (`n вүҘ n_0`), it is necessary and sufficient that
    `S_n = S_1вҒҝ` (`n вүҘ n_0`).
1. If `(f_Оұ)` is a set of homogeneous elements of `S_+` such that `S_+` is the
    radical in `S_+` of the ideal of `S_+` generated by the `f_Оұ`, then `S_+^вҷ®` is
    the radical in `S_+^вҷ®` of the ideal of `S_+^вҷ®` generated by the `f_Оұ^вҷ®`.

**Proof.**

(i) If `S^вҷ®` is an `S_0^вҷ®`-algebra of finite type, then `S_+ = S_1^вҷ®` is a module of
finite type over `S = S_0^вҷ®`, by (2.1.6, i), and so `S` is an `S_0`-algebra of
finite type (2.1.4); if `S^вҷ®` is a Noetherian ring, then so too is `S_0^вҷ® = S`
(2.1.5). Conversely, if `S` is an `S_0`-algebra

<!-- original page 160 -->

of finite type, then we know (2.1.6, ii) that there exist `h > 0` and `m_0 > 0`
such that `S_{n+h} = S_h S_n` for `n вүҘ m_0`; we can clearly assume `m_0 вүҘ h`.
Furthermore, the `S_m` are `S_0`-modules of finite type (2.1.6, i). So, if
`n вүҘ m_0 + h`, then `S_n^вҷ® = S_h S_{nвҲ’h}^вҷ® = S_h^вҷ® S_{nвҲ’h}^вҷ®`; and if
`m < m_0 + h` then, letting `E = S_{m_0} + вӢҜ + S_{m_0+hвҲ’1}`, we have

```text
  S_m^вҷ® = S_m + вӢҜ + S_{m_0+hвҲ’1} + S_h E + S_hВІ E + вӢҜ.
```

For `1 вүӨ m вүӨ m_0`, let `G_m` be the union of finite systems of generators of the
`S_0`-modules `S_i` for `m вүӨ i вүӨ m_0 + h вҲ’ 1`, considered as a subset of `S_{[m]}`.
For `m_0 + 1 вүӨ m вүӨ m_0 + h вҲ’ 1`, let `G_m` be the union of finite systems of
generators of the `S_0`-modules `S_i` for `m вүӨ i вүӨ m_0 + h вҲ’ 1` and of `S_h E`,
considered as a subset of `S_{[m]}`. It is clear that `S_m^вҷ® = S_0^вҷ® G_m` for
`1 вүӨ m вүӨ m_0 + h вҲ’ 1`, and so the union `G` of the `G_m` for
`1 вүӨ m вүӨ m_0 + h вҲ’ 1` is a system of generators of the `S_0^вҷ®`-algebra `S^вҷ®`. We
conclude that, if `S = S_0^вҷ®` is a Noetherian ring, then so too is `S^вҷ®`.

(ii) It is clear that, if `S_{n+1} = S_1 S_n` for `n вүҘ n_0`, then
`S_{n+1}^вҷ® = S_1 S_n^вҷ®`, and _a fortiori_ `S_{n+1}^вҷ® = S_1^вҷ® S_n^вҷ®` for `n вүҘ n_0`.
Conversely, this last equality can be written

```text
  S_{n+1} + S_{n+2} + вӢҜ = (S_1 + S_2 + вӢҜ)(S_n + S_{n+1} + вӢҜ)
```

and comparing terms of degree `n + 1` (in `S`) on both sides gives
`S_{n+1} = S_1 S_n`.

(iii) If `S_n = S_1вҒҝ` for `n вүҘ n_0`, then `S_n^вҷ® = S_1вҒҝ + S_1^{n+1} + вӢҜ`; since
`S_1^вҷ®` contains `S_1 + S_1ВІ + вӢҜ`, we have `S_n^вҷ® вҠӮ (S_1^вҷ®)вҒҝ`, hence
`S_n^вҷ® = (S_1^вҷ®)вҒҝ` for `n вүҘ n_0`. Conversely, the only terms of
`(S_1^вҷ®)вҒҝ = (S_1 + S_2 + вӢҜ)вҒҝ` that are of degree `n` in `S` are those of `S_1вҒҝ`;
the equality `S_n^вҷ® = (S_1^вҷ®)вҒҝ` thus implies `S_n = S_1вҒҝ`.

(iv) It suffices to show that, if an element `g вҲҲ S_{k+h}` is considered as an
element of `S_k^вҷ®` (`k > 0`, `h вүҘ 0`), then there exists an integer `n > 0` such
that `gвҒҝ` is a linear combination (in `S_{kn}^вҷ®`) of the `f_Оұ^вҷ®` with coefficients
in `S^вҷ®`. By hypothesis, there exists an integer `m_0` such that, for `m вүҘ m_0`,
we have, _in `S`_, `g^m = вҲ‘_Оұ c_{Оұm} f_Оұ`, where the indices `Оұ` are
_independent of `m`_; furthermore, we can clearly assume that the `c_{Оұm}` are
homogeneous, with

```text
  deg(c_{Оұm}) = m(k + h) вҲ’ deg f_Оұ
```

in `S`. So take `m_0` sufficiently large to ensure `k m_0 > deg f_Оұ` for all
`f_Оұ` appearing in `g^{m_0}`; for all `Оұ`, let `cвҖІ_{Оұm}` be the element `c_{Оұm}`
considered as having degree `km вҲ’ deg f_Оұ` in `S^вҷ®`; we then have, in `S^вҷ®`,
`g^m = вҲ‘_Оұ cвҖІ_{Оұm} f_Оұ^вҷ®`, which completes the proof.

**(8.2.11)**

<!-- label: II.8.2.11 -->

Consider the graded `S_0`-algebra

```text
  S^вҷ® вҠ—_S S_0 = S^вҷ®/S_+ S^вҷ® = вҠ•_{nвүҘ0} S_{[n]}/S_+ S_{[n]}.                  (8.2.11.1)
```

Since `S_n` is a quotient `S_0`-module of `S_{[n]}/S_+ S_{[n]}`, there is a
canonical homomorphism of graded `S_0`-algebras

```text
  S^вҷ® вҠ—_S S_0 вҶ’ S                                                          (8.2.11.2)
```

which is clearly _surjective_, and consequently corresponds (2.9.2) to a canonical
_closed immersion_

```text
  Proj(S) вҶ’ Proj(S^вҷ® вҠ—_S S_0).                                              (8.2.11.3)
```

<!-- original page 161 -->

**Proposition.**

<!-- label: II.8.2.12 -->

The canonical morphism (8.2.11.3) is bijective. For the homomorphism (8.2.11.2) to
be (TN)-bijective, it is necessary and sufficient that there exist some `n_0` such
that `S_{n+1} = S_1 S_n` for `n вүҘ n_0`. If this latter condition is satisfied,
then (8.2.11.3) is an isomorphism; the converse is true whenever `S` is
Noetherian.

**Proof.** To prove the first claim, it suffices (2.8.3) to show that the kernel
`рқ”Қ` of the homomorphism (8.2.11.2) consists of _nilpotent_ elements. But if
`f вҲҲ S_{[n]}` is an element whose class modulo `S_+ S_{[n]}` belongs to this
kernel, then this implies `f вҲҲ S_{[n+1]}`; then `f^{n+1}`, considered as an element
of `S_{[n(n+1)]}`, is also an element of `S_+ S_{[n(n+1)]}`, since it can be
written as `f В· fвҒҝ`; so the class of `f^{n+1}` modulo `S_+ S_{[n(n+1)]}` is zero,
which proves our claim. Since the hypothesis that `S_{n+1} = S_1 S_n` for
`n вүҘ n_0` is equivalent to `S_{n+1}^вҷ® = S_1^вҷ® S_n^вҷ®` for `n вүҘ n_0` (8.2.10, ii),
this hypothesis is equivalent, by definition, to (8.2.11.2) being (TN)-injective,
and thus (TN)-bijective, and so (8.2.11.3) is an isomorphism, by (2.9.1).
Conversely, if (8.2.11.3) is an isomorphism, then the sheaf `рқ”ҚМғ` on
`Proj(S^вҷ® вҠ—_S S_0)` is zero (2.9.2, i); since `S^вҷ® вҠ—_S S_0` is Noetherian, as a
quotient of `S^вҷ®` (8.2.10, i), we conclude from (2.7.3) that `рқ”Қ` satisfies
condition (TN), and so `S_{n+1}^вҷ® = S_1^вҷ® S_n^вҷ®` for `n вүҘ n_0`, which finishes the
proof, by (8.2.10, ii).

**(8.2.13)**

Consider now the canonical injections `(S_+)вҒҝ вҶ’ S_{[n]}`, which define an injective
homomorphism of degree zero of graded rings


**Proposition.**

<!-- label: II.8.2.14 -->

For the homomorphism (8.2.13.1) to be a (TN)-isomorphism, it is necessary and
sufficient that there exist some `n_0` such that `S_n = S_1вҒҝ` for all `n вүҘ n_0`.
Whenever this is the case, the morphism corresponding to (8.2.13.1) is everywhere
defined and is also an isomorphism


the converse is true whenever `S` is Noetherian.

**Proof.** The first two claims are evident, given (8.2.10, iii) and (2.9.1). The
third will follow from (8.2.10, i and iii) and the following lemma:

**Lemma (8.2.14.1).**

<!-- label: II.8.2.14.1 -->

Let `T` be a positively-graded ring that is also a `T_0`-algebra of finite type. If
the morphism corresponding to the injective homomorphism `вҠ•_{nвүҘ0} T_1вҒҝ вҶ’ T` is
everywhere defined and is also an isomorphism
`Proj(T) вҶ’ Proj(вҠ•_{nвүҘ0} T_1вҒҝ)`, then there exists some `n_0` such that
`T_n = T_1вҒҝ` for `n вүҘ n_0`.

Let `g_i` (`1 вүӨ i вүӨ r`) be generators of the `T_0`-module `T_1`. The hypothesis
implies first that the `D_+(g_i)` cover `Proj(T)` (2.8.1). Let `(h_j)_{1вүӨjвүӨs}` be a
system of homogeneous elements of `T_+`, with `deg(h_j) = n_j`, that form,
together with the `g_i`, a system of generators of the ideal `T_+`, or,
equivalently (2.1.3), a system of generators of `T` as a `T_0`-algebra; if we set
`TвҖІ = вҠ•_{nвүҘ0} T_1вҒҝ`, then the element `h_j / g_i^{n_j}` of the ring `T_{(g_i)}`
must, by hypothesis, belong to the subring `TвҖІ_{(g_i)}`, and so there exists some
integer `k` such that `T_1^k h_j вҠӮ T_1^{k+n_j}` for all `j`. We thus conclude, by
induction on `r`, that `T_1^k h_j^r вҠӮ TвҖІ` for all `r вүҘ 1`, and, by definition of
the `h_j`, we thus have `T_1^k T вҠӮ TвҖІ`. Also, there exists, for all `j`, an
integer `m_j` such that `h_j^{m_j}` belongs to the ideal of `T` generated by the
`g_i` (2.3.14), so `h_j^{m_j} вҲҲ T_1 T`, and

<!-- original page 162 -->

`h_j^{m_j k} вҲҲ T_1^k T вҠӮ TвҖІ`. There is thus an integer `m_0 вүҘ k` such that
`h_j^m вҲҲ T_1^{m n_j}` for `m вүҘ m_0`. So, if `q` is the largest of the integers
`n_j`, then `n_0 = q s m_0 + k` is the required number. Indeed, an element of
`S_n`, for `n вүҘ n_0`, is the sum of monomials belonging to `T_1^Оұ u`, where `u` is
a product of powers of the `h_j`; if `Оұ вүҘ k`, then it follows from the above that
`T_1^Оұ u вҠӮ T_1вҒҝ`; in the other case, one of the exponents of the `h_j` is
`вүҘ m_0`, so `u вҲҲ T_1^ОІ v` where `ОІ вүҘ k` and `v` is again a product of powers of
the `h_j`; we can then reduce to the previous case, and so we conclude that
`T_1^Оұ u вҠӮ T_1вҒҝ` in all cases.

**Remark.**

<!-- label: II.8.2.15 -->

The condition `S_n = S_1вҒҝ` for `n вүҘ n_0` clearly implies that `S_{n+1} = S_1 S_n`
for `n вүҘ n_0`, but the converse is not necessarily true, even if we assume that
`S` is Noetherian. For example, let `K` be a field, `A = K[рқҗұ]`, and
`B = K[рқҗІ]/рқҗІВІK[рқҗІ]`, where `рқҗұ` and `рқҗІ` are indeterminates, with `рқҗұ` of degree 1 and
`рқҗІ` of degree 2, and let `S = A вҠ—_K B`, so that `S` is a graded algebra over `K`
with a basis given by the elements `1`, `рқҗұвҒҝ` (`n вүҘ 1`), and `рқҗұвҒҝрқҗІ` (`n вүҘ 0`). It is
immediate that `S_{n+1} = S_1 S_n` for `n вүҘ 2`, but `S_1вҒҝ = K рқҗұвҒҝ` while
`S_n = K рқҗұвҒҝ + K рқҗұвҒҝ рқҗІ` for `n вүҘ 2`.

## 8.3. Projecting cones

**(8.3.1)**

<!-- label: II.8.3.1 -->

Let `Y` be a prescheme; in all of this section, we consider only _`Y`-preschemes_
and _`Y`-morphisms_. Let `рқ’®` be a quasi-coherent _positively_-graded `рқ’Ә_Y`-algebra;
_we further assume that `рқ’®вӮҖ = рқ’Ә_Y`_. Following the notation introduced in (8.2.2),
we let

```text
  рқ’®МӮ = рқ’®[рқҗі] = рқ’® вҠ—_{рқ’Ә_Y} рқ’Ә_Y[рқҗі]                                              (8.3.1.1)
```

which we consider as a positively-graded `рқ’Ә_Y`-algebra by defining the degrees as
in (8.2.2.2), so that, for every affine open `U` of `Y`,

```text
  О“(U, рқ’®МӮ) = О“(U, рқ’®)[рқҗі].
```

In what follows, we write

```text
  X = Proj(рқ’®),    C = Spec(рқ’®),    ДҲ = Proj(рқ’®МӮ)                              (8.3.1.2)
```

(where, in the definition of `C`, we consider `рқ’®` as a non-graded `рқ’Ә_Y`-algebra),
and we say that `C` (resp. `ДҲ`) is the _affine cone_ (resp. _projective cone_)
defined by `рқ’®`; we will sometimes say "cone" instead of "affine cone". By abuse of
language, we also say that `C` (resp. `ДҲ`) is the _affine projecting cone_ (resp.
_projective projecting cone_) of `X`, with the implicit understanding that the
prescheme `X` is given in the form `Proj(рқ’®)`; finally, we say that `ДҲ` is the
_projective closure_ of `C` (with the datum of `рқ’®` being implicit in the structure
of `C`).

**Proposition.**

<!-- label: II.8.3.2 -->

There exist canonical `Y`-morphisms

```text
  Y в”Җв”ҖОөв”Җв”ҖвҶ’ C в”Җв”Җiв”Җв”ҖвҶ’ ДҲ                                                       (8.3.2.1)
```

```text
  X в”Җв”Җjв”Җв”ҖвҶ’ ДҲ                                                                (8.3.2.2)
```

such that `Оө` and `j` are closed immersions, and `i` is an affine morphism which
is a dominant open immersion, for which

```text
  i(C) = ДҲ вҲ– j(X);                                                          (8.3.2.3)
```

furthermore, `ДҲ` is the smallest closed subprescheme of `ДҲ` containing `i(C)`.

**Proof.** To define `i`, consider the open subset of `ДҲ` given by

```text
  ДҲ_рқҗі = Spec(рқ’®МӮ/(рқҗі вҲ’ 1)рқ’®МӮ)                                                  (8.3.2.4)
```

(3.1.4), where `рқҗі` is canonically identified with a section of `рқ’®МӮ` over `Y`. The
isomorphism `i : C вҘІ ДҲ_рқҗі` then corresponds to the canonical isomorphism (8.2.3.1)

```text
  рқ’®МӮ/(рқҗі вҲ’ 1)рқ’®МӮ вҘІ рқ’®.
```

The morphism `Оө` corresponds to the augmentation homomorphism `рқ’® вҶ’ рқ’®вӮҖ = рқ’Ә_Y`,
which has kernel `рқ’®_+` (1.2.7), and, since the latter is surjective, `Оө` is a
closed immersion (1.4.10). Finally, `j` corresponds (3.5.1) to the surjective
homomorphism of degree zero `рқ’®МӮ вҶ’ рқ’®`, which restricts to the identity on `рқ’®` and is
zero on `рқҗі рқ’®МӮ`, the kernel; `j` is everywhere defined and is a closed immersion, by
(3.6.2).

To prove the other claims of (8.3.2), we may clearly restrict to the case where
`Y = Spec(A)` is affine, and `рқ’® = SМғ`, with `S` a graded `A`-algebra, whence
`рқ’®МӮ = (Ең)Мғ`; the homogeneous elements `f` of `S_+` can then be identified with
sections of `рқ’®МӮ` over `Y`, and the open subset of `ДҲ`, denoted `D_+(f)` in (2.3.3),
can be written as `ДҲ_f` (3.1.4); similarly, the open subset of `C` denoted `D(f)`
in `(I, 1.1.1)` can be written as `C_f` `(0, 5.5.2)`. With this in mind, it
follows from (2.3.14) and from the definition of `Ең` that, in this case, the open
subsets `ДҲ_рқҗі = i(C)` and `ДҲ_f` (with `f` homogeneous in `S_+`) form a _cover_ of
`ДҲ`. Furthermore, with this notation,

```text
  iвҒ»В№(ДҲ_f) = C_f;                                                            (8.3.2.5)
```

indeed, `ДҲ_f вҲ© i(C) = ДҲ_f вҲ© ДҲ_рқҗі = ДҲ_{fрқҗі} = Spec(Ең_{(fрқҗі)})`. But, if
`d = deg(f)`, then `Ең_{(fрқҗі)}` is canonically isomorphic to
`(Ең_{(рқҗі)})_{f/рқҗі^d}` (2.2.2), and it follows from the definition of the isomorphism
(8.2.3.1) that the image of `(Ең_{(рқҗі)})_{f/рқҗі^d}` under the corresponding isomorphism
of rings of fractions is exactly `S_f`. Since `C_f = Spec(S_f)`, this proves
(8.3.2.5) and shows at the same time that the morphism `i` is affine; furthermore,
the restriction of `i` to `C_f`, considered as a morphism to `ДҲ_f`, corresponds
`(I, 1.7.3)` to the canonical homomorphism `Ең_{(f)} вҶ’ Ең_{(fрқҗі)}`, and, by the above
and (8.2.3.2), we may claim the following result:

**(8.3.2.6)**

<!-- label: II.8.3.2.6 -->

If `Y = Spec(A)` is affine and `рқ’® = SМғ`, then, for every homogeneous `f` in `S_+`,
`ДҲ_f` is canonically identified with `Spec(S_f^вүӨ)`, and the morphism `C_f вҶ’ ДҲ_f`
given by restricting `i` then corresponds to the canonical injection
`S_f^вүӨ вҶ’ S_f`.

Now note that (for `Y` affine) the complement of `ДҲ_рқҗі` in `ДҲ = Proj(Ең)`

<!-- original page 164 -->

is, by definition, the set of graded prime ideals of `Ең` containing `рқҗі`, which is
exactly `j(X)`, by definition of `j`, which proves (8.3.2.3).

Finally, to prove the last claim of (8.3.2), we may assume `Y` affine. With the
above notation, note that in the ring `Ең`, `рқҗі` is not a zero divisor; since
`i(C) = ДҲ_рқҗі`, it suffices to prove the following lemma:

**Lemma (8.3.2.7).**

<!-- label: II.8.3.2.7 -->

Let `T` be a positively-graded ring, `Z = Proj(T)`, and `g` a homogeneous element
of `T` of degree `d > 0`. If `g` is not a zero divisor in `T`, then `Z` is the
smallest closed subprescheme of `Z` containing `Z_g = D_+(g)`.

By `(I, 4.1.9)`, the question is local on `Z`; for every homogeneous element
`h вҲҲ T_e` (`e > 0`), it thus suffices to prove that `Z_h` is the smallest closed
subprescheme of `Z_h` containing `Z_{gh}`; it follows from the definitions and
from `(I, 4.3.2)` that this condition is equivalent to asking for the canonical
homomorphism `T_{(h)} вҶ’ T_{(gh)}` to be _injective_. But this homomorphism can be
identified with the canonical homomorphism `T_{(h)} вҶ’ (T_{(h)})_{g^e/h^d}` (2.2.3).
But since `g^e` is not a zero divisor in `T`, `g^e/h^d` is not a zero divisor in
`T_h` (nor _a fortiori_ in `T_{(h)}`), since the fact that
`(g^e/h^d)(t/h^m) = 0` (for `t вҲҲ T` and `m > 0`) implies the existence of some
`n > 0` such that `hвҒҝ g^e t = 0`, whence `hвҒҝ t = 0`, and thus `t/h^m = 0` in
`T_h`. This finishes the proof `(0, 1.2.2)`.

**(8.3.3)**

<!-- label: II.8.3.3 -->

We will often identify the affine cone `C` with the subprescheme induced by the
projective cone `ДҲ` on the open subset `i(C)` by means of the open immersion `i`.
The closed subprescheme of `C` associated to the closed immersion `Оө` is called
the _apex prescheme_ of `C`; we also say that `Оө`, which is a `Y`-section of `C`,
is the _apex section_, or _zero section_, of `C`; we may identify `Y` with the
apex prescheme of `C` by means of `Оө`. Also, `i вҲҳ Оө` is a `Y`-section of `ДҲ`, and
thus also a closed immersion `(I, 5.4.6)`, corresponding to the canonical
surjective homomorphism of degree zero `рқ’®МӮ = рқ’®[рқҗі] вҶ’ рқ’Ә_Y[рқҗі]` (3.1.7), whose kernel
is `рқ’®_+[рқҗі] = рқ’®_+ рқ’®МӮ`; the subprescheme of `ДҲ` associated to this closed immersion is
also called the _apex prescheme_ of `ДҲ`, and `i вҲҳ Оө` the _apex section_ of `ДҲ`;
it may be identified with `Y` by means of `i вҲҳ Оө`. Finally, the closed
subprescheme of `ДҲ` associated to `j` is called the _locus at infinity_ of `ДҲ`,
and may be identified with `X` by means of `j`.

**(8.3.4)**

<!-- label: II.8.3.4 -->

The subpreschemes of `C` (resp. `ДҲ`) induced on the _open_ subsets

```text
  E = C вҲ– Оө(Y),     ГҠ = ДҲ вҲ– i(Оө(Y))                                         (8.3.4.1)
```

are called (by an abuse of language) the _punctured affine cone_ and the
_punctured projective cone_ (respectively) defined by `рқ’®`; we note that, despite
this nomenclature, _`E` is not necessarily affine over `Y`_, nor `ГҠ` projective
over `Y` (8.4.3). When we identify `C` with `i(C)`, we thus have the underlying
spaces

```text
  C вҲӘ ГҠ = ДҲ,        C вҲ© ГҠ = E                                               (8.3.4.2)
```

so that `ДҲ` may be regarded as being obtained by _gluing_ the open subpreschemes
`C` and `ГҠ`; furthermore, by (8.3.2.3),

```text
  E = ГҠ вҲ– j(X).                                                              (8.3.4.3)
```

If `Y = Spec(A)` is affine, then, with the notation of (8.3.2),

```text
  E = вӢғ C_f,    ГҠ = вӢғ ДҲ_f,    C_f = C вҲ© ДҲ_f                                 (8.3.4.4)
```

where `f` runs over the set of homogeneous elements of `S_+` (or only a subset
`M` of this set, with `M` generating an ideal of `S_+` whose radical in `S_+` is
`S_+` itself, or, equivalently, such that the `X_f` for `f вҲҲ M` cover `X`
(2.3.14)). The gluing of `C` and `ДҲ_f` along `C_f` is thus determined by the
injection morphisms `C_f вҶ’ C` and `C_f вҶ’ ДҲ_f`, which, as we have seen (8.3.2.6),
correspond (respectively) to the canonical homomorphisms `S вҶ’ S_f` and
`S_f^вүӨ вҶ’ S_f`.

**Proposition.**

<!-- label: II.8.3.5 -->

With the notation of (8.3.1) and (8.3.4), the morphism associated (3.5.1) to the
canonical injection `ПҶ : рқ’® вҶ’ рқ’®МӮ = рқ’®[рқҗі]` is a surjective affine morphism (called the
_canonical retraction_)

```text
  p : ГҠ вҶ’ X                                                                  (8.3.5.1)
```

such that

```text
  p вҲҳ j = 1_X.                                                               (8.3.5.2)
```

**Proof.** To prove the proposition, we may restrict to the case where `Y` is
affine. Taking into account the expression (8.3.4.4) for `ГҠ`, the fact that the
domain of definition `G(ПҶ)` of `p` is equal to `ГҠ` will follow from the first of
the following claims:

**(8.3.5.3)**

<!-- label: II.8.3.5.3 -->

If `Y = Spec(A)` is affine and `рқ’® = SМғ`, then, for every homogeneous `f вҲҲ S_+`,

```text
  pвҒ»В№(X_f) = ДҲ_f                                                             (8.3.5.4)
```

and the restriction of `p` to `ДҲ_f = Spec(S_f^вүӨ)`, considered as a morphism from
`ДҲ_f` to `X_f`, corresponds to the canonical injection `S_{(f)} вҶ’ S_f^вүӨ`. If
moreover `f вҲҲ S_1`, then `ДҲ_f` is isomorphic to `X_f вҠ—_в„Ө в„Ө[T]` (where `T` is an
indeterminate).

Indeed, equation (8.3.5.4) is exactly a particular case of (2.8.1.1), and the
second claim is exactly the definition of `Proj(ПҶ)` when `Y` is affine (2.8.1).
Then equation (8.3.5.2) and the fact that `p` is surjective show that the
composition `рқ’® вҶ’ рқ’®МӮ вҶ’ рқ’®` of the canonical homomorphisms is the identity on `рқ’®`.
Finally, the last claim of (8.3.5.3) follows from the fact that `S_f^вүӨ` is
isomorphic to `S_{(f)}[T]` whenever `f вҲҲ S_1` (2.2.1).

**Corollary.**

<!-- label: II.8.3.6 -->

The restriction

```text
  ПҖ : E вҶ’ X                                                                  (8.3.6.1)
```

of `p` to `E` is a surjective affine morphism. If `Y` is affine and `f` is
homogeneous in `S_+`, then

```text
  ПҖвҒ»В№(X_f) = C_f                                                             (8.3.6.2)
```

and the restriction of `ПҖ` to `C_f` corresponds to the canonical injection
`S_{(f)} вҶ’ S_f`. If moreover `f вҲҲ S_1`, then `C_f` is isomorphic to
`X_f вҠ—_в„Ө в„Ө[T, TвҒ»В№]` (where `T` is an indeterminate).

**Proof.** Equation (8.3.6.2) follows immediately from (8.3.5.3) and (8.3.2.5),
and shows the surjectivity of `ПҖ`; we have already seen that the immersion `i`,
restricted to `C_f`, corresponds

<!-- original page 166 -->

to the injection `S_f^вүӨ вҶ’ S_f` (8.3.2). Finally, the last claim is a consequence
of the fact that, for `f вҲҲ S_1`, `S_f` is isomorphic to `S_{(f)}[T, TвҒ»В№]` (2.2.1).

**Remark.**

<!-- label: II.8.3.7 -->

When `Y` is affine, the elements of the underlying space of `E` are the
(not-necessarily-graded) prime ideals `рқ”ӯ` of `S` not containing `S_+`, by
definition of the immersion `Оө` (8.3.2). For such an ideal `рқ”ӯ`, the `рқ”ӯ вҲ© S_n`
clearly satisfy the conditions of (2.1.9), and so there exists exactly one
_graded_ prime ideal `рқ”®` of `S` such that `рқ”® вҲ© S_n = рқ”ӯ вҲ© S_n` for all `n`; the
map `ПҖ : E вҶ’ X` of underlying spaces can then be understood via the equation

```text
  ПҖ(рқ”ӯ) = рқ”®.                                                                  (8.3.7.1)
```

Indeed, to prove this equation, it suffices to consider some homogeneous `f` in
`S_+` such that `рқ”ӯ вҲҲ D(f)`, and to note that `рқ”®_{(f)}` is the inverse image of
`рқ”ӯ_f` under the injection `S_{(f)} вҶ’ S_f`.

**Corollary.**

<!-- label: II.8.3.8 -->

If `рқ’®` is generated by `рқ’®_1`, then the morphisms `p` and `ПҖ` are of finite type;
for every `x вҲҲ X`, the fibre `pвҒ»В№(x)` is isomorphic to `Spec(Оә(x)[T])`, and the
fibre `ПҖвҒ»В№(x)` is isomorphic to `Spec(Оә(x)[T, TвҒ»В№])`.

**Proof.** This follows immediately from (8.3.5) and (8.3.6) by noting that, when
`Y` is affine and `S` is generated by `S_1`, the `X_f` for `f вҲҲ S_1` form a cover
of `X` (2.3.14).

**Remark.**

<!-- label: II.8.3.9 -->

The punctured affine cone corresponding to the graded `рқ’Ә_Y`-algebra `рқ’Ә_Y[T]`
(where `T` is an indeterminate) may be identified with `рқ”ҫ_m = Spec(рқ’Ә_Y[T, TвҒ»В№])`,
since it is exactly `C_T`, as we have seen in (8.3.2) (see (8.4.4) for a more
general result). This prescheme is canonically endowed with the structure of a
"_`Y`-scheme in commutative groups_". This idea will be explained in detail later,
but, for now, may be quickly summarised as follows. A `Y`-scheme in groups is a
`Y`-scheme `G` endowed with two `Y`-morphisms, `p : G Г—_Y G вҶ’ G` and `s : G вҶ’ G`,
satisfying conditions formally analogous to the axioms of the composition law and
the symmetry law of a group: the diagram

```text
   G Г— G Г— G в”Җв”ҖpГ—1в”Җв”ҖвҶ’ G Г— G
        в”Ӯ              в”Ӯ
    1Г—p в”Ӯ              в”Ӯ p
        вҶ“              вҶ“
     G Г— G в”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җpв”Җв”Җв”Җв”ҖвҶ’ G
```

should commute (_associativity_), and there should be a condition corresponding to
the fact that, for groups, the maps

```text
  (x, y) вҶҰ (x, xвҒ»В№, y) вҶҰ (x, xвҒ»В№y) вҶҰ x(xвҒ»В№y)
```

and

```text
  (x, y) вҶҰ (x, xвҒ»В№, y) вҶҰ (x, y xвҒ»В№) вҶҰ (y xвҒ»В№) x
```

should both reduce to `(x, y) вҶҰ y`; the sequence of morphisms corresponding, for
example, to the first composite map is

```text
  G Г— G в”Җв”Җ(1,s)Г—1в”Җв”ҖвҶ’ G Г— G Г— G в”Җв”Җ1Г—pв”Җв”ҖвҶ’ G Г— G в”Җв”Җpв”Җв”ҖвҶ’ G
```

and the reader should write down the second sequence.

<!-- original page 167 -->

It is immediate `(I, 3.4.3)` that the datum of a structure of a `Y`-scheme in
groups on a `Y`-scheme `G` is equivalent to the datum, for _every_ `Y`-prescheme
`Z`, of a _group_ structure on the set `Hom_Y(Z, G)`, with these structures being
such that, for every `Y`-morphism `Z вҶ’ ZвҖІ`, the corresponding map
`Hom_Y(ZвҖІ, G) вҶ’ Hom_Y(Z, G)` is a group homomorphism. In the particular case of
`рқ”ҫ_m` considered here, `Hom_Y(Z, рқ”ҫ_m)` may be identified with the set of
`Z`-sections of `Z Г—_Y рқ”ҫ_m` `(I, 3.3.14)`, and thus with the set of `Z`-sections
of `Spec(рқ’Ә_Z[T, TвҒ»В№])`; finally, the same reasoning as in `(I, 3.3.15)` shows
that this set is canonically identified with the set of _invertible_ elements of
the ring `О“(Z, рқ’Ә_Z)`, and the group structure on this set is the structure coming
from the multiplication in the ring `О“(Z, рқ’Ә_Z)`. The reader may verify that the
morphisms `p` and `s` above are obtained as follows: they correspond, by (1.2.7)
and (1.4.6), to the homomorphisms of `рқ’Ә_Y`-algebras

```text
  ПҖ : рқ’Ә_Y[T, TвҒ»В№] вҶ’ рқ’Ә_Y[T, TвҒ»В№, TвҖІ, TвҖІвҒ»В№]
  Пғ : рқ’Ә_Y[T, TвҒ»В№] вҶ’ рқ’Ә_Y[T, TвҒ»В№]
```

and are entirely defined by the data `ПҖ(T) = T TвҖІ` and `Пғ(T) = TвҒ»В№`.

With this in mind, `рқ”ҫ_m` can be considered as a "_universal domain of operators_"
for every _affine cone_ `C = Spec(рқ’®)`, where `рқ’®` is a quasi-coherent
positively-graded `рқ’Ә_Y`-algebra. This means that we may canonically define a
`Y`-morphism `рқ”ҫ_m Г—_Y C вҶ’ C` having the formal properties of an external law of a
set endowed with a group of operators; or, again, as above for schemes in groups,
we may give, for every `Y`-prescheme `Z`, an external law on `Hom_Y(Z, C)`, having
the group `Hom_Y(Z, рқ”ҫ_m)` as its set of operators, with the usual axioms of sets
endowed with a group of operators, and a compatibility condition with respect to
the `Y`-morphisms `Z вҶ’ ZвҖІ`. In the current case, the morphism `рқ”ҫ_m Г—_Y C вҶ’ C` is
defined by the datum of a homomorphism of `рқ’Ә_Y`-algebras
`рқ’® вҶ’ рқ’® вҠ—_{рқ’Ә_Y} рқ’Ә_Y[T, TвҒ»В№] = рқ’®[T, TвҒ»В№]`, which associates, to each section
`s_n вҲҲ О“(U, рқ’®_n)` (where `U` is an open subset of `Y`), the section
`s_n TвҒҝ вҲҲ О“(U, рқ’® вҠ—_{рқ’Ә_Y} рқ’Ә_Y[T, TвҒ»В№])`.

Conversely, suppose that we are given a quasi-coherent, _a priori non-graded_,
`рқ’Ә_Y`-algebra, and, on `C = Spec(рқ’®)`, a structure of a "_`Y`-scheme in sets
endowed with a group of operators_" having the `Y`-scheme in groups `рқ”ҫ_m` as its
domain of operators; then we canonically obtain a _grading_ of `рқ’Ә_Y`-algebras on
`рқ’®`. Indeed, the datum of a `Y`-morphism `рқ”ҫ_m Г—_Y C вҶ’ C` is equivalent to that of
a homomorphism of `рқ’Ә_Y`-algebras `ПҲ : рқ’® вҶ’ рқ’®[T, TвҒ»В№]`, which may be written
`ПҲ = вҲ‘_{n вҲҲ в„Ө} ПҲ_n TвҒҝ`, where the `ПҲ_n : рқ’® вҶ’ рқ’®` are homomorphisms of
`рқ’Ә_Y`-modules (with `ПҲ_n(s) = 0` except for finitely many `n` for every section
`s вҲҲ О“(U, рқ’®)`, for any open subset `U` of `Y`). One can then prove that the
axioms of sets endowed with a group of operators imply that the `ПҲ_n(рқ’®) = рқ’®_n`
define a grading (in positive or negative degree) of `рқ’Ә_Y`-algebras on `рқ’®`, with
the `ПҲ_n` being the corresponding projectors. We also have the notion of a
structure of an "_affine cone_" on every affine `Y`-scheme, defined in a
"geometric" way without any reference to any prior grading.

We will not develop this point of view further here, and we leave the work of
precisely formulating the definitions and results corresponding to the information
given above to the reader.

## 8.4. Projective closure of a vector bundle

**(8.4.1)**

<!-- label: II.8.4.1 -->

Let `Y` be a prescheme and `рқ“”` a quasi-coherent `рқ’Ә_Y`-module. If we take `рқ’®` to be
the graded `рқ’Ә_Y`-algebra `рқ•Ҡ_{рқ’Ә_Y}(рқ“”)`, then Definition (8.3.1.1) shows that `рқ’®МӮ`
may be identified with `рқ•Ҡ_{рқ’Ә_Y}(рқ“” вҠ• рқ’Ә_Y)`. With the affine cone `Spec(рқ’®)` defined
by `рқ’®` being, by definition, `рқ•Қ(рқ“”)`, and `Proj(рқ’®)` being, by definition, `в„ҷ(рқ“”)`,
we see that:

**Proposition.**

<!-- label: II.8.4.2 -->

The projective closure of a vector bundle `рқ•Қ(рқ“”)` over `Y` is canonically isomorphic
to `в„ҷ(рқ“” вҠ• рқ’Ә_Y)`, and the locus at infinity of the latter is canonically isomorphic
to `в„ҷ(рқ“”)`.

**Remark.**

<!-- label: II.8.4.3 -->

Take, for example, `рқ“” = рқ’Ә_Y^r` with `r вүҘ 2`; then the punctured cones `E` and `ГҠ`
defined by `рқ’®` are neither affine nor projective over `Y` if `Y вү  вҲ…`. The second
claim is immediate, because `ДҲ = в„ҷ(рқ’Ә_Y^{r+1})` is projective over `Y`, and the
underlying spaces of `E` and `ГҠ` are non-closed open subsets of `ДҲ`, and so the
canonical immersions `E вҶ’ ДҲ` and `ГҠ вҶ’ ДҲ` are not projective (5.5.3), and we
conclude by appealing to (5.5.5, v). Now, supposing for example that
`Y = Spec(A)` is affine and `r = 2`, then `C = Spec(A[T_1, T_2])`, and `E` is the
prescheme induced by `C` on the open subset `D(T_1) вҲӘ D(T_2)`; but we have
already seen that the latter is not affine `(I, 5.5.11)`; _a fortiori_ `ГҠ` cannot
be affine, since `E` is the open subset where the section `рқҗі` over `ГҠ` does not
vanish (8.3.2).

However:

**Proposition.**

<!-- label: II.8.4.4 -->

If `в„’` is an invertible `рқ’Ә_Y`-module, then there are canonical isomorphisms for
both the punctured cones `E` and `ГҠ` corresponding to `C = рқ•Қ(в„’)`:

```text
  Spec(вҠ•_{n вҲҲ в„Ө} в„’^{вҠ— n}) вҘІ E                                                (8.4.4.1)
```

```text
  рқ•Қ(в„’вҒ»В№) вҘІ ГҠ.                                                                (8.4.4.2)
```

Furthermore, there exists a canonical isomorphism from the projective closure of
`рқ•Қ(в„’)` to the projective closure of `рқ•Қ(в„’вҒ»В№)` that sends the zero section (resp.
the locus at infinity) of the former to the locus at infinity (resp. the zero
section) of the latter.

**Proof.** We have `рқ’® = вҠ•_{nвүҘ0} в„’^{вҠ— n}`; the canonical injection

```text
  рқ’® вҶ’ вҠ•_{n вҲҲ в„Ө} в„’^{вҠ— n}
```

defines a canonical dominant morphism

```text
  Spec(вҠ•_{n вҲҲ в„Ө} в„’^{вҠ— n}) вҶ’ рқ•Қ(в„’) = Spec(вҠ•_{nвүҘ0} в„’^{вҠ— n})                    (8.4.4.3)
```

and it suffices to prove that this morphism is an isomorphism from the scheme
`Spec(вҠ•_{n вҲҲ в„Ө} в„’^{вҠ— n})` to `E`. Since the question is local on `Y`, we may
assume `Y = Spec(A)` is affine

<!-- original page 169 -->

and `в„’ = рқ’Ә_Y`, so that `рқ’® = (A[T])Мғ` and `вҠ•_{n вҲҲ в„Ө} в„’^{вҠ— n} = (A[T, TвҒ»В№])Мғ`. But
`A[T, TвҒ»В№]` is the ring of fractions `A[T]_T` of `A[T]`, and thus (8.4.4.3)
identifies `Spec(вҠ•_{n вҲҲ в„Ө} в„’^{вҠ— n})` with the prescheme induced by `C = рқ•Қ(в„’)` on
the open subset `D(T)`; the complement `V(T)` of this open subset in `C` is the
underlying space of the closed subprescheme of `C` defined by the ideal `T A[T]`,
which is exactly the zero section of `C`, and so `E = D(T)`.

The isomorphism (8.4.4.2) will be a consequence of the last claim, since `рқ•Қ(в„’вҒ»В№)`
is the complement of the locus at infinity of its projective closure, and `ГҠ` is
the complement of the zero section of the projective closure `C = рқ•Қ(в„’)`. But
these projective closures are `в„ҷ(в„’вҒ»В№ вҠ• рқ’Ә_Y)` and `в„ҷ(в„’ вҠ• рқ’Ә_Y)` respectively; we
can write `в„’ вҠ• рқ’Ә_Y = в„’ вҠ— (в„’вҒ»В№ вҠ• рқ’Ә_Y)`. The existence of the desired canonical
isomorphism then follows from (4.1.4), and everything reduces to showing that
this isomorphism swaps the zero sections and the loci at infinity. For this, we
may reduce to the case where `Y = Spec(A)` is affine, `L = Ac`, and `LвҒ»В№ = AcвҖІ`,
with the canonical isomorphism `L вҠ— LвҒ»В№ вҶ’ A` sending `c вҠ— cвҖІ` to the element `1`
of `A`. Then `рқ•Ҡ(L вҠ• A)` is the tensor product of `A[рқҗі]` with `вҠ•_{nвүҘ0} A c^{вҠ— n}`,
and `рқ•Ҡ(LвҒ»В№ вҠ• A)` is the tensor product of `A[рқҗі]` with `вҠ•_{nвүҘ0} A cвҖІ^{вҠ— n}`, and
the isomorphism defined in (4.1.4) sends `рқҗі^h вҠ— cвҖІ^{вҠ— (nвҲ’h)}` to the element
`рқҗі^{nвҲ’h} вҠ— c^{вҠ— h}`. But, in `в„ҷ(в„’вҒ»В№ вҠ• рқ’Ә_Y)`, the locus at infinity is the set of
points where the section `рқҗі` vanishes, and the zero section is the set of points
where the section `cвҖІ` vanishes; since we have analogous definitions for
`в„ҷ(в„’ вҠ• рқ’Ә_Y)`, the conclusion follows immediately from the above.

## 8.5. Functorial behaviour

**(8.5.1)**

<!-- label: II.8.5.1 -->

Let `Y` and `YвҖІ` be preschemes, `q : YвҖІ вҶ’ Y` a morphism, and `рқ’®` (resp. `рқ’®вҖІ`) a
quasi-coherent _positively_-graded `рқ’Ә_Y`-algebra (resp. quasi-coherent
_positively_-graded `рқ’Ә_{YвҖІ}`-algebra). Consider a `q`-morphism of graded algebras

```text
  ПҶ : рқ’® вҶ’ рқ’®вҖІ.                                                                (8.5.1.1)
```

We know (1.5.6) that this corresponds, canonically, to a morphism


such that the diagram

```text
   CвҖІ в”Җв”ҖОҰв”Җв”ҖвҶ’ C

   YвҖІ в”Җв”Җqв”Җв”ҖвҶ’ Y
```

commutes, where we write `C = Spec(рқ’®)` and `CвҖІ = Spec(рқ’®вҖІ)`. _Suppose further that
`рқ’®вӮҖ = рқ’Ә_Y` and `рқ’®вҖІвӮҖ = рқ’Ә_{YвҖІ}`_; let `Оө : Y вҶ’ C` and `ОөвҖІ : YвҖІ вҶ’ CвҖІ` be the

```text
   в”Ӯ         в”Ӯ
  ОөвҖІ         Оө                                                              (8.5.1.3)
   вҶ“         вҶ“
   CвҖІ в”Җв”ҖОҰв”Җв”ҖвҶ’ C
```

which corresponds to the diagram

```text
   рқ’® в”Җв”ҖПҶв”Җв”ҖвҶ’ рқ’®вҖІ
   в”Ӯ         в”Ӯ
```

where the vertical arrows are the augmentation homomorphisms, and so commutativity
follows from the hypothesis that `ПҶ` is a homomorphism of _graded_ algebras.

**Proposition.**

<!-- label: II.8.5.2 -->

If `E` (resp. `EвҖІ`) is the punctured affine cone defined by `рқ’®` (resp. `рқ’®вҖІ`), then
`ОҰвҒ»В№(E) вҠӮ EвҖІ`; if furthermore `Proj(ПҶ) : G(ПҶ) вҶ’ Proj(рқ’®)` is everywhere defined
(or, equivalently, if `G(ПҶ) = Proj(рқ’®вҖІ)`), then `ОҰвҒ»В№(E) = EвҖІ`, and conversely.

**Proof.** The first claim follows from the commutativity of (8.5.1.3). To prove
the second, we may restrict to the case where `Y = Spec(A)` and `YвҖІ = Spec(AвҖІ)`
are affine, and `рқ’® = SМғ`, `рқ’®вҖІ = SМғвҖІ`. For every homogeneous `f` in `S_+`, writing
`fвҖІ = ПҶ(f)`, we have `ОҰвҒ»В№(C_f) = CвҖІ_{fвҖІ}` `(I, 2.2.4.1)`; saying that
`G(ПҶ) = Proj(SвҖІ)` implies that the radical (in `SвҖІ_+`) of the ideal generated by
the `fвҖІ = ПҶ(f)` is `SвҖІ_+` itself ((2.8.1) and (2.3.14)), and this is equivalent
to saying that the `CвҖІ_{fвҖІ}` cover `EвҖІ` (8.3.4.4).

**(8.5.3)**

<!-- label: II.8.5.3 -->

The `q`-morphism `ПҶ` canonically extends to a `q`-morphism of graded algebras

```text
  ПҶМӮ : рқ’®МӮ вҶ’ рқ’®МӮвҖІ                                                               (8.5.3.1)
```

by setting `ПҶМӮ(рқҗі) = рқҗі`. This induces a morphism

```text
  ОҰМӮ = Proj(ПҶМӮ) : G(ПҶМӮ) вҶ’ ДҲ = Proj(рқ’®МӮ)
```

such that the diagram

```text
   G(ПҶМӮ) в”Җв”ҖОҰМӮв”Җв”ҖвҶ’ ДҲ
    в”Ӯ           в”Ӯ
    вҶ“           вҶ“
    YвҖІ в”Җв”Җqв”Җв”Җв”ҖвҶ’ Y
```

commutes (3.5.6). It follows immediately from the definitions that, if we write
`i : C вҶ’ ДҲ` and `iвҖІ : CвҖІ вҶ’ ДҲвҖІ` to mean the canonical open immersions (8.3.2),
then `iвҖІ(CвҖІ) вҠӮ G(ПҶМӮ)`, and the diagram

```text
   CвҖІ в”Җв”ҖОҰв”Җв”ҖвҶ’ C
   в”Ӯ         в”Ӯ
   iвҖІ        i                                                              (8.5.3.2)

   G(ПҶМӮ) в”ҖОҰМӮв”ҖвҶ’ ДҲ
```

commutes. Finally, if we let `X = Proj(рқ’®)` and `XвҖІ = Proj(рқ’®вҖІ)`, and if
`j : X вҶ’ ДҲ` and `jвҖІ : XвҖІ вҶ’ ДҲвҖІ` are the canonical closed immersions (8.3.2), then
it follows from the definition of these immersions that `jвҖІ(G(ПҶ)) вҠӮ G(ПҶМӮ)`, and
that the diagram

```text
   G(ПҶ) в”Җв”ҖProj(ПҶ)в”Җв”ҖвҶ’ X
    в”Ӯ                в”Ӯ
    jвҖІ               j                                                      (8.5.3.3)
    вҶ“                вҶ“
   G(ПҶМӮ) в”Җв”Җв”ҖОҰМӮв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”ҖвҶ’ ДҲ
```

commutes.

**Proposition.**

<!-- label: II.8.5.4 -->

If `ГҠ` (resp. `ГҠвҖІ`) is the punctured projective cone defined by `рқ’®` (resp. by
`рқ’®вҖІ`), then `ОҰМӮвҒ»В№(ГҠ) вҠӮ ГҠвҖІ`; furthermore, if `p : ГҠ вҶ’ X` and `pвҖІ : ГҠвҖІ вҶ’ XвҖІ` are the
canonical retractions, then `pвҖІ(ОҰМӮвҒ»В№(ГҠ)) вҠӮ G(ПҶ)`, and the diagram

```text
   ОҰМӮвҒ»В№(ГҠ) в”Җв”ҖОҰМӮв”Җв”ҖвҶ’ ГҠ
       в”Ӯ           в”Ӯ
       pвҖІ          p                                                        (8.5.4.1)
       вҶ“           вҶ“
     G(ПҶ) в”ҖProj(ПҶ)в”ҖвҶ’ X
```

commutes. If `Proj(ПҶ)` is everywhere defined, then so too is `ОҰМӮ`, and we have
`ОҰМӮвҒ»В№(ГҠ) = ГҠвҖІ`.

**Proof.** The first claim follows from the commutativity of diagrams (8.5.1.3)
and (8.5.3.2), and the two following claims from the definition of the canonical
retractions (8.3.5) and the definition of `ПҶМӮ`. To see that `ОҰМӮ` is everywhere
defined when `Proj(ПҶ)` is, we may restrict to the case where `Y = Spec(A)` and
`YвҖІ = Spec(AвҖІ)` are affine, and `рқ’® = SМғ` and `рқ’®вҖІ = SМғвҖІ`; the hypothesis is that,
when `f` runs over the set of homogeneous elements of `S_+`, the radical in `SвҖІ_+`
of the ideal generated in `SвҖІ_+` by the `ПҶ(f)` is `SвҖІ_+` itself; we immediately
conclude that the radical in `(SвҖІ[рқҗі])_+` of the ideal generated by `рқҗі` and the
`ПҶ(f)` is `(SвҖІ[рқҗі])_+` itself, whence our claim; this also shows that `ГҠвҖІ` is the
union of the `ДҲвҖІ_{ПҶ(f)}`, and hence equal to `ОҰМӮвҒ»В№(ГҠ)`.

**Corollary.**

<!-- label: II.8.5.5 -->

Whenever `Proj(ПҶ)` is everywhere defined, the inverse image under `ОҰМӮ` of the
underlying space of the locus at infinity (resp. of the apex prescheme) of `ДҲвҖІ`
is the underlying space of the locus at infinity (resp. of the apex prescheme) of
`ДҲ`.

**Proof.** This follows immediately from (8.5.4) and (8.5.2), taking into account
equalities (8.3.4.1) and (8.3.4.2).

## 8.6. A canonical isomorphism for punctured cones

**(8.6.1)**

<!-- label: II.8.6.1 -->

Let `Y` be a prescheme, `рқ’®` a quasi-coherent positively-graded `рқ’Ә_Y`-algebra _such
that `рқ’®вӮҖ = рқ’Ә_Y`_, and let `X` be the `Y`-scheme `Proj(рқ’®)`. We are going to apply
the results of В§8.5 to the case where `YвҖІ = X` and `q : X вҶ’ Y` is the structure
morphism; let

```text
  рқ’®_X = вҠ•_{n вҲҲ в„Ө} рқ’Ә_X(n)                                                    (8.6.1.1)
```

<!-- original page 172 -->

which is a quasi-coherent graded `рқ’Ә_X`-algebra, with multiplication defined by
means of the canonical homomorphisms (3.2.6.1)

```text
  рқ’Ә_X(m) вҠ—_{рқ’Ә_X} рқ’Ә_X(n) вҶ’ рқ’Ә_X(m+n)
```

whose associativity is ensured by the commutative diagram (2.5.11.4). Let `рқ’®вҖІ` be
the quasi-coherent positively-graded `рқ’Ә_X`-subalgebra
`рқ’®_X^вүҘ = вҠ•_{nвүҘ0} рқ’Ә_X(n)` of `рқ’®_X`.

Finally, consider the canonical `q`-morphism

```text
  Оұ : рқ’® вҶ’ рқ’®_X^вүҘ                                                              (8.6.1.2)
```

defined in (3.3.2.3) as a homomorphism `рқ’® вҶ’ q_*(рқ’®_X)`, but which clearly sends
`рқ’®` to `q_*(рқ’®_X^вүҘ)`. Write

```text
  C_X = Spec(рқ’®_X^вүҘ),    ДҲ_X = Proj(рқ’®_X^вүҘ[рқҗі]),    XвҖІ = Proj(рқ’®_X^вүҘ)            (8.6.1.3)
```

and denote by `E_X` and `ГҠ_X` the corresponding punctured affine and punctured
projective cones (respectively); denote the canonical morphisms defined in В§8.3
by `Оө_X : X вҶ’ C_X`, `i_X : C_X вҶ’ ДҲ_X`, `j_X : XвҖІ вҶ’ ДҲ_X`, `p_X : ГҠ_X вҶ’ XвҖІ`, and
`ПҖ_X : E_X вҶ’ XвҖІ`.

**Proposition.**

<!-- label: II.8.6.2 -->

The structure morphism `u : XвҖІ вҶ’ X` is an _isomorphism_, and the morphism
`Proj(Оұ)` is everywhere defined and identical to `u`. The morphism
`Proj(ОұМӮ) : ДҲ_X вҶ’ ДҲ` is everywhere defined, and its restrictions to `ГҠ_X` and
`E_X` are _isomorphisms_ onto `ГҠ` and `E` respectively. Finally, if we identify
`XвҖІ` with `X` via `u`, then the morphisms `p_X` and `ПҖ_X` are identified with the
structure morphisms of the `X`-preschemes `ГҠ_X` and `E_X`.

**Proof.** We may clearly restrict to the case where `Y = Spec(A)` is affine, and
`рқ’® = SМғ`; then `X` is the union of affine open subsets `X_f`, where `f` runs over
the set of homogeneous elements of `S_+`, with the ring of each `X_f` being
`S_{(f)}`. It follows from (8.2.7.1) that

```text
  О“(X_f, рқ’®_X^вүҘ) = S_f^вүҘ.                                                    (8.6.2.1)
```

So `uвҒ»В№(X_f) = Proj(S_f^вүҘ)`. But if `f вҲҲ S_d` (`d > 0`), then `Proj(S_f^вүҘ)` is
canonically isomorphic to `Proj((S_f^вүҘ)^{(d)})` (2.4.7), and we also know that
`(S_f^вүҘ)^{(d)} = (S^{(d)})_f^вүҘ` may be identified with `S_{(f)}[T]` (2.2.1) by the
map `T вҶҰ f/1`; we thus conclude (3.1.7) that the structure morphism
`uвҒ»В№(X_f) вҶ’ X_f` is an isomorphism, whence the first claim. To prove the second,
note that the restriction `uвҒ»В№(X_f) вҲ© G(Оұ) вҶ’ X = Proj(S)` of `Proj(Оұ)` corresponds
to the canonical map `x вҶҰ x/1` from `S` to `S_f^вүҘ` (2.6.2); we deduce first that
`G(Оұ) = XвҖІ`, and then, taking into account the fact that
`uвҒ»В№(X_f) = (uвҒ»В№(X_f))_{f/1}`, that it follows from (2.8.1.1) that the image of
`uвҒ»В№(X_f)` under `Proj(Оұ)` is contained in `X_f`, and the restriction of
`Proj(Оұ)` to `uвҒ»В№(X_f)`, considered as a morphism to `X_f = Spec(S_{(f)})`, is
indeed identical to that of `u`. Finally, applying (8.3.5.4) to `p_X` instead of
`p`, we see that `p_XвҒ»В№(uвҒ»В№(X_f)) = Spec((S_f^вүҘ)_{f/1}^вүӨ)`, and this open subset
is, by (8.5.4.1), the inverse image under `Proj(ОұМӮ)` of
`pвҒ»В№(X_f) = Spec(S_f^вүӨ)` (8.3.5.3). Taking (8.2.3.2) into account, the
restriction of `Proj(ОұМӮ)` to `p_XвҒ»В№(uвҒ»В№(X_f))` corresponds to the isomorphism
inverse to (8.2.7.2), restricted to `S_f^вүӨ`, whence the third claim; the last
claim is evident by definition.

<!-- original page 173 -->

We note also that it follows from the commutative diagram (8.5.3.2) that _the
restriction to `C_X` of `Proj(ОұМӮ)` is exactly the morphism `Spec(Оұ)`_.

**Corollary.**

<!-- label: II.8.6.3 -->

Considered as `X`-schemes, `ГҠ_X` is canonically isomorphic to `Spec(рқ’®_X^вүӨ)`, and
`E_X` to `Spec(рқ’®_X)`.

**Proof.** Since we know that the morphisms `p_X` and `ПҖ_X` are affine ((8.3.5)
and (8.3.6)), it suffices (given (1.3.1)) to prove the corollary in the case
where `Y = Spec(A)` is affine and `рқ’® = SМғ`. The first claim follows from the
existence of the canonical isomorphisms (8.2.7.2)
`(S_f^вүҘ)_{f/1}^вүӨ вҘІ S_f^вүӨ` and from the fact that these isomorphisms are
compatible with the map sending `f` to `f g` (where `f` and `g` are homogeneous in
`S_+`). Similarly, applying (8.3.6.2) to `ПҖ_X` instead of `ПҖ`, we see that
`ПҖ_XвҒ»В№(uвҒ»В№(X_f)) = Spec((S_f^вүҘ)_{f/1})` for `f` homogeneous in `S_+`, and the
second claim then follows from the existence of the canonical isomorphisms
(8.2.7.2) `(S_f^вүҘ)_{f/1} вҘІ S_f`.

We can then say that `ДҲ_X`, considered as an `X`-scheme, is given by _gluing_ the
affine `X`-schemes `C_X = Spec(рқ’®_X^вүҘ)` and `ГҠ_X = Spec(рқ’®_X^вүӨ)` over `X`, where the
intersection of the two affine `X`-schemes is the open subset
`E_X = Spec(рқ’®_X)`.

**Corollary.**

<!-- label: II.8.6.4 -->

Assume that `рқ’Ә_X(1)` is an invertible `рқ’Ә_X`-module, and that `рқ’®_X` is isomorphic
to `вҠ•_{n вҲҲ в„Ө} (рқ’Ә_X(1))^{вҠ— n}` (which will be the case, in particular, whenever
`рқ’®` is generated by `рқ’®_1` ((3.2.5) and (3.2.7))). Then the punctured projective
cone `ГҠ` may be identified with the rank-1 vector bundle `рқ•Қ(рқ’Ә_X(вҲ’1))` on `X`, and
the punctured affine cone `E` with the subprescheme of this vector bundle induced
on the complement of the zero section. With this identification, the canonical
retraction `ГҠ вҶ’ X` is identified with the structure morphism of the `X`-scheme
`рқ•Қ(рқ’Ә_X(вҲ’1))`. Finally, there exists a canonical `Y`-morphism `рқ•Қ(рқ’Ә_X(1)) вҶ’ C`,
whose restriction to the complement of the zero section of `рқ•Қ(рқ’Ә_X(1))` is an
isomorphism from this complement to the punctured affine cone `E`.

**Proof.** If we write `в„’ = рқ’Ә_X(1)`, then `рқ’®_X^вүҘ` is identical to `рқ•Ҡ_{рқ’Ә_X}(в„’)`, so
`ГҠ_X` is canonically identified with `рқ•Қ(в„’вҒ»В№)`, by (8.6.3), and `C_X` with `рқ•Қ(в„’)`.
The morphism `рқ•Қ(в„’) вҶ’ C` is the restriction of `Proj(ОұМӮ)`, and the claims of the
corollary are then particular cases of (8.6.2).

We note that the inverse image under the morphism `рқ•Қ(рқ’Ә_X(1)) вҶ’ C` of the
underlying space of the apex prescheme of `C` is the underlying space of the zero
section of `рқ•Қ(рқ’Ә_X(1))` (8.5.5); but, in general, the corresponding subpreschemes
of `C` and of `рқ•Қ(рқ’Ә_X(1))` are not isomorphic. This problem will be studied below.

## 8.7. Blowing up the projecting cones

**(8.7.1)**

<!-- label: II.8.7.1 -->

Under the conditions of (8.6.1), writing `r = Proj(ОұМӮ)`, we have a commutative
diagram

```text
   X в”Җв”Җi_XвҲҳОө_Xв”Җв”ҖвҶ’ ДҲ_X
   в”Ӯ              в”Ӯ
   q              r                                                         (8.7.1.1)
   вҶ“              вҶ“
   Y в”Җв”ҖiвҲҳОөв”Җв”ҖвҶ’ ДҲ
```

<!-- original page 174 -->

by (8.5.1.3) and (8.5.3.2); furthermore, the restriction of `r` to the complement
`ДҲ_X вҲ– i_X(Оө_X(X))` of the zero section is an _isomorphism_ onto the complement
`ДҲ вҲ– i(Оө(Y))` of the zero section, by (8.6.2). If we suppose, to simplify, that
`Y` is affine, that `рқ’®` is of finite type and generated by `рқ’®_1`, and that `X` is
projective over `Y` and `ДҲ_X` is projective over `X` (5.5.1), then `ДҲ_X` is
projective over `Y` (5.5.5, ii), and _a fortiori_ over `ДҲ` (5.5.5, v). We then
have a projective `Y`-morphism `r : ДҲ_X вҶ’ ДҲ` (whose restriction to `C_X` is a
projective `Y`-morphism `C_X вҶ’ C`) that _contracts `X` to `Y`_ and that induces an
_isomorphism_ when we restrict to the _complements of `X` and `Y`_. We thus have
a connection between `C_X` and `C`, analogous to that which exists between a
blow-up prescheme and the original prescheme (8.1.3). We will effectively show
that `C_X` can be identified with the homogeneous spectrum of a graded
`рқ’Ә_C`-algebra.

**(8.7.2)**

<!-- label: II.8.7.2 -->

Keeping the notation of (8.6.1), consider, for every `n вүҘ 0`, the quasi-coherent
ideal

```text
  рқ’®_{[n]} = вҠ•_{mвүҘn} рқ’®_m                                                     (8.7.2.1)
```

of the graded `рқ’Ә_Y`-algebra `рқ’®`. It is clear that

```text
  рқ’®_{[0]} = рқ’®,    рқ’®_{[n]} вҠӮ рқ’®_{[m]}      for m вүӨ n                          (8.7.2.2)
```

```text
  рқ’®_n В· рқ’®_{[m]} вҠӮ рқ’®_{[m+n]}.                                                (8.7.2.3)
```

```text
  X вҶ’ PвҖІ = Proj(рқ’®вҖІ).
```

Furthermore, since `ПҶ` is injective, `rвҖІ` is also a _dominant immersion_ (3.7.6);
the same argument as for `r` then shows that `rвҖІ` is a _surjective closed
immersion_; since `rвҖІ` factors as `X в”Җв”Җrв”Җв”ҖвҶ’ Proj(рқ’®) в”Җв”ҖОҰв”Җв”ҖвҶ’ Proj(рқ’®вҖІ)`, where

But this implies that `ОҰ` is an _isomorphism_; we may restrict to the case where
`Y = Spec(A)` is affine, and `рқ’® = SМғ` and `рқ’®вҖІ = SМғвҖІ`, with `S` a graded

definition of `Proj(ПҶ)` (2.8.1), we see that it suffices to prove that, if `BвҖІ`
is a subring of a ring `B`, and if the morphism `Spec(B) вҶ’ Spec(BвҖІ)` corresponding
to the canonical injection `BвҖІ вҶ’ B` is a closed immersion, then this morphism is
necessarily an _isomorphism_; but this follows from `(I, 4.2.3)`. Furthermore,
`ОҰ*(рқ’Ә_{PвҖІ}(n)) = рқ’Ә_P(n)` ((3.5.2, ii) and (3.5.4)), and so `rвҖІ*(рқ’Ә_{PвҖІ}(n))` is
isomorphic to `в„’^{вҠ— n}` (4.6.3). Let `рқ’®вҖі = рқ’®вҖІ^{(d)}`, so that (3.1.8, i) `X` is
canonically identified with `PвҖі = Proj(рқ’®вҖі)`, and `в„’вҖі = в„’^{вҠ— d}` with `рқ’Ә_{PвҖі}(1)`
(3.2.9, ii).

Now, if `CвҖі = Spec(рқ’®вҖі)`, then `рқ’®_{PвҖі}^вүҘ = вҠ•_{nвүҘ0} рқ’Ә_{PвҖі}(n)` can be identified
with `вҠ•_{nвүҘ0} в„’вҖі^{вҠ— n}`, and thus `C_{PвҖі} = Spec(рқ’®_{PвҖі}^вүҘ)` with `рқ•Қ(в„’вҖі)`; we
also know (8.7.3) that `C_{PвҖі}` is `CвҖі`-isomorphic to `Proj(рқ’®вҖі^вҷ®)`; by the
definition of `рқ’®вҖі`, we know that `рқ’®вҖі^вҷ®` is generated by `рқ’®_1вҖі^вҷ®`, and that
`рқ’®_1вҖі^вҷ®` is of finite type over `рқ’®_0вҖі^вҷ® = рқ’®вҖі` ((8.2.10, i and iii)), and so
`Proj(рқ’®вҖі^вҷ®)` is _projective_ over `CвҖі` (5.5.1). Consider the diagram

```text
   рқ•Қ(в„’) в”Җв”Җgв”Җв”ҖвҶ’ Spec(рқ’®) = C
    в”Ӯ            в”Ӯ
    u            v                                                          (8.8.4.1)
    вҶ“            вҶ“
```

where `g` and `gвҖі` correspond, by (1.5.6), to the canonical `f`-morphisms

```text
  рқ’® вҶ’ вҠ•_{nвүҘ0} в„’^{вҠ— n}      and     рқ’®вҖі вҶ’ вҠ•_{nвүҘ0} в„’вҖі^{вҠ— n}
```

(3.3.2.3) (see (8.8.5) below), and `v` and `u` to the inclusion morphisms

and so `gвҖі вҲҳ u` is a projective morphism (5.5.5, ii); by commutativity of

**Proposition.**

<!-- label: II.8.8.6 -->

Assume (with the notation of (8.8.5)) that, if we write `f = (f_0, О»)`, then the
homomorphism `О» : рқ’Ә_Y вҶ’ f_*(рқ’Ә_X)` is bijective; then:

1. if we write `g = (g_0, Ој)`, then `Ој : рқ’Ә_C вҶ’ g_*(рқ’Ә_V)` is an isomorphism; and
1. if `X` is integral (resp. locally integral and normal), then `C` is integral
    (resp. normal).

**Proof.** Indeed, the `f`-morphism `П„^вҷӯ` is then an _isomorphism_

```text
  П„^вҷӯ : рқ’® = ПҲ_*(рқ’Ә_C) вҶ’ f_*(ПҖ_*(рқ’Ә_V)) = ПҲ_*(g_*(рқ’Ә_V))
```

and the `Y`-morphism `g` may be considered as that for which the homomorphism
`рқ’ң(g)` (1.1.2) is equal to `П„^вҷӯ`. To see that `Ој` is an isomorphism of
`рқ’Ә_C`-modules, it suffices (1.4.2) to see that
`рқ’ң(Ој) : ПҲ_*(рқ’Ә_C) вҶ’ ПҲ_*(g_*(рқ’Ә_V))` is an isomorphism. But, by Definition (1.1.2),
we have `рқ’ң(Ој) = рқ’ң(g)`, whence the conclusion of (i).

To prove (ii), we may restrict to the case where `Y` is affine, so that `рқ’® = SМғ`,
with

<!-- original page 182 -->

`S = вҠ•_{nвүҘ0} О“(X, в„’^{вҠ— n})`; the hypothesis that `X` is integral implies that
the ring `S` is integral `(I, 7.4.4)`, and thus so too is `C` `(I, 5.1.4)`. To
show that `C` is normal, we use the following lemma:

**Lemma (8.8.6.1).**

<!-- label: II.8.8.6.1 -->

Let `Z` be a normal integral prescheme. Then the ring `О“(Z, рқ’Ә_Z)` is integral and
integrally closed.

**Proof.** It follows from `(I, 8.2.1.1)` that `О“(Z, рқ’Ә_Z)` is the intersection,
in the field of rational functions `в„ӣ(Z)`, of the integrally closed rings `рқ’Ә_z`
over all `z вҲҲ Z`.

With this in mind, we first show that `V` is _locally integral_ and _normal_;
for this, we may restrict to the case where `X = Spec(A)` is affine, with `A` an
integral and integrally closed ring (6.3.8), and `в„’ = рқ’Ә_X`. Since then
`V = Spec(A[T])`, and `A[T]` is integral and integrally closed (Bourbaki,
_AlgГЁbre commutative_, p. 99), this proves our claim. For every affine open
subset `U` of `C`, `gвҒ»В№(U)` is quasi-compact, since the morphism `g` is
quasi-compact; since `V` is locally integral, the connected components of
`gвҒ»В№(U)` are open integral preschemes in `gвҒ»В№(U)`, and thus finite in number, and,
since `V` is normal, these preschemes are also normal (6.3.8). Then
`О“(U, рқ’Ә_C)`, which is equal to `О“(gвҒ»В№(U), рқ’Ә_V)` by (i), is the _direct sum_ of
finitely many integral and integrally closed rings (8.8.6.1), which proves that
`C` is normal (6.3.4).

## 8.9. Grauert's ampleness criterion: statement

We intend to show that the properties proved in (8.8.2) _characterise_ `f`-ample
`рқ’Ә_X`-modules, and, more precisely, to prove the following criterion:

**Theorem (8.9.1).**

<!-- label: II.8.9.1 -->

_(Grauert's criterion.)_ Let `Y` be a prescheme, `p : X вҶ’ Y` a separated and
quasi-compact morphism, and `в„’` an invertible `рқ’Ә_X`-module. For `в„’` to be ample
relative to `p`, it is necessary and sufficient that there exist a `Y`-prescheme
`C`, a `Y`-section `Оө : Y вҶ’ C` of `C`, and a `Y`-morphism `q : рқ•Қ(в„’) вҶ’ C`,
satisfying the following properties:

1. the diagram

    ```text
       X в”Җв”Җjв”Җв”ҖвҶ’ рқ•Қ(в„’)
       в”Ӯ         в”Ӯ
       p         q                                                          (8.9.1.1)
       вҶ“         вҶ“
       Y в”Җв”ҖОөв”Җв”ҖвҶ’ C
    ```

    commutes, where `j` is the zero section of the vector bundle `рқ•Қ(в„’)`; and
1. the restriction of `q` to `рқ•Қ(в„’) вҲ– j(X)` is a quasi-compact open immersion

    ```text
      рқ•Қ(в„’) вҲ– j(X) вҶ’ C
    ```

    whose image does not intersect `Оө(Y)`.

Note that, if `C` is _separated_ over `Y`, we may, in condition (ii), remove the
hypothesis that the open immersion is quasi-compact; to see that this property
(of quasi-compactness) is in fact a consequence of the other conditions, we may
restrict to the case where `Y` is affine, and the claim then follows from
`(I, 5.5.1, i)` and `(I, 5.5.10)`. We may also remove

<!-- original page 183 -->

the same hypothesis if we assume that `X` is Noetherian, since then `V` is also
Noetherian, and the claim follows from `(I, 6.3.5)`.

**Corollary.**

<!-- label: II.8.9.2 -->

If the morphism `p : X вҶ’ Y` is proper, then we may, in the statement of Theorem
(8.9.1), assume that `q` is proper, and replace "open immersion" by
"isomorphism".

In a more suggestive manner, we may say (whenever `p : X вҶ’ Y` is proper) that _`в„’`
is ample relative to `p` if and only if we can "contract" the zero section of the
vector bundle `рқ•Қ(в„’)` to the base prescheme `Y`._ An important particular case is
that where `Y` is the spectrum of a field, and where the operation of
"contraction" consists of contracting the zero section of `рқ•Қ(в„’)` _to a single
point_.

**(8.9.3)**

<!-- label: II.8.9.3 -->

The necessity of the conditions in Theorem (8.9.1) and Corollary (8.9.2) follows
immediately from (8.8.2) and (8.8.4).

To show that the conditions of (8.9.1) suffice, consider a slightly more general
situation. For this, let (with the notation of (8.8.2))

```text
  рқ’®вҖІ = вҠ•_{nвүҘ0} в„’^{вҠ— n}
```

and

```text
  V = рқ•Қ(в„’) = Spec(рқ’®вҖІ).
```

The closed subprescheme `j(X)`, zero section of `рқ•Қ(в„’)`, is defined by the
quasi-coherent sheaf of ideals `рқ’Ҙ = (рқ’®вҖІ_+)Мғ` of `рқ’Ә_V` (1.4.10). This
`рқ’Ә_V`-module is _invertible_, since this property is local on `X`, and this
reduces to noting that the ideal `T A[T]` in a polynomial ring `A[T]` is a free
cyclic `A[T]`-module. Furthermore, it is immediate (again because the question
is local on `X`) that

```text
  в„’ = j*(рқ’Ҙ)
```

and

```text
  j_*(в„’) = рқ’Ҙ/рқ’ҘВІ.
```

Now, if

```text
  ПҖ : рқ•Қ(в„’) вҶ’ X
```

is the structure morphism, then `ПҖ_*(рқ’Ҙ) = рқ’®вҖІ_+` and `ПҖ_*(рқ’Ҙ/рқ’ҘВІ) = в„’`; there are
thus canonical homomorphisms `в„’ вҶ’ ПҖ_*(рқ’Ҙ) вҶ’ в„’`, the first being the canonical
injection `в„’ вҶ’ рқ’®вҖІ_+`, and the second the canonical projection from `рқ’®вҖІ_+` to
`рқ’®вҖІ_1 = в„’`, and their composition being the identity. We can also canonically
embed `ПҖ_*(рқ’Ҙ) = рқ’®вҖІ_+ = вҠ•_{nвүҘ1} в„’^{вҠ— n}` into the _product_
`вҲҸ_{nвүҘ1} в„’^{вҠ— n} = lim_вҶҗ ПҖ_*(рқ’Ҙ/рқ’Ҙ^{n+1})` (since
`ПҖ_*(рқ’Ҙ/рқ’Ҙ^{n+1}) = в„’ вҠ• в„’^{вҠ— 2} вҠ• вӢҜ вҠ• в„’^{вҠ— n}`), and we thus have canonical
homomorphisms

```text
  в„’ вҶ’ lim_вҶҗ ПҖ_*(рқ’Ҙ/рқ’Ҙ^{n+1}) вҶ’ в„’                                              (8.9.3.1)
```

whose composition is the identity.

With this in mind, the generalisation of (8.9.1) that we are going to prove is
the following:

**Proposition (8.9.4).**

<!-- label: II.8.9.4 -->

Let `Y` be a prescheme, `V` a `Y`-prescheme, and `X` a closed subprescheme of `V`
defined by an ideal `рқ’Ҙ` of `рқ’Ә_V`, which is an _invertible_ `рқ’Ә_V`-module; if
`j : X вҶ’ V` is

<!-- original page 184 -->

the canonical injection, then let `в„’ = j*(рқ’Ҙ) = рқ’Ҙ вҠ—_{рқ’Ә_V} рқ’Ә_X`, so that
`j_*(в„’) = рқ’Ҙ/рқ’ҘВІ`. Assume that the structure morphism `p : X вҶ’ Y` is separated and
quasi-compact, and that the following conditions are satisfied:

1. there exists a `Y`-morphism `ПҖ : V вҶ’ X` of finite type such that
    `ПҖ вҲҳ j = 1_X`, and so `ПҖ_*(рқ’Ҙ/рқ’ҘВІ) = в„’`;
1. there exists a homomorphism of `рқ’Ә_X`-modules
    `ПҶ : в„’ вҶ’ lim_вҶҗ ПҖ_*(рқ’Ҙ/рқ’Ҙ^{n+1})` such that the composition

    ```text
      в„’ в”Җв”ҖПҶв”Җв”ҖвҶ’ lim_вҶҗ ПҖ_*(рқ’Ҙ/рқ’Ҙ^{n+1}) в”Җв”ҖОұв”Җв”ҖвҶ’ ПҖ_*(рқ’Ҙ/рқ’ҘВІ) = в„’
    ```

    (where `Оұ` is the canonical homomorphism) is the identity;
1. there exists a `Y`-prescheme `C`, a `Y`-section `Оө` of `C`, and a
    `Y`-morphism `q : V вҶ’ C` such that the diagram

    ```text
       X в”Җв”Җjв”Җв”ҖвҶ’ V
       в”Ӯ        в”Ӯ
       p        q                                                           (8.9.4.1)
       вҶ“        вҶ“
       Y в”Җв”ҖОөв”Җв”ҖвҶ’ C
    ```

    commutes; and
1. the restriction of `q` to `W = V вҲ– j(X)` is a quasi-compact open immersion

Then `в„’` is ample relative to `p`.

## 8.10. Grauert's ampleness criterion: proof

**Lemma (8.10.1).**

<!-- label: II.8.10.1 -->

Let `ПҖ : V вҶ’ X` be a morphism, `j : X вҶ’ V` an `X`-section of `V` that is also a
closed immersion, and `рқ’Ҙ` a quasi-coherent sheaf of ideals of `рқ’Ә_V` that defines
the closed subprescheme of `V` associated to `j`. Then the following all hold.

1. For every `n вүҘ 0`, `ПҖ_*(рқ’Ә_V/рқ’Ҙ^{n+1})` and `ПҖ_*(рқ’Ҙ/рқ’Ҙ^{n+1})` are quasi-coherent
    `рқ’Ә_X`-modules, and `ПҖ_*(рқ’Ә_V/рқ’Ҙ) = рқ’Ә_X` and `ПҖ_*(рқ’Ҙ/рқ’ҘВІ) = j*(рқ’Ҙ)`.
1. If `X = {Оҫ} = Spec(k)`, where `k` is a field, then `lim_вҶҗ ПҖ_*(рқ’Ә_V/рқ’Ҙ^{n+1})`
    is isomorphic to the separated completion of the local ring `рқ’Ә_{j(Оҫ)}` for
    the `рқ”Ә_{j(Оҫ)}`-preadic topology.
1. Assume that `рқ’Ҙ` is an invertible `рқ’Ә_V`-module (which implies that

    ```text
      в„’ = j*(рқ’Ҙ) = ПҖ_*(рқ’Ҙ/рқ’ҘВІ)
    ```

    is an invertible `рқ’Ә_X`-module), and that there exists a homomorphism
    `ПҶ : в„’ вҶ’ lim_вҶҗ ПҖ_*(рқ’Ҙ/рқ’Ҙ^{n+1})` such that the composition
    `в„’ в”Җв”ҖПҶв”Җв”ҖвҶ’ lim_вҶҗ ПҖ_*(рқ’Ҙ/рқ’Ҙ^{n+1}) в”Җв”ҖОұв”Җв”ҖвҶ’ ПҖ_*(рқ’Ҙ/рқ’ҘВІ)` (where `Оұ` is the canonical
    homomorphism) is the identity. If we write `рқ’® = вҠ•_{nвүҘ0} в„’^{вҠ— n}`, then `ПҶ`
    canonically induces an isomorphism of `рқ’Ә_X`-algebras from the completion
    `рқ’®МӮ` of `рқ’®` relative to its canonical filtration (the completion being
    isomorphic to the product `вҲҸ_{nвүҘ0} в„’^{вҠ— n}`) to `lim_вҶҗ ПҖ_*(рқ’Ә_V/рқ’Ҙ^{n+1})`.

**Proof.** Note first that the support of the `рқ’Ә_V`-module `рқ’Ә_V/рқ’Ҙ^{n+1}` is
`j(X)`, and the support of `рқ’Ҙ/рқ’Ҙ^{n+1}` is contained in `j(X)`. In the case of
(ii), `j(X)` is a closed point `j(Оҫ)` of `V`,

<!-- original page 185 -->

and, by definition, `ПҖ_*(рқ’Ә_V/рқ’Ҙ^{n+1})` is the fibre of `рқ’Ә_V/рқ’Ҙ^{n+1}` at the point
`j(Оҫ)`, or, equivalently, setting `C = рқ’Ә_{j(Оҫ)}` and denoting by `рқ”Ә` the maximal
ideal of `C`, the `C`-module `C/рқ”Ә^{n+1}`; claim (ii) is then evident.

To prove (i), note that the question is local on `X`; we may thus restrict to
the case where `X` is affine. Let `U` be an affine open subset of `V`; then
`j(X) вҲ© U` is an affine open subset of `j(X)`, so `U_0 = ПҖ(j(X) вҲ© U)`, which is
isomorphic to it, is an affine open subset of `X`; for every affine open subset
`W_0 вҠӮ U_0` in `X`, `W = ПҖвҒ»В№(W_0) вҲ© U` is an affine open subset of `V`, since
`X` is a scheme `(I, 5.5.10)`; in particular, `UвҖІ = U вҲ© ПҖвҒ»В№(U_0)` is an affine
open subset of `V`, and clearly `ПҖ(UвҖІ) = U_0` and `j(U_0) = j(X) вҲ© U`. Then, by
definition, `О“(W_0, ПҖ_*(рқ’Ә_V/рқ’Ҙ^{n+1})) = О“(ПҖвҒ»В№(W_0), рқ’Ә_V/рқ’Ҙ^{n+1})`; but since
every point of `ПҖвҒ»В№(W_0)` not belonging to `j(W_0)` has an open neighbourhood in
`ПҖвҒ»В№(W_0)` not intersecting `j(X)`, and in which `рқ’Ә_V/рқ’Ҙ^{n+1}` is thus zero, it

## 8.11. Uniqueness of contractions

**Lemma (8.11.1).**

<!-- label: II.8.11.1 -->

Let `U` and `V` be preschemes, and `h = (h_0, О») : U вҶ’ V` a surjective morphism.
Suppose that:

1. `О» : рқ’Ә_V вҶ’ h_*(рқ’Ә_U) = (h_0)_*(рқ’Ә_U)` is an isomorphism;
1. the underlying space of `V` may be identified with the quotient of the
    underlying space of `U` by the relation `h_0(x) = h_0(y)` (_a condition that
    always holds whenever the morphism `h` is open or closed, or, a fortiori,
    when `h` is proper._)

Then, for every prescheme `W`, the map

```text
  Hom(V, W) вҶ’ Hom(U, W)                                                    (8.11.1.1)
```

that, to each morphism `v = (v_0, ОҪ)` from `V` to `W`, associates the morphism
`u = v вҲҳ h = (u_0, Ој)`, is a bijection from `Hom(V, W)` to the set of `u` such
that `u_0` is constant on every fibre `h_0вҒ»В№(x)`.

**Proof.** It is clear that, if `u = v вҲҳ h`, so that `u_0 = v_0 вҲҳ h_0`, then
`u_0` is constant on every set `h_0вҒ»В№(x)`. Conversely, if `u` has this property,
we will show that there exists exactly one `v вҲҲ Hom(V, W)` such that
`u = v вҲҳ h`. The existence and uniqueness of the continuous map `v_0 : V вҶ’ W`
such that `u_0 = v_0 вҲҳ h_0` follows from the hypotheses, since `h_0` may be
identified with the canonical map from `U` to `U/R`. We may also, replacing `V`
by some isomorphic prescheme if necessary, suppose that `О»` is the identity; by
hypothesis, `Ој` is then a homomorphism
`Ој : рқ’Ә_W вҶ’ (u_0)_*(рқ’Ә_U) = (v_0)_*((h_0)_*(рқ’Ә_U))` such that the corresponding
homomorphism `Ој^вҷҜ : u_0*(рқ’Ә_W) вҶ’ рқ’Ә_U` is _local_ on every fibre. Since
`(v_0)_*((h_0)_*(рқ’Ә_U)) = (v_0)_*(рқ’Ә_V)`, we necessarily have `ОҪ = Ој`, and
everything then reduces to showing that the corresponding homomorphism
`ОҪ^вҷҜ : v_0*(рқ’Ә_W) вҶ’ рқ’Ә_V` is local on every fibre. But every `y вҲҲ V` is of the
form `h_0(x)` for some `x вҲҲ U`; let `z = v_0(y) = u_0(x)`. Then `(0, 3.5.5)` the
homomorphism `Ој_x^вҷҜ` factors as

```text
  Ој_x^вҷҜ : рқ’Ә_z в”Җв”ҖОҪ_y^вҷҜв”Җв”ҖвҶ’ рқ’Ә_y в”Җв”ҖО»_x^вҷҜв”Җв”ҖвҶ’ рқ’Ә_x.
```

By hypothesis, `О»_x^вҷҜ` and `Ој_x^вҷҜ` are local homomorphisms; thus `О»_x^вҷҜ` sends
every invertible element of `рқ’Ә_y` to an invertible element of `рқ’Ә_x`; if
`ОҪ_y^вҷҜ` sent a non-invertible element of `рқ’Ә_z` to an invertible element of
`рқ’Ә_y`, then `Ој_x^вҷҜ` would send this element of `рқ’Ә_z` to an invertible element
of `рқ’Ә_x`, contradicting the hypothesis, whence the lemma.

**Corollary.**

<!-- label: II.8.11.2 -->

Let `U` be an integral prescheme, and `V` a normal prescheme; then every
morphism `h : U вҶ’ V` that is universally closed, birational, and radicial, is
also an isomorphism.

**Proof.** If `h = (h_0, О»)`, then it follows from the hypotheses that `h_0` is
injective and closed, and that `h_0(U)`

<!-- original page 190 -->

is dense in `V`, and so `h_0` is a _homeomorphism_ from `U` to `V`. To prove the
corollary, it will suffice to show that `О» : рқ’Ә_V вҶ’ (h_0)_*(рқ’Ә_U)` is an
isomorphism: we can then apply (8.11.1), which proves that the map (8.11.1.1) is
bijective (the fibres `h_0вҒ»В№(x)` each consisting of a single point); thus `h`
will be an isomorphism. The question being clearly local on `V`, we may suppose
that `V = Spec(A)` is affine, of an integral and integrally closed ring
(8.8.6.1); `h` then corresponds `(I, 2.2.4)` to a homomorphism
`ПҶ : A вҶ’ О“(U, рқ’Ә_U)`, and everything reduces to showing that `ПҶ` is an
isomorphism. But, if `K` is the field of fractions of `A`, then `О“(U, рқ’Ә_U)` has,
by hypothesis, `K` as its field of fractions, and `A` is a subring of
`О“(U, рқ’Ә_U)`, with `ПҶ` being the canonical injection `(I, 8.2.7)`. Since the
morphism `h` satisfies the hypotheses of (7.3.11), `О“(U, рқ’Ә_U)` is a subring of
the integral closure of `A` in `K`, and is thus identical to `A` by hypothesis.

**Remark.**

<!-- label: II.8.11.3 -->

We will see in Chapter III `(III, 4.4.11)` that, whenever `V` is a _locally
Noetherian_ prescheme, every morphism `h : U вҶ’ V` that is proper and quasi-finite
(in particular, every morphism satisfying the hypotheses of (8.11.2)) is
necessarily _finite_. The conclusion of (8.11.2) then follows in this case from
(6.1.15).

**(8.11.4)**

<!-- label: II.8.11.4 -->

We will now see that, in Grauert's criterion, we can often prove that the
prescheme `C` and the "contraction" `q` are determined in an _essentially
unique_ manner.

**Lemma (8.11.5).**

<!-- label: II.8.11.5 -->

Let `Y` be a prescheme, `p : X вҶ’ Y` a proper morphism, `в„’` a `p`-ample invertible
`рқ’Ә_X`-module, `C` a `Y`-prescheme, `Оө : Y вҶ’ C` a `Y`-section, and
`q : V = рқ•Қ(в„’) вҶ’ C` a `Y`-morphism, all such that the diagram (8.9.1.1) commutes.
Suppose further that, if `p = (p_0, Оё)`, then `Оё : рқ’Ә_Y вҶ’ p_*(рқ’Ә_X)` is an
isomorphism. Let `рқ’®вҖІ = вҠ•_{nвүҘ0} p_*(в„’^{вҠ— n})` and `CвҖІ = Spec(рқ’®вҖІ)`, and let
`qвҖІ : рқ•Қ(в„’) вҶ’ CвҖІ` be the canonical `Y`-morphism (8.8.5). Then there exists
exactly one `Y`-morphism `u : CвҖІ вҶ’ C` such that `q = u вҲҳ qвҖІ`.

**Proof.** The hypothesis on `Оё` implies, in particular, that `p` is surjective;
since, by (8.8.4), the restriction of `qвҖІ` to `рқ•Қ(в„’) вҲ– j(X)` is an _isomorphism_
onto `CвҖІ вҲ– ОөвҖІ(Y)` (where `ОөвҖІ` is the apex section of `CвҖІ`), it follows from
(8.8.4) that `qвҖІ` is _proper_ and _surjective_; furthermore, by (8.8.6), if we
let `qвҖІ = (qвҖІ_0, П„)`, then `П„ : рқ’Ә_{CвҖІ} вҶ’ qвҖІ_*(рқ’Ә_V)` is an isomorphism. We are
thus in a situation where we can apply (8.11.1), and we will have proved the
lemma if we show that `q` is constant on every fibre `qвҖІвҒ»В№(zвҖІ)` for `zвҖІ вҲҲ CвҖІ`.
But this condition is trivially satisfied for `zвҖІ вҲү ОөвҖІ(Y)`. If `zвҖІ вҲҲ ОөвҖІ(Y)`,
then there exists exactly one `y вҲҲ Y` such that `zвҖІ = ОөвҖІ(y)`, and, by the
commutativity of (8.8.5.2) and the fact that `qвҖІ` sends `рқ•Қ(в„’) вҲ– j(X)` to
`CвҖІ вҲ– ОөвҖІ(Y)`, `qвҖІвҒ»В№(zвҖІ) = j(pвҒ»В№(y))`; the commutativity of (8.9.1.1) then
proves our claim.

**Corollary.**

<!-- label: II.8.11.6 -->

Under the hypotheses of (8.11.5), suppose further that `q` is proper, and that
the restriction of `q` to `рқ•Қ(в„’) вҲ– j(X)` is an isomorphism onto `C вҲ– Оө(Y)`. Then
the morphism `u` is universally closed, surjective, and radicial, and its
restriction to `CвҖІ вҲ– ОөвҖІ(Y)` is an isomorphism onto `C вҲ– Оө(Y)`.

**Proof.** Since `qвҖІ` is an isomorphism from `рқ•Қ(в„’) вҲ– j(X)` to `CвҖІ вҲ– ОөвҖІ(Y)`
(8.8.4), the last claim follows immediately from the fact that `q = u вҲҳ qвҖІ`.
Furthermore, the commutativity of the diagrams

<!-- original page 191 -->

(8.8.5.2) and (8.9.1.1) shows that the restriction of `u` to the closed
subprescheme `ОөвҖІ(Y)` of `CвҖІ` is an isomorphism onto the closed subprescheme
`Оө(Y)` of `C`, from which we immediately deduce that, for every `zвҖІ вҲҲ ОөвҖІ(Y)`,
if `z = u(zвҖІ)`, then `u` defines an isomorphism from `Оә(z)` to `Оә(zвҖІ)`. These
remarks prove that `u` is bijective and radicial; furthermore, if
`ПҲ : C вҶ’ Y` and `ПҲвҖІ : CвҖІ вҶ’ Y` are the structure morphisms, then
`ПҲвҖІ = ПҲ вҲҳ u`, and, since `ПҲвҖІ` is separated (1.2.4), so too is `u`
`(I, 5.5.1, v)`. We have already seen, in the proof of (8.11.5), that `qвҖІ` is
surjective; since `q = u вҲҳ qвҖІ` is proper, we finally conclude, from (5.4.3) and
(5.4.9), that `u` is universally closed.

**Proposition.**

<!-- label: II.8.11.7 -->

Let `Y` be a prescheme, `X` an _integral_ prescheme, `p : X вҶ’ Y` a proper
morphism, `в„’` a `p`-ample invertible `рқ’Ә_X`-module, `C` a _normal_ `Y`-prescheme,
`Оө : Y вҶ’ C` a `Y`-section, and `q : V = рқ•Қ(в„’) вҶ’ C` a `Y`-morphism, all such that
the diagram (8.9.1.1) commutes. Suppose further that, if `p = (p_0, Оё)`, then
`Оё : рқ’Ә_Y вҶ’ p_*(рқ’Ә_X)` is an isomorphism. Let `рқ’®вҖІ = вҠ•_{nвүҘ0} p_*(в„’^{вҠ— n})` and
`CвҖІ = Spec(рқ’®вҖІ)`, and let `qвҖІ : рқ•Қ(в„’) вҶ’ CвҖІ` be the canonical `Y`-morphism (8.8.5).
Then the unique `Y`-morphism `u : CвҖІ вҶ’ C` such that `q = u вҲҳ qвҖІ` is an
_isomorphism_.

**Proof.** It follows from (8.8.6) that `CвҖІ` is integral; since `u` is a
homeomorphism of the underlying subspaces `CвҖІ вҶ’ C` (`u` being bijective and
closed, by (8.11.6)), `C` is irreducible, thus integral, and, since the
restriction of `u` to a non-empty open subset of `CвҖІ` is an isomorphism onto an
open subset of `C`, `u` is birational. Since `C` is assumed to be normal, it
suffices to apply (8.11.2) to obtain the conclusion.

**Remark.**

<!-- label: II.8.11.8 -->

1. The hypothesis that `C` is normal implies that `X` is also normal. Indeed,
    `CвҖІ = Spec(рқ’®вҖІ)` is then normal, being isomorphic to `C`, and integral, by
    (8.8.6); we thus conclude that `Proj(рқ’®вҖІ)` is _normal_. Indeed, the question
    is local on `Y`; if `Y` is affine, with `рқ’®вҖІ = SМғвҖІ`, then the ring
    `SвҖІ = О“(CвҖІ, рқ’®вҖІ)` is integral and integrally closed (8.8.6.1), and so, for
    every homogeneous element `f вҲҲ SвҖІ_+`, the graded ring `SвҖІ_f` is integral and
    integrally closed (Bourbaki, _AlgГЁbre commutative_, t. I, p. 257 and 261),
    and thus so too is the ring `SвҖІ_{(f)}` of its degree-zero terms, because the
    intersection of `SвҖІ_f` with the field of fractions of `SвҖІ_{(f)}` is equal to
    `SвҖІ_{(f)}`; this proves our claim (6.3.4). Finally, since `X` is isomorphic
    to an open subprescheme of `Proj(рқ’®вҖІ)` (8.8.1), `X` is indeed normal. We can
    thus express (8.11.7) in the following form: _If `X` is integral and normal,
    and `p = (p_0, Оё) : X вҶ’ Y` is a proper morphism such that
    `Оё : рқ’Ә_Y вҶ’ p_*(рқ’Ә_X)` is an isomorphism, then, for every `p`-ample
    `рқ’Ә_X`-module `в„’`, there exists exactly one way of contracting the zero
    section of `V = рқ•Қ(в„’)` to obtain a normal `Y`-scheme `C` and a proper
    `Y`-morphism `q : V вҶ’ C`._
1. When `p` is proper, the hypothesis `p_*(рқ’Ә_X) = рқ’Ә_Y` may be considered as an
    auxiliary hypothesis not really restricting the generality of the result.
    Indeed, if it is not satisfied, then it suffices to replace `Y` with the
    `Y`-scheme `YвҖІ = Spec(p_*(рқ’Ә_X))` and to consider `X` as a `YвҖІ`-scheme. We
    will return to this general method in Chapter III, В§4.

## 8.13. Projective closures of submodules and closed subschemes

**(8.13.1)**

<!-- label: II.8.13.1 -->

With hypotheses and notation as in (8.12.1), consider a _not-necessarily graded_
quasi-coherent sub-`рқ’®`-module `рқ’©` of `в„і`. We can then consider the quasi-coherent
`рқ’Ә_C`-module `рқ’©Мғ` associated to `рқ’©`, which is a sub-`рқ’Ә_C`-module of `в„іМғ`. We
have seen elsewhere (8.12.2.1) that `в„іМғ` can be identified with the restriction
of `в„ів–Ў` to `C`. Since the canonical injection `i : C вҶ’ ДҲ` is an affine morphism
(8.3.2), and _a fortiori_ quasi-compact, the _canonical extension_ `(рқ’©Мғ)вҒ»`,
the largest sub-`рқ’Ә_{ДҲ}`-module contained in `в„ів–Ў` and inducing `рқ’©Мғ` on `C`, is a
_quasi-coherent_ `рқ’Ә_{ДҲ}`-module `(I, 9.4.2)`. We will give a more explicit
description by using a graded `рқ’®МӮ`-module.

**(8.13.2)**

<!-- label: II.8.13.2 -->

For this, consider, for every integer `n вүҘ 0`, the homomorphism
`вҠ•_{iвүӨn} в„і_i вҶ’ в„і` which, for every open `U` of `Y`, sends the family

```text
  (s_i) вҲҲ вҠ•_{iвүӨn} О“(U, в„і_i)
```

to the section `вҲ‘_i s_i вҲҲ О“(U, в„і)`. Denote by `рқ’©вҖІ_n` the inverse image of `рқ’©`
under this homomorphism, which is a quasi-coherent sub-`рқ’®`-module of
`вҠ•_{iвүӨn} в„і_i`. Now consider the homomorphism `вҠ•_{iвүӨn} в„і_i вҶ’ в„іМӮ = в„і[рқҗі]` which
sends `(s_i)` to the section `вҲ‘_{iвүӨn} s_i рқҗі^{nвҲ’i} вҲҲ О“(U, в„іМӮ_n)`, and let `рқ’©_n`
be the image of `рқ’©вҖІ_n` under this homomorphism; we immediately have that
`рқ’©М„ = вҠ•_{nвүҘ0} рқ’©_n` is a (quasi-coherent) sub-`рқ’®МӮ`-module of `в„іМӮ`; we say that
`рқ’©М„` is obtained from `рқ’©` by _homogenisation_, via the "homogenisation variable"
`рқҗі`. We note

<!-- original page 196 -->

that, if `рқ’©` is already a _graded_ sub-`рқ’®`-module of `в„і`, then `рқ’©М„` can be
identified with the direct sum of the components `рқ’©МӮ_n` of degree `n вүҘ 0` in
`рқ’©МӮ = рқ’©[рқҗі]`.

**Proposition.**

<!-- label: II.8.13.3 -->

The `рқ’Ә_{ДҲ}`-module `рқ’«рқ“»рқ“ёрқ“івӮҖ(рқ’©М„)` is the canonical extension `(рқ’©Мғ)вҒ»` of `рқ’©Мғ` to `ДҲ`.

**Proof.** The question is local on `Y` and `ДҲ` by the definition of the
canonical extension `(I, 9.4.1)`. We can thus already suppose that
`Y = Spec(A)` is affine, with `рқ’® = SМғ`, `в„і = MМғ`, and `рқ’© = Г‘`, where `N` is a
not-necessarily-graded sub-`S`-module of `M`. Furthermore (8.3.2.6), `ДҲ` is a
union of affine opens `ДҲ_рқҗі = C` and `ДҲ_f = Spec(S_f^вүӨ)` (with `f` homogeneous
in `S_+`). It thus suffices to show that: (1) the restriction of `рқ’«рқ“»рқ“ёрқ“івӮҖ(рқ’©М„)`
to `C` is `рқ’©Мғ`; (2) the restriction of `рқ’«рқ“»рқ“ёрқ“івӮҖ(рқ’©М„)` to each `ДҲ_f` is the
canonical extension of the restriction of `рқ’©` to `C вҲ© ДҲ_f = Spec(S_f)`
(8.3.2.6). For the first point, note that `рқ’«рқ“»рқ“ёрқ“івӮҖ(рқ’©М„)|C` can be identified with
`(NМ„_{(рқҗі)})Мғ` (8.3.2.4); but `NМ„_{(рқҗі)}` is canonically identified (2.2.5) with
the image of `NМ„` in `MМӮ/(рқҗі вҲ’ 1)MМӮ`, and by the canonical isomorphism of the
latter with `M` (8.2.5), this image can be identified with `N`, by the
definition of `NМ„` in (8.13.2).

To prove the second point, note that the injection
`i : C вҲ© ДҲ_f вҶ’ ДҲ_f` corresponds to the canonical injection `S_f^вүӨ вҶ’ S_f`
(8.3.2.6); we also have `О“(ДҲ_f, в„ів–Ў) = M_f^вүӨ`, that `О“(ДҲ_f, i_*(рқ’©Мғ)) = N`, and,
by (8.12.2.1), that `О“(ДҲ_f, i_*(i*(в„ів–Ў))) = M_f`. Taking `(I, 9.4.2)` into
account, we are thus led to showing that `NМ„_{(f)} вҠӮ MМӮ_{(f)} = M_f^вүӨ` is
canonically identified with the inverse image of `N_f` under the canonical
injection `M_f^вүӨ вҶ’ M_f`. Indeed, let `d = deg(f) > 0`, and suppose that an
element `(вҲ‘_{kвүӨmd} x_k)/f^m` of `M_f` (with `x_k вҲҲ M_k`) is of the form `y/f^m`
with `y вҲҲ N`. By multiplying `y` and the `x_k` by one and the same suitable
`f^h`, we may already assume that `вҲ‘_{kвүӨmd} x_k = y`. But, in the identification
of (8.2.5.2), `(вҲ‘_{kвүӨmd} x_k)/f^m` corresponds to
`вҲ‘_{kвүӨmd} x_k рқҗі^{mdвҲ’k}/f^m`, and this is indeed an element of `NМ„_{(f)}`, since
`вҲ‘_{kвүӨmd} x_k вҲҲ N`; the converse is evident.

**Remark.**

<!-- label: II.8.13.4 -->

1. The most important case of application of (8.13.3) is that where `в„і = рқ’®`,
    with `рқ’©Мғ` then being an _arbitrary_ quasi-coherent sheaf of ideals `рқ’Ҙ` of
    `рқ’Ә_C` (1.4.3), corresponding bijectively to a _closed subprescheme_ `Z` of
    `C`. Then the canonical extension `рқ’ҘМ„` of `рқ’Ҙ` is the quasi-coherent sheaf
    of ideals of `рқ’Ә_{ДҲ}` that defines the _closure_ `ZМ„` of `Z` in `ДҲ`
    `(I, 9.5.10)`; Proposition (8.13.3) gives a canonical way of defining `ZМ„`
    by using a graded ideal in `рқ’®МӮ = рқ’®[рқҗі]`.
1. Suppose, to simplify, that `Y` is affine, and adopt the notation from the
    proof of (8.13.3). For every non-zero `x вҲҲ N`, let `d(x)` be the largest
    degree of the homogeneous components `x_i` of `x` in `M`; by definition,
    `NМ„` is the submodule of `MМӮ` consisting of `0` and elements of the form
    `h(x, k) = рқҗі^k вҲ‘_{iвүӨd(x)} x_i рқҗі^{d(x)вҲ’i}` (for integral `k вүҘ 0`); it is
    thus generated, as a module over `Ең = S[рқҗі]`, by the elements of the form

    ```text
      h(x, 0) = вҲ‘_{iвүӨd(x)} x_i рқҗі^{d(x)вҲ’i}.
    ```

    <!-- original page 197 -->

    We say that `h(x, 0)` is obtained from `x` by _homogenisation_ via the
    "homogenisation variable" `рқҗі`. But since `h(x, 0)` does not depend
    additively on `x` (nor _a fortiori_ `S`-linearly), _we will refrain from
    believing_ (even when `M = S`) that the `h(x, 0)` form a _system of
    generators_ of the graded `Ең`-module `NМ„` when we let `x` run over a
    _system of generators_ of the `S`-module `N`. This is, however, the case
    (considered only in elementary algebraic geometry) when `N` is a _free
    cyclic_ `S`-module, since, if `t` is a basis of `N`, then `h(t, 0)`
    generates the `Ең`-module `NМ„`.

**(8.14.1)**

**Proposition.**

<!-- label: II.8.14.3 -->

graded `рқ’®`-module `в„і` admits a finite presentation (3.1.1).

**Proof.** The question is clearly local on `X` and `Y`; we may thus suppose
that `Y = Spec(A)` is affine, with `рқ’® = SМғ`, `в„і = MМғ`, and `рқ’© = Г‘`, where `S`
is a positively-graded `A`-algebra, and `M` and `N` are graded `S`-modules. If
`f` is a homogeneous element of `S_+`, then the homomorphisms (8.14.2.1) and
(8.14.2.2), restricted to the affine open `D_+(f)`, correspond to the canonical
homomorphisms (2.5.11.1) and (2.5.12.1):

```text
  M(m)_{(f)} вҠ—_{S_{(f)}} N(n)_{(f)} вҶ’ (M вҠ—_S N)(m+n)_{(f)}
  (Hom_S(M, N))(n вҲ’ m)_{(f)} вҶ’ Hom_{S_{(f)}}(M(m)_{(f)}, N(n)_{(f)}).
```

(8.2.9.1) into account) that the restriction of (8.14.2.4) to `D_+(f)`
corresponds to the canonical homomorphism

```text

```

defined in `(0, 1.3.4)`, and we know that this latter homomorphism is an
isomorphism. Similarly, the restriction of (8.14.2.3) to `D_+(f)` corresponds to
the canonical homomorphism `(0, 1.3.5)`

```text

```

taking into account the fact that, since `M` is of finite type, the module
`Hom_S(M, N)`, the direct sum of the subgroups consisting of _homogeneous_
homomorphisms of `S`-modules (2.1.2), agrees with the set of _all_
homomorphisms `M вҶ’ N` of `S`-modules. The hypothesis that `M` admits a finite
presentation then implies `(0, 1.3.5)` that the canonical homomorphism in
question is indeed an isomorphism.

**Proposition.**

<!-- label: II.8.14.4 -->

**(8.14.6)**

<!-- label: II.8.14.6 -->

In general, for a quasi-coherent graded `рқ’®_X`-module `рқ“•`, it is not certain that
the graded `рқ’®`-module `рқҡӘ_*(рқ“•)` will necessarily be quasi-coherent. Consider an

```text
  рқ’®_{XвҖІ} = рқ’®_X|XвҖІ = вҠ•_{n вҲҲ в„Ө} рқ’Ә_X(n)|XвҖІ                                     (8.14.6.1)
```

```text
  рқҡӘвҖІ_*(рқ“•вҖІ) = вҠ•_{n вҲҲ в„Ө} qвҖІ_*(рқ“•вҖІ_n).                                          (8.14.6.2)
```

The previous remark then shows that, if `рқ“•вҖІ` is a quasi-coherent
`рқ’®_{XвҖІ}`-module, then `рқҡӘвҖІ_*(рқ“•вҖІ)` is a graded _quasi-coherent_ `рқ’®`-module
`(I, 9.6.1)`.

We note also that the canonical injection `j : XвҖІ вҶ’ X` is _quasi-compact_,
because `qвҖІ = q вҲҳ j` is quasi-compact and `q` is separated `(I, 6.6.4, v)`. Then
`рқ“• = j_*(рқ“•вҖІ)` is a quasi-coherent graded `рқ’®_X`-module for every quasi-coherent
graded `рқ’®_{XвҖІ}`-module `рқ“•вҖІ`, and it follows from the previous definitions that

```text
  рқҡӘвҖІ_*(рқ“•вҖІ) = рқҡӘ_*(рқ“•).                                                        (8.14.6.3)
```

With the same hypotheses on `XвҖІ`, for every quasi-coherent graded `рқ’®`-module
`в„і`, we set

```text
  рқ’«рқ“»рқ“ёрқ“івҖІ(в„і) = рқ’«рқ“»рқ“ёрқ“і(в„і)|XвҖІ                                                   (8.14.6.4)
```

which is a quasi-coherent graded `рқ’®_{XвҖІ}`-module. The canonical homomorphism

```text
  рқ’«рқ“»рқ“ёрқ“і(в„і) вҶ’ j_*(рқ’«рқ“»рқ“ёрқ“івҖІ(в„і))
```

`(0, 4.4.3)` thus gives a canonical homomorphism
`рқҡӘ_*(рқ’«рқ“»рқ“ёрқ“і(в„і)) вҶ’ рқҡӘвҖІ_*(рқ’«рқ“»рқ“ёрқ“івҖІ(в„і))` of graded `рқ’®`-modules, and, by
composition with (8.14.5.4), we obtain a functorial canonical homomorphism (of
degree 0) of quasi-coherent graded `рқ’®`-modules

```text
  ОұвҖІ : в„і вҶ’ рқҡӘвҖІ_*(рқ’«рқ“»рқ“ёрқ“івҖІ(в„і)).                                                 (8.14.6.5)
```

**(8.14.7)**

<!-- label: II.8.14.7 -->

Keeping the hypotheses on `XвҖІ` from (8.14.6), let `рқ“•вҖІ` be a _quasi-coherent
graded `рқ’®_{XвҖІ}`-module_ such that `рқ’«рқ“»рқ“ёрқ“івҖІ(рқҡӘвҖІ_*(рқ“•вҖІ))` is also a graded
_quasi-coherent_ `рқ’®_{XвҖІ}`-module.

<!-- original page 200 -->

We will define a functorial canonical homomorphism (of degree 0) of graded
`рқ’®_{XвҖІ}`-modules

```text
  ОІвҖІ : рқ’«рқ“»рқ“ёрқ“івҖІ(рқҡӘвҖІ_*(рқ“•вҖІ)) вҶ’ рқ“•вҖІ.                                                (8.14.7.1)
```

Suppose first that `Y = Spec(A)` is affine, and that `рқ’® = SМғ`, where `S` is a
positively-graded `A`-algebra; then `рқҡӘвҖІ_*(рқ“•вҖІ) = MМғ`, where
`M = вҠ•_{n вҲҲ в„Ө} О“(XвҖІ, рқ“•вҖІ_n)` is a graded `S`-module. Let `f вҲҲ S_d` be such that
`D_+(f) вҠӮ XвҖІ`; by definition (2.6.2), `Оұ_d(f)` restricted to `D_+(f)` is the
section of `рқ’Ә_X(d)` over `D_+(f)` corresponding to the element `f/1` of
`(S(d))_{(f)}`, and is thus invertible; thus so too is `Оұ_d(fвҒҝ)` for every
`n > 0`. From this, we immediately conclude that we have defined an
`S_f`-homomorphism (of degree 0) of graded modules
`ОІ_f : M_f вҶ’ О“(D_+(f), рқ“•вҖІ)` by sending each element `z/f^n вҲҲ M_f` (where
`z вҲҲ M`) to the section
`(z|D_+(f))(Оұ_d(f^n)|D_+(f))вҒ»В№` of `рқ“•вҖІ` over `D_+(f)`. Furthermore, we have a
commutative diagram corresponding to (2.6.4.1), whence the definition of `ОІвҖІ`
in this case. To pass to the general case, we must consider an `A`-algebra `AвҖІ`,
the graded `AвҖІ`-algebra `SвҖІ = S вҠ—_A AвҖІ`, and use the commutative diagram
analogous to (2.8.13.2); we leave the details to the reader.

**Proposition.**

<!-- label: II.8.14.8 -->

If `XвҖІ` is an open of `X = Proj(рқ’®)` such that `qвҖІ : XвҖІ вҶ’ Y` is quasi-compact,
then the homomorphism `ОІвҖІ` defined in (8.14.7) is bijective.

**Proof.** We can clearly restrict to the case where `Y` is affine, and
everything then reduces to proving (with the notation of (8.14.7)) that the
homomorphism `ОІ_f : M_f вҶ’ О“(D_+(f), рқ“•вҖІ)` is an isomorphism. But replacing `f`
by one of its powers changes neither `D_+(f)` nor `ОІ_f`; since `XвҖІ` is
_quasi-compact_ by hypothesis, we may always assume, by (8.14.4), that the
sheaf `рқ’Ә_X(d)` is _invertible_. Since `XвҖІ` is a scheme (because `qвҖІ` is
separated), the proposition is then exactly `(I, 9.3.1)`.

**Corollary.**

<!-- label: II.8.14.9 -->

Under the hypotheses of (8.14.8), every quasi-coherent graded `рқ’®_{XвҖІ}`-module
is isomorphic to a graded `рқ’®_{XвҖІ}`-module of the form `рқ’«рқ“»рқ“ёрқ“івҖІ(в„і)`, where `в„і`
is a quasi-coherent graded `рқ’®`-module. Further, if `рқ“•вҖІ` is of finite type, and
if we assume that `Y` is a quasi-compact scheme, or a prescheme whose underlying
space is Noetherian, then we can assume that `в„і` is of finite type.

**Proof.** The proof starting from (8.14.8) follows exactly the same route as
the proof of (3.4.5) starting from (3.4.4), and we leave the details to the
reader.

<!-- label: II.8.14.10 -->


**Remark.**

<!-- label: II.8.14.12 -->

The situation described in (8.14.4) is a particular case of the following. Let
`X` be a ringed space, and `рқ’®` a (positively- and negatively-) graded
`рқ’Ә_X`-algebra; suppose that there exists an integer `d > 0` such that `рқ’®_d` and
`рқ’®_{вҲ’d}` are _invertible_, with the canonical homomorphism

```text
  рқ’®_d вҠ—_{рқ’Ә_X} рқ’®_{вҲ’d} вҶ’ рқ’Ә_X                                                 (8.14.12.1)
```

being an _isomorphism_ (so that `рқ’®_{вҲ’d}` is identified with `рқ’®_dвҒ»В№`). We then
say that the graded `рқ’Ә_X`-algebra `рқ’®` is _periodic_, _of period `d`_. This
nomenclature stems from the following property: _under the preceding hypotheses,
for every graded `рқ’®`-module `рқ“•`, the canonical homomorphism_

```text
  рқ’®_d вҠ— рқ“•_n вҶ’ рқ“•_{n+d}                                                      (8.14.12.2)
```

_is an isomorphism for every `n вҲҲ в„Ө`._ Indeed, the question is local on `X`, and
we may assume that `рқ’®_d` has an _invertible_ section `s` over `X`, with its
inverse `sвҖІ` being a section of `рқ’®_{вҲ’d}`. The homomorphism
`рқ“•_{n+d} вҶ’ рқ’®_d вҠ— рқ“•_n`, which sends each section `z вҲҲ О“(U, рқ“•_{n+d})` to the
section `(s|U) вҠ— (sвҖІ|U) z` of `рқ’®_d вҠ— рқ“•_n` over `U`, is then the inverse of
(8.14.12.2), whence our claim. This induces, for every `k вҲҲ в„Ө`, a canonical
isomorphism

```text
  (рқ’®_d)^{вҠ— k} вҠ— рқ“•_n вҘІ рқ“•_{n+kd}.
```

Then _the data of a graded `рқ’®`-module `рқ“•` is equivalent to the data of
`рқ’®_0`-modules `рқ“•_i` (`0 вүӨ i вүӨ d вҲ’ 1`) and canonical homomorphisms_

```text
  рқ’®_i вҠ— рқ“•_j вҶ’ рқ“•_{i+j}      for 0 вүӨ i, j вүӨ d вҲ’ 1
```

(setting `рқ“•_{i+j} = рқ’®_d вҠ—_{рқ’®_0} рқ“•_{i+jвҲ’d}` whenever `i + j вүҘ d`). Of course,
for these homomorphisms to give a well-defined `рқ’®`-module structure on the
direct sum of the `(рқ’®_d)^{вҠ— k} вҠ— рқ“•_i` (`k вҲҲ в„Ө`, `0 вүӨ i вүӨ d вҲ’ 1`), they should
satisfy some associativity conditions that we will not detail.

In the case where `d = 1` (which is the one considered in В§3.3), we may thus
say that the category of graded `рқ’®`-modules (resp. quasi-coherent `рқ’®`-modules if
`X` is a prescheme and `рқ’®` is quasi-coherent) is _equivalent_ to the category
of arbitrary `рқ’®_0`-modules (resp. quasi-coherent `рқ’®_0`-modules); it is in this
way that one can think of the results of this paragraph as generalising those of
В§3. Furthermore, we see that, under suitable finiteness conditions, the results
of this paragraph (along with (8.14.11)) reduces, in some sense, the study of
quasi-coherent graded algebras on a prescheme, and graded modules "modulo (TN)"
on such algebras, to the study of the particular case where the algebras in
question are _periodic_ (and where condition (TN) for `в„і` (3.4.2) thus implies
that `в„і = 0`).

of finite type, and `в„і` a quasi-coherent graded `рқ’®`-module satisfying condition
