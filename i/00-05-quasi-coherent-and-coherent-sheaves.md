# Chapter 0 вҖ” Preliminaries

## В§5. Quasi-coherent and Coherent Sheaves

<!-- label: 0.5 -->

### 5.1. Quasi-coherent sheaves

<!-- label: 0.5.1 -->

**(5.1.1)** Let `(X, рқ’Ә_X)` be a ringed space and `в„ұ` an `рқ’Ә_X`-Module. To give a homomorphism `u : рқ’Ә_X вҶ’ в„ұ` of
`рқ’Ә_X`-Modules is the same as to give the section `s = u(1) вҲҲ О“(X, в„ұ)`. Indeed, once `s` is given, for every section
`t вҲҲ О“(U, рқ’Ә_X)` one necessarily has `u(t) = t В· (s|U)`; we say that `u` is _defined by the section_ `s`. If `I` is an
arbitrary index set, consider the direct sum sheaf `рқ’Ә_X^{(I)}`, and for `i вҲҲ I` let `h_i` be the canonical injection of
the `i`-th factor. The map `u вҶҰ (u вҲҳ h_i)` is an isomorphism of `Hom_{рқ’Ә_X}(рқ’Ә_X^{(I)}, в„ұ)` onto the product
`(Hom_{рқ’Ә_X}(рқ’Ә_X, в„ұ))^I`. There is thus a canonical bijection between homomorphisms `u : рқ’Ә_X^{(I)} вҶ’ в„ұ` and _families of
sections_ `(s_i)_{i вҲҲ I}` of `в„ұ` over `X`. The homomorphism `u` corresponding to `(s_i)` sends
`(a_i) вҲҲ (О“(U, рқ’Ә_X))^{(I)}` to `вҲ‘_{i вҲҲ I} a_i В· (s_i|U)`.

`в„ұ` is said to be _generated by the family_ `(s_i)` if the corresponding homomorphism `рқ’Ә_X^{(I)} вҶ’ в„ұ` is _surjective_ вҖ”
equivalently, if for every `x вҲҲ X`, `в„ұ_x` is the `рқ’Ә_x`-module generated by the `(s_i)_x`. `в„ұ` is _generated by its
sections over_ `X` if it is generated by the family of all such sections (or a subfamily); equivalently, if there is a
surjective homomorphism `рқ’Ә_X^{(I)} вҶ’ в„ұ` for some `I`.

Note that an `рқ’Ә_X`-Module `в„ұ` may be such that some point `x_0 вҲҲ X` has the property that `в„ұ|U` is not generated by its
sections over `U` for _any_ open neighborhood `U` of `x_0`: take `X = в„қ`, `рқ’Ә_X` the simple sheaf `в„Ө`, and `в„ұ` the
sub-sheaf with `в„ұ_0 = {0}` and `в„ұ_x = в„Ө` for `x вү  0`, and `x_0 = 0`. The only section of `в„ұ|U` over `U` is `0` for a
neighborhood `U` of `0`.

**(5.1.2)** Let `f : X вҶ’ Y` be a morphism of ringed spaces. If `в„ұ` is an `рқ’Ә_X`-Module generated by its sections over
`X`, then the canonical homomorphism `f*(f_*(в„ұ)) вҶ’ в„ұ` (4.4.3.3) is _surjective_: with the notation of (5.1.1), `s_i вҠ— 1`
is a section of `f*(f_*(в„ұ))` over `X`, and its image in `в„ұ` is `s_i`. The example of (5.1.1) with `f` the identity shows
that the converse fails in general.

**(5.1.3)** An `рқ’Ә_X`-Module `в„ұ` is said to be _quasi-coherent_ if for every `x вҲҲ X` there is an open neighborhood `U`
such that `в„ұ|U` is isomorphic to the _cokernel_ of a homomorphism `рқ’Ә_X^{(I)}|U вҶ’ рқ’Ә_X^{(J)}|U` for some index sets
`I, J`. Clearly `рқ’Ә_X` itself is quasi-coherent, and every direct sum of quasi-coherent `рқ’Ә_X`-Modules is quasi-coherent.
An `рқ’Ә_X`-Algebra `рқ’ң` is _quasi-coherent_ if it is quasi-coherent as an `рқ’Ә_X`-Module.

**(5.1.4)** Let `f : X вҶ’ Y` be a morphism of ringed spaces. If `рқ’ў` is a quasi-coherent `рқ’Ә_Y`-Module, then `f*(рқ’ў)` is a
quasi-coherent `рқ’Ә_X`-Module. Indeed, for every `x вҲҲ X` there is an open neighborhood `V` of `f(x)` in `Y` such that
`рқ’ў|V` is the cokernel of `рқ’Ә_Y^{(I)}|V вҶ’ рқ’Ә_Y^{(J)}|V`. Set `U = fвҒ»В№(V)` and let `f_U = f|U`; then `f*(рқ’ў)|U = f_U*(рқ’ў|V)`.
Since `f_U*` is right exact and commutes with direct sums, `f_U*(рқ’ў|V)` is the cokernel of `рқ’Ә_X^{(I)}|U вҶ’ рқ’Ә_X^{(J)}|U`.

### 5.2. Sheaves of finite type

<!-- label: 0.5.2 -->

**(5.2.1)** An `рқ’Ә_X`-Module `в„ұ` is said to be _of finite type_ if every `x вҲҲ X` admits an open neighborhood `U` such
that `в„ұ|U` is generated by _finitely many_ sections over `U`; equivalently, `в„ұ|U` is a quotient sheaf of `(рқ’Ә_X|U)^p` for
some finite `p`. Every quotient of an `рқ’Ә_X`-Module of finite type is of finite type; so is every finite direct sum and
every finite tensor product of `рқ’Ә_X`-Modules of finite type. An `рқ’Ә_X`-Module of finite type need not be quasi-coherent,
as one sees for `рқ’Ә_X / в„ұ` where `в„ұ` is the sheaf of (5.1.1). If `в„ұ` is of finite type, then `в„ұ_x` is an `рқ’Ә_x`-module of
finite type for every `x вҲҲ X`; the example of (5.1.1) shows that this necessary condition is not in general sufficient.

**(5.2.2)** Let `в„ұ` be an `рқ’Ә_X`-Module _of finite type_. If `s_i` (`1 вүӨ i вүӨ n`) are sections of `в„ұ` over an open
neighborhood `U` of a point `x вҲҲ X` and the `(s_i)_x` generate `в„ұ_x`, then there is an open neighborhood `V вҠӮ U` of `x`
such that the `(s_i)_y` generate `в„ұ_y` for every `y вҲҲ V` (FAC, I, В§2, no. 12, Prop. 1). In particular, the support of
`в„ұ` is _closed_.

Similarly, if `u : в„ұ вҶ’ рқ’ў` is a homomorphism with `u_x = 0`, there is a neighborhood `U` of `x` such that `u_y = 0` for
every `y вҲҲ U`.

**(5.2.3)** Suppose `X` is _quasi-compact_ and let `в„ұ`, `рқ’ў` be `рқ’Ә_X`-Modules with `рқ’ў` of _finite type_, `u : в„ұ вҶ’ рқ’ў` a
_surjective_ homomorphism. Suppose moreover that `в„ұ = limвғ— в„ұ_О»` is the inductive limit of a system of `рқ’Ә_X`-Modules.
Then there exists an index `Ој` such that the homomorphism `в„ұ_Ој вҶ’ рқ’ў` is _surjective_. Indeed, for every `x вҲҲ X` there is
a finite system of sections `s_i` of `рқ’ў` over an open neighborhood `U(x)` of `x` whose germs generate `рқ’ў_y` for every
`y вҲҲ U(x)`; there is also an open `V(x) вҠӮ U(x)` and sections `t_i` of `в„ұ` over `V(x)` with `s_i|V(x) = u(t_i)`. We may
assume the `t_i` are canonical images of sections of some `в„ұ_{О»(x)}` over `V(x)`. Cover `X` by finitely many `V(x_k)`
and let `Ој` exceed all the `О»(x_k)`; plainly `Ој` answers the question.

Still assuming `X` quasi-compact, if `в„ұ` is of finite type and generated by its sections over `X` (5.1.1), then `в„ұ` is
generated by a _finite_ subfamily of those sections: cover `X` by finitely many open `U_k` such that for each `k` there
are finitely many sections `s_{ik}` of `в„ұ` over `X` whose restrictions to `U_k` generate `в„ұ|U_k`; plainly the `s_{ik}`
then generate `в„ұ`.

**(5.2.4)** Let `f : X вҶ’ Y` be a morphism of ringed spaces. If `рқ’ў` is an `рқ’Ә_Y`-Module of finite type, then `f*(рқ’ў)` is an
`рқ’Ә_X`-Module of finite type. Indeed, for every `x вҲҲ X` there is an open `V` containing `f(x)` and a surjective
homomorphism `v : рқ’Ә_Y^p|V вҶ’ рқ’ў|V`. Set `U = fвҒ»В№(V)` and `f_U = f|U`; then `f*(рқ’ў)|U = f_U*(рқ’ў|V)`. Since `f_U*` is right
exact (4.3.1) and commutes with direct sums, `f_U*(v)` is a surjective homomorphism `рқ’Ә_X^p|U вҶ’ f*(рқ’ў)|U`.

**(5.2.5)** An `рқ’Ә_X`-Module `в„ұ` _admits a finite presentation_ if for every `x вҲҲ X` there is an open neighborhood `U`
such that `в„ұ|U` is isomorphic to the cokernel of a `(рқ’Ә_X|U)`-homomorphism `рқ’Ә_X^p|U вҶ’ рқ’Ә_X^q|U` with `p, q > 0` finite.
Such an `рқ’Ә_X`-Module is of finite type and quasi-coherent. If `f : X вҶ’ Y` is a morphism of ringed spaces and `рқ’ў` admits
a finite presentation, so does `f*(рқ’ў)`, by the argument of (5.1.4).

**(5.2.6)** Let `в„ұ` be an `рқ’Ә_X`-Module admitting a finite presentation (5.2.5). Then for every `рқ’Ә_X`-Module `в„Ӣ`, the
canonical functorial homomorphism

```
(в„Ӣom_{рқ’Ә_X}(в„ұ, в„Ӣ))_x вҶ’ Hom_{рқ’Ә_x}(в„ұ_x, в„Ӣ_x)
```

is _bijective_ (T, 4.1.1).

**(5.2.7)** Let `в„ұ`, `рқ’ў` be `рқ’Ә_X`-Modules admitting finite presentations. If for some `x вҲҲ X` the `рқ’Ә_x`-modules `в„ұ_x`
and `рқ’ў_x` are _isomorphic_, then there is an open neighborhood `U` of `x` such that `в„ұ|U` and `рқ’ў|U` are _isomorphic_.
Indeed, if `ПҶ : в„ұ_x вҶ’ рқ’ў_x` and `ПҲ : рқ’ў_x вҶ’ в„ұ_x` are inverse isomorphisms, then by (5.2.6) there is an open `V вҲӢ x` and
sections `u` (resp. `v`) of `в„Ӣom_{рқ’Ә_X}(в„ұ, рқ’ў)` (resp. `в„Ӣom_{рқ’Ә_X}(рқ’ў, в„ұ)`) over `V` with `u_x = ПҶ` (resp. `v_x = ПҲ`). Since
`(u вҲҳ v)_x` and `(v вҲҳ u)_x` are the identity automorphisms, there is an open `U вҠӮ V` containing `x` on which `(u вҲҳ v)|U`
and `(v вҲҳ u)|U` are identities, whence the assertion.

### 5.3. Coherent sheaves

<!-- label: 0.5.3 -->

**(5.3.1)** An `рқ’Ә_X`-Module `в„ұ` is _coherent_ if:

> a) `в„ұ` is of finite type; b) For every open `U вҠӮ X`, every integer `n > 0`, and every homomorphism
> `u : рқ’Ә_X^n|U вҶ’ в„ұ|U`, the kernel of `u` is of finite type.

Both conditions are _local_.

For most of the proofs of the properties of coherent sheaves recalled below, see (FAC), I, В§2.

**(5.3.2)** Every coherent `рқ’Ә_X`-Module admits a finite presentation (5.2.5); the converse is not in general true, since
`рқ’Ә_X` itself need not be coherent.

Every sub-`рқ’Ә_X`-Module _of finite type_ of a coherent `рқ’Ә_X`-Module is coherent; every _finite_ direct sum of coherent
`рқ’Ә_X`-Modules is coherent.

**(5.3.3)** In an exact sequence `0 вҶ’ в„ұ вҶ’ рқ’ў вҶ’ в„Ӣ вҶ’ 0` of `рқ’Ә_X`-Modules, if two are coherent then so is the third.

**(5.3.4)** If `в„ұ` and `рқ’ў` are coherent `рқ’Ә_X`-Modules and `u : в„ұ вҶ’ рқ’ў` is a homomorphism, then `Im(u)`, `Ker(u)`, and
`Coker(u)` are coherent. In particular, sub-`рқ’Ә_X`-Modules `в„ұ`, `рқ’ў` of a coherent `рқ’Ә_X`-Module have `в„ұ + рқ’ў` and `в„ұ вҲ© рқ’ў`
coherent.

**(5.3.5)** If `в„ұ` and `рқ’ў` are coherent `рқ’Ә_X`-Modules, so are `в„ұ вҠ—_{рқ’Ә_X} рқ’ў` and `в„Ӣom_{рқ’Ә_X}(в„ұ, рқ’ў)`.

**(5.3.6)** Let `в„ұ` be coherent and `рқ’Ҙ` a coherent sheaf of ideals of `рқ’Ә_X`. Then `рқ’Ҙв„ұ` is coherent, being the image of
`рқ’Ҙ вҠ—_{рқ’Ә_X} в„ұ` under the canonical map `рқ’Ҙ вҠ—_{рқ’Ә_X} в„ұ вҶ’ в„ұ` (5.3.4 and 5.3.5).

**(5.3.7)** An `рқ’Ә_X`-Algebra `рқ’ң` is _coherent_ if it is a coherent `рқ’Ә_X`-Module. In particular, `рқ’Ә_X` is a _coherent
sheaf of rings_ if and only if, for every open `U вҠӮ X` and every `u : рқ’Ә_X^n|U вҶ’ рқ’Ә_X|U`, the kernel of `u` is a
`(рқ’Ә_X|U)`-Module of finite type.

If `рқ’Ә_X` is a coherent sheaf of rings, every `рқ’Ә_X`-Module `в„ұ` admitting a finite presentation (5.2.5) is coherent, by
(5.3.4).

The _annihilator_ of an `рқ’Ә_X`-Module `в„ұ` is the kernel `рқ’Ҙ` of the canonical homomorphism `рқ’Ә_X вҶ’ в„Ӣom_{рқ’Ә_X}(в„ұ, в„ұ)` sending
`s вҲҲ О“(U, рқ’Ә_X)` to multiplication by `s` in `Hom(в„ұ|U, в„ұ|U)`. If `рқ’Ә_X` is coherent and `в„ұ` coherent, then `рқ’Ҙ` is coherent
(5.3.4 and 5.3.5), and `рқ’Ҙ_x` is the annihilator of `в„ұ_x` for every `x вҲҲ X` (5.2.6).

**(5.3.8)** Suppose `рқ’Ә_X` is coherent. Let `в„ұ` be a coherent `рқ’Ә_X`-Module, `x вҲҲ X`, and `M` a finite-type submodule of
`в„ұ_x`. There is an open neighborhood `U` of `x` and a coherent sub-`(рқ’Ә_X|U)`-Module `рқ’ў` of `в„ұ|U` with `рқ’ў_x = M` (T, 4.1,
Lemma 1).

This, together with the properties of sub-`рқ’Ә_X`-Modules of a coherent `рқ’Ә_X`-Module, imposes necessary conditions on the
rings `рқ’Ә_x` for `рқ’Ә_X` to be coherent. For example (5.3.4), the intersection of two finite-type ideals of `рқ’Ә_x` must
still be a finite-type ideal.

**(5.3.9)** Suppose `рқ’Ә_X` is coherent, and let `M` be an `рқ’Ә_x`-module admitting a finite presentation, so the cokernel
of some `ПҶ : рқ’Ә_x^p вҶ’ рқ’Ә_x^q`. There is an open neighborhood `U` of `x` and a coherent `(рқ’Ә_X|U)`-Module `в„ұ` with
`в„ұ_x вү… M`. Indeed, by (5.2.6) there is a section `u` of `в„Ӣom_{рқ’Ә_X}(рқ’Ә_X^p, рқ’Ә_X^q)` over some `U` with `u_x = ПҶ`; the
cokernel of `u : рқ’Ә_X^p|U вҶ’ рқ’Ә_X^q|U` answers the question (5.3.4).

**(5.3.10)** Suppose `рқ’Ә_X` is coherent and `рқ’Ҙ` is a coherent sheaf of ideals of `рқ’Ә_X`. For an `(рқ’Ә_X / рқ’Ҙ)`-Module `в„ұ` to
be coherent, it is necessary and sufficient that it be coherent as an `рқ’Ә_X`-Module. In particular `рқ’Ә_X / рқ’Ҙ` is a
coherent sheaf of rings.

**(5.3.11)** Let `f : X вҶ’ Y` be a morphism of ringed spaces, and suppose `рқ’Ә_X` is coherent. Then for every coherent
`рқ’Ә_Y`-Module `рқ’ў`, `f*(рқ’ў)` is coherent. Indeed, with the notation of (5.2.4), one may assume `рқ’ў|V` is the cokernel of
`v : рқ’Ә_Y^q|V вҶ’ рқ’Ә_Y^p|V`. Since `f_U*` is right exact, `f*(рқ’ў)|U = f_U*(рқ’ў|V)` is the cokernel of
`f_U*(v) : рқ’Ә_X^q|U вҶ’ рқ’Ә_X^p|U`.

**(5.3.12)** Let `Y вҠӮ X` be a closed subset, `j : Y вҶ’ X` the canonical injection, `рқ’Ә_Y` a sheaf of rings on `Y`, and
`рқ’Ә_X = j_*(рқ’Ә_Y)`. An `рқ’Ә_Y`-Module `рқ’ў` is of finite type (resp. quasi-coherent, coherent) if and only if `j_*(рқ’ў)` is of
finite type (resp. quasi-coherent, coherent) as an `рқ’Ә_X`-Module.

### 5.4. Locally free sheaves

<!-- label: 0.5.4 -->

**(5.4.1)** Let `X` be a ringed space. An `рқ’Ә_X`-Module `в„ұ` is _locally free_ if for every `x вҲҲ X` there is an open
neighborhood `U` such that `в„ұ|U` is isomorphic to `рқ’Ә_X^{(I)}|U` for some `I` (which may depend on `U`). If `I` may
always be taken finite, `в„ұ` is _of finite rank_; if `I` may always be taken finite with the same number of elements `n`,
`в„ұ` is _of rank_ `n`. A locally free `рқ’Ә_X`-Module of rank 1 is also called _invertible_ (cf. (5.4.3)). If `в„ұ` is locally
free of finite rank, then for every `x вҲҲ X`, `в„ұ_x` is a free `рқ’Ә_x`-module of finite rank `n(x)`, and there is a
neighborhood `U` of `x` on which `в„ұ|U` has rank `n(x)`; if `X` is connected, `n(x)` is _constant_.

Every locally free sheaf is quasi-coherent; if `рқ’Ә_X` is a coherent sheaf of rings, every locally free `рқ’Ә_X`-Module of
finite rank is coherent.

If `в„’` is locally free, then `в„’ вҠ—_{рқ’Ә_X} в„ұ` is _exact_ in `в„ұ` on `рқ’Ә_X`-Modules.

We shall mostly consider locally free `рқ’Ә_X`-Modules of finite rank; when we speak of locally free sheaves without
qualification, _of finite rank_ is to be understood.

**(5.4.2)** If `в„’`, `в„ұ` are two `рқ’Ә_X`-Modules, there is a canonical functorial homomorphism

```
(5.4.2.1)    в„’МҢ вҠ—_{рқ’Ә_X} в„ұ = в„Ӣom_{рқ’Ә_X}(в„’, рқ’Ә_X) вҠ—_{рқ’Ә_X} в„ұ вҶ’ в„Ӣom_{рқ’Ә_X}(в„’, в„ұ)
```

defined as follows: for `U` open and `(u, t)` with `u вҲҲ О“(U, в„Ӣom_{рқ’Ә_X}(в„’, рқ’Ә_X)) = Hom(в„’|U, рқ’Ә_X|U)` and `t вҲҲ О“(U, в„ұ)`,
send `(u, t)` to the element of `Hom(в„’|U, в„ұ|U)` carrying `s_x вҲҲ в„’_x` to `u_x(s_x) В· t_x вҲҲ в„ұ_x`. If `в„’` is locally free
of finite rank, this map is _bijective_: the question being local, reduce to `в„’ = рқ’Ә_X^n`; since
`в„Ӣom_{рқ’Ә_X}(рқ’Ә_X^n, рқ’ў) вү… рқ’ў^n` canonically for every `рқ’Ә_X`-Module `рқ’ў`, the assertion reduces to `в„’ = рқ’Ә_X`, which is
immediate.

**(5.4.3)** If `в„’` is invertible, so is its dual `в„’МҢ = в„Ӣom_{рқ’Ә_X}(в„’, рқ’Ә_X)`, as one sees locally by reducing to `в„’ = рқ’Ә_X`.
Moreover, there is a canonical isomorphism

```
(5.4.3.1)    в„Ӣom_{рқ’Ә_X}(в„’, рқ’Ә_X) вҠ—_{рқ’Ә_X} в„’ вҘІ рқ’Ә_X.
```

Indeed, by (5.4.2) it suffices to define a canonical isomorphism `в„Ӣom_{рқ’Ә_X}(в„’, в„’) вҘІ рқ’Ә_X`. For _any_ `рқ’Ә_X`-Module `в„ұ`
there is a canonical homomorphism `рқ’Ә_X вҶ’ в„Ӣom_{рқ’Ә_X}(в„ұ, в„ұ)` (5.3.7); when `в„ұ = в„’` is invertible, this homomorphism is
bijective вҖ” locally the question reduces to `в„’ = рқ’Ә_X`, which is immediate.

Accordingly, write `в„’вҒ»В№ = в„Ӣom_{рқ’Ә_X}(в„’, рқ’Ә_X)`, called the _inverse_ of `в„’`. The terminology "invertible sheaf" is
motivated as follows for `X` a single point and `рқ’Ә_X` a _local_ ring `A` with maximal ideal `рқ”Ә`. If `M`, `MвҖІ` are
`A`-modules with `M` of finite type and `M вҠ—_A MвҖІ вү… A`, then `(A/рқ”Ә) вҠ—_A (M вҠ—_A MвҖІ) вү… (M/рқ”ӘM) вҠ—_{A/рқ”Ә} (MвҖІ/рқ”ӘMвҖІ)`; this
tensor product of `A/рқ”Ә`-vector spaces is `вү… A/рқ”Ә`, forcing `M/рқ”ӘM` and `MвҖІ/рқ”ӘMвҖІ` to be 1-dimensional. For any `z вҲҲ M вҲ– рқ”ӘM`,
`M = Az + рқ”ӘM`, so `M = Az` by Nakayama (since `M` is of finite type). The annihilator of `z` annihilates `M вҠ—_A MвҖІ вү… A`,
hence is `(0)`; so `M вү… A`. In general, this shows: if `в„’` is an `рқ’Ә_X`-Module of finite type with some `рқ’Ә_X`-Module `в„ұ`
satisfying `в„’ вҠ—_{рқ’Ә_X} в„ұ вү… рқ’Ә_X`, and the rings `рқ’Ә_x` are local, then `в„’_x вү… рқ’Ә_x` for every `x вҲҲ X`. If `рқ’Ә_X` and `в„’` are
coherent, one concludes that `в„’` is invertible by (5.2.7).

**(5.4.4)** If `в„’` and `в„’вҖІ` are invertible `рқ’Ә_X`-Modules, so is `в„’ вҠ—_{рқ’Ә_X} в„’вҖІ` (reducing locally to `в„’ = в„’вҖІ = рқ’Ә_X`). For
`n вүҘ 1`, write `в„’^{вҠ—n}` for the tensor product of `n` copies of `в„’`; set `в„’^{вҠ—0} = рқ’Ә_X` and `в„’^{вҠ—(вҲ’n)} = (в„’вҒ»В№)^{вҠ—n}`.
There is a canonical functorial isomorphism

```
(5.4.4.1)    в„’^{вҠ—m} вҠ—_{рқ’Ә_X} в„’^{вҠ—n} вҘІ в„’^{вҠ—(n+m)}
```

for any integers `m, n`: by the definitions one reduces to `m = вҲ’1, n = 1`, the case treated in (5.4.3).

**(5.4.5)** Let `f : Y вҶ’ X` be a morphism of ringed spaces. If `в„’` is a locally free (resp. invertible) `рқ’Ә_X`-Module,
then `f*(в„’)` is a locally free (resp. invertible) `рқ’Ә_Y`-Module вҖ” using that inverse images of locally isomorphic sheaves
are locally isomorphic, that `f*` commutes with finite direct sums, and that `f*(рқ’Ә_X) = рқ’Ә_Y` (4.3.4). There is a
canonical functorial homomorphism `f*(в„’МҢ) вҶ’ (f*(в„’))МҢ` (4.4.6) which is _bijective_ when `в„’` is locally free (reducing
again to `в„’ = рқ’Ә_X`). Consequently, for `в„’` invertible, `f*(в„’^{вҠ—n})` is canonically identified with `(f*(в„’))^{вҠ—n}` for
every integer `n`.

**(5.4.6)** Let `в„’` be an invertible `рқ’Ә_X`-Module. Write `О“_*(X, в„’)` (or simply `О“_*(в„’)`) for the abelian group
`вҠ•_{n вҲҲ в„Ө} О“(X, в„’^{вҠ—n})`, made a _graded ring_ by the multiplication sending `(s_n, s_m) вҲҲ О“(X, в„’^{вҠ—n}) Г— О“(X, в„’^{вҠ—m})`
to the section of `в„’^{вҠ—(n+m)}` corresponding canonically (5.4.4.1) to `s_n вҠ— s_m`. Associativity is immediate. Plainly
`О“_*(X, в„’)` is a covariant functor in `в„’` with values in graded rings.

For any `рқ’Ә_X`-Module `в„ұ`, set

```
О“_*(в„’, в„ұ) = вҠ•_{n вҲҲ в„Ө} О“(X, в„ұ вҠ—_{рқ’Ә_X} в„’^{вҠ—n}),
```

made a _graded module_ over `О“_*(в„’)` by sending `(s_n, u_m) вҲҲ О“(X, в„’^{вҠ—n}) Г— О“(X, в„ұ вҠ— в„’^{вҠ—m})` to the section of
`в„ұ вҠ— в„’^{вҠ—(n+m)}` corresponding canonically (5.4.4.1) to `s_n вҠ— u_m`. The module axioms hold by inspection. For `X, в„’`
fixed, `О“_*(в„’, в„ұ)` is a covariant functor in `в„ұ` with values in graded `О“_*(в„’)`-modules; for `X, в„ұ` fixed, a covariant
functor in `в„’`.

If `f : Y вҶ’ X` is a morphism of ringed spaces, the canonical homomorphism (4.4.3.2) `ПҒ : в„’^{вҠ—n} вҶ’ f_*(f*(в„’^{вҠ—n}))`
defines `О“(X, в„’^{вҠ—n}) вҶ’ О“(Y, f*(в„’^{вҠ—n}))`; since `f*(в„’^{вҠ—n}) = (f*(в„’))^{вҠ—n}`, the canonical homomorphisms (4.4.3.2) and
(5.4.4.1) yield a _functorial homomorphism of graded rings_ `О“_*(в„’) вҶ’ О“_*(f*(в„’))`. The same canonical homomorphism
(4.4.3) similarly yields `О“(X, в„ұ вҠ— в„’^{вҠ—n}) вҶ’ О“(Y, f*(в„ұ вҠ— в„’^{вҠ—n}))`; using

```
f*(в„ұ вҠ— в„’^{вҠ—n}) = f*(в„ұ) вҠ— (f*(в„’))^{вҠ—n}    (4.3.3.1),
```

these (for varying `n`) define a _di-homomorphism of graded modules_ `О“_*(в„’, в„ұ) вҶ’ О“_*(f*(в„’), f*(в„ұ))`.

**(5.4.7)** One can show that there is a _set_ `рқ”җ` (also written `рқ”җ(X)`) of invertible `рқ’Ә_X`-Modules such that every
invertible `рқ’Ә_X`-Module is isomorphic to a unique element of `рқ”җ`.[^5-1] Endow `рқ”җ` with the composition law sending
`(в„’, в„’вҖІ)` to the unique element of `рқ”җ` isomorphic to `в„’ вҠ— в„’вҖІ`. With this law, `рқ”җ` is a _group_ isomorphic to the
cohomology group `HВ№(X, рқ’Ә_X^*)`, where `рқ’Ә_X^*` is the subsheaf of `рқ’Ә_X` whose sections over `U` are the invertible
elements of `О“(U, рқ’Ә_X)` (so `рқ’Ә_X^*` is a sheaf of _multiplicative_ abelian groups).

For this, note that for every open `U вҠӮ X`, `О“(U, рқ’Ә_X^*)` is canonically identified with the _automorphism group_ of the
`(рқ’Ә_X|U)`-Module `рқ’Ә_X|U`: a section `Оө` corresponds to the automorphism `u` with `u_x(s_x) = Оө_x В· s_x`. Given an open
cover `рқ”ҳ = (U_О»)` of `X`, giving an automorphism `Оё_{О»Ој}` of `рқ’Ә_X|(U_О» вҲ© U_Ој)` for each pair amounts to giving a
1-_cochain_ on `рқ”ҳ` with values in `рқ’Ә_X^*`; the gluing condition (3.3.1) for the `Оё_{О»Ој}` says exactly that the cochain
is a _cocycle_. Likewise, giving an automorphism `Пү_О»` of `рқ’Ә_X|U_О»` for each `О»` is a 0-cochain, and its _coboundary_ is
the family `(Пү_О»|U_О» вҲ© U_Ој) вҲҳ (Пү_Ој|U_О» вҲ© U_Ој)вҒ»В№`. To each 1-cocycle of `рқ”ҳ` we associate the element of `рқ”җ` isomorphic to
the invertible `рқ’Ә_X`-Module obtained by gluing via the `(Оё_{О»Ој})`; cohomologous cocycles yield the same element of `рқ”җ`
(3.3.2). This defines `ПҶ_рқ”ҳ : HВ№(рқ”ҳ, рқ’Ә_X^*) вҶ’ рқ”җ`. If `рқ”ҷ` refines `рқ”ҳ`, the diagram

```
HВ№(рқ”ҳ, рқ’Ә_X^*) в”Җв”ҖПҶ_рқ”ҳв”Җв”ҖвҶ’ рқ”җ
     в”Ӯ              вҶ—
     вҶ“           ПҶ_рқ”ҷ
HВ№(рқ”ҷ, рқ’Ә_X^*)
```

(left arrow the canonical homomorphism (G, II, 5.7)) commutes, by (3.3.3). Passage to the inductive limit gives
`HВ№(X, рқ’Ә_X^*) вҶ’ рқ”җ`, since ДҢech `ИһВ№` agrees with derived `HВ№` here (G, II, 5.9, Cor. of Th. 5.9.1). This map is
_surjective_: every invertible `в„’` is obtained by gluing copies of `рқ’Ә_X|U_О»` for some cover. It is _injective_: this
reduces to injectivity of each `ПҶ_рқ”ҳ`, which is (3.3.2). To see it is a group homomorphism: for invertible `в„’, в„’вҖІ`,
choose a cover `(U_О»)` and `a_О» вҲҲ О“(U_О», в„’)`, `aвҖІ_О» вҲҲ О“(U_О», в„’вҖІ)` with `О“(U_О», в„’) = О“(U_О», рқ’Ә_X) В· a_О»`,
`О“(U_О», в„’вҖІ) = О“(U_О», рқ’Ә_X) В· aвҖІ_О»`. The corresponding cocycles `(Оө_{О»Ој})`, `(ОөвҖІ_{О»Ој})` satisfy `s_О» В· a_О» = s_Ој В· a_Ој`
iff `s_О» = Оө_{О»Ој} s_Ој` (similarly for `в„’вҖІ`). Sections of `в„’ вҠ—_{рқ’Ә_X} в„’вҖІ` over `U_О»` are finite sums of
`s_О» sвҖІ_О» В· (a_О» вҠ— aвҖІ_О»)`; hence `(Оө_{О»Ој} ОөвҖІ_{О»Ој})` is the cocycle of `в„’ вҠ—_{рқ’Ә_X} в„’вҖІ`, completing the proof.[^5-2]

**(5.4.8)** Let `f = (ПҲ, Пү) : Y вҶ’ X` be a morphism of ringed spaces. The functor `f*(в„’)` on invertible `рқ’Ә_X`-Modules
defines (by abuse of language, still written `f*`) a map `рқ”җ(X) вҶ’ рқ”җ(Y)`. There is also a canonical homomorphism (T,
3.2.2)

```
(5.4.8.1)    HВ№(X, рқ’Ә_X^*) вҶ’ HВ№(Y, рқ’Ә_Y^*).
```

Under the canonical identifications (5.4.7) `рқ”җ(X) вү… HВ№(X, рқ’Ә_X^*)` (resp. `рқ”җ(Y) вү… HВ№(Y, рқ’Ә_Y^*)`), the homomorphism
(5.4.8.1) _is identified with_ `f*`. Indeed, if `в„’` comes from a cocycle `(Оө_{О»Ој})` for a cover `(U_О»)` of `X`, it
suffices to show `f*(в„’)` comes from a cocycle whose cohomology class is the image under (5.4.8.1) of that of `(Оө_{О»Ој})`.
If `Оё_{О»Ој}` is the automorphism corresponding to `Оө_{О»Ој}`, then `f*(в„’)` is obtained by gluing the `рқ’Ә_Y|ПҲвҒ»В№(U_О»)` via the
`f*(Оё_{О»Ој})`; one checks these correspond to the cocycle `(Пү^вҷҜ(Оө_{О»Ој}))` (after identifying `Оө_{О»Ој}` with its canonical
image under `ПҒ` (3.7.2), a section of `ПҲ*(рқ’Ә_X^*)` over `ПҲвҒ»В№(U_О» вҲ© U_Ој)`).

**(5.4.9)** Let `в„°`, `в„ұ` be `рқ’Ә_X`-Modules with `в„ұ` _locally free_, and let `рқ’ў` be an _extension of_ `в„ұ` _by_ `в„°` вҖ” an
exact sequence `0 вҶ’ в„° в”Җв”Җjв”Җв”ҖвҶ’ рқ’ў в”Җв”Җpв”Җв”ҖвҶ’ в„ұ вҶ’ 0`. Then for every `x вҲҲ X` there is an open `U вҲӢ x` such that
`рқ’ў|U вү… (в„°|U) вҠ• (в„ұ|U)`. One reduces to `в„ұ = рқ’Ә_X^n`; let `e_i` (`1 вүӨ i вүӨ n`) be the canonical sections of `рқ’Ә_X^n` (5.5.5).
There is an open `U` and sections `s_i вҲҲ О“(U, рқ’ў)` with `p(s_i|U) = e_i|U` (`1 вүӨ i вүӨ n`). Let `f : в„ұ|U вҶ’ рқ’ў|U` be the
homomorphism defined by the `s_i|U` (5.1.1). For open `V вҠӮ U` and `s вҲҲ О“(V, рқ’ў)`, `s вҲ’ f(p(s)) вҲҲ О“(V, в„°)`, whence the
assertion.

**(5.4.10)** Let `f : X вҶ’ Y` be a morphism of ringed spaces, `в„ұ` an `рқ’Ә_X`-Module, and `в„’` a locally free `рқ’Ә_Y`-Module of
finite rank. There is a canonical isomorphism

```
(5.4.10.1)    f_*(в„ұ) вҠ—_{рқ’Ә_Y} в„’ вҘІ f_*(в„ұ вҠ—_{рқ’Ә_X} f*(в„’)).
```

Indeed, for any `рқ’Ә_Y`-Module `в„’` there is a canonical homomorphism

```
f_*(в„ұ) вҠ—_{рқ’Ә_Y} в„’ в”Җв”Җ1вҠ—ПҒв”Җв”ҖвҶ’ f_*(в„ұ) вҠ—_{рқ’Ә_Y} f_*(f*(в„’)) в”Җв”ҖОұв”Җв”ҖвҶ’ f_*(в„ұ вҠ—_{рқ’Ә_X} f*(в„’))
```

(`ПҒ` from (4.4.3.2), `Оұ` from (4.2.2.1)). To see this is an isomorphism when `в„’` is locally free, the question being
local on `Y`, reduce to `в„’ = рқ’Ә_Y^n`; since `f_*` and `f*` commute with finite direct sums, reduce to `n = 1`, where the
assertion follows immediately from the definitions and `f*(рқ’Ә_Y) = рқ’Ә_X`.

### 5.5. Sheaves on a space ringed in local rings

<!-- label: 0.5.5 -->

**(5.5.1)** A ringed space `(X, рқ’Ә_X)` is _ringed in local rings_ if for every `x вҲҲ X`, `рқ’Ә_x` is a local ring; these are
by far the most common ringed spaces we shall encounter. We then write `рқ”Ә_x` for the maximal ideal of `рқ’Ә_x` and `рқ‘ҳ(x)`
for the residue field `рқ’Ә_x / рқ”Ә_x`. For an `рқ’Ә_X`-Module `в„ұ`, an open `U вҠӮ X`, a point `x вҲҲ U`, and a section
`f вҲҲ О“(U, в„ұ)`, we write `f(x)` for the class of the germ `f_x вҲҲ в„ұ_x` mod `рқ”Ә_x в„ұ_x`, and call it the _value of_ `f` _at_
`x`. The relation `f(x) = 0` thus means `f_x вҲҲ рқ”Ә_x в„ұ_x`; when it holds, we say (by abuse of language) that `f` _vanishes
at_ `x`. Do not confuse this with `f_x = 0`.

**(5.5.2)** Let `X` be a space ringed in local rings, `в„’` an invertible `рқ’Ә_X`-Module, and `f` a section of `в„’` over `X`.
At a point `x вҲҲ X`, the following are equivalent:

> a) `f_x` is a generator of `в„’_x`; b) `f_x вҲү рқ”Ә_x в„’_x` (i.e., `f(x) вү  0`); c) There is a section `g` of `в„’вҒ»В№` over some
> open `V вҲӢ x` such that the canonical image of `f вҠ— g` in `О“(V, рқ’Ә_X)` (5.4.3) is the unit section.

Indeed, the question being local, reduce to `в„’ = рқ’Ә_X`. Equivalence of a) and b) is then immediate, and c) вҹ№ b). For b) вҹ№
c): if `f_x вҲү рқ”Ә_x`, `f_x` is invertible in `рқ’Ә_x`, say `f_x В· g_x = 1_x`; by the definition of germs, there is a
neighborhood `V вҲӢ x` and a section `g` over `V` with `fg = 1` on `V`.

By c), the set `X_f` of `x` satisfying the equivalent conditions a)вҖ“c) is _open_ in `X`; in the terminology of (5.5.1),
it is the set of `x` _where_ `f` _does not vanish_.

**(5.5.3)** Under the hypotheses of (5.5.2), let `в„’вҖІ` be a second invertible `рқ’Ә_X`-Module. For `f вҲҲ О“(X, в„’)`,
`g вҲҲ О“(X, в„’вҖІ)`,

```
X_f вҲ© X_g = X_{f вҠ— g}.
```

Reduce locally to `в„’ = в„’вҖІ = рқ’Ә_X`; then `f вҠ— g` is canonically identified with the product `fg`, and the assertion is
obvious.

**(5.5.4)** Let `в„ұ` be a locally free `рқ’Ә_X`-Module of rank `n`. Then `вӢҖ^p в„ұ` is locally free of rank `(n choose p)` if
`p вүӨ n`, and zero if `p > n` вҖ” the question being local, reduce to `в„ұ = рқ’Ә_X^n`. For every `x вҲҲ X`,
`(вӢҖ^p в„ұ)_x / рқ”Ә_x (вӢҖ^p в„ұ)_x` is a `рқ‘ҳ(x)`-vector space of dimension `(n choose p)`, canonically identified with
`вӢҖ^p (в„ұ_x / рқ”Ә_x в„ұ_x)`. For sections `s_1, вҖҰ, s_p` of `в„ұ` over an open `U`, set `s = s_1 вҲ§ вҖҰ вҲ§ s_p вҲҲ О“(U, вӢҖ^p в„ұ)`
(4.1.5); then `s(x) = s_1(x) вҲ§ вҖҰ вҲ§ s_p(x)`, so `s_1(x), вҖҰ, s_p(x)` are _linearly dependent_ iff `s(x) = 0`. Hence _the
set of_ `x вҲҲ X` _at which_ `s_1(x), вҖҰ, s_p(x)` _are linearly independent is open in_ `X`: reducing to `в„ұ = рқ’Ә_X^n` and
applying (5.5.2) to the image of `s` under a projection of `вӢҖ^p в„ұ = рқ’Ә_X^{(n choose p)}` onto one of its `(n choose p)`
factors.

In particular, if `s_1, вҖҰ, s_n` are `n` sections of `в„ұ` over `U` with `s_1(x), вҖҰ, s_n(x)` linearly independent at every
`x вҲҲ U`, then the homomorphism `u : рқ’Ә_X^n|U вҶ’ в„ұ|U` defined by the `s_i` (5.1.1) is an _isomorphism_: reduce to
`в„ұ = рқ’Ә_X^n` and identify `вӢҖ^n в„ұ` with `рқ’Ә_X`; then `s = s_1 вҲ§ вҖҰ вҲ§ s_n` is an invertible section of `рқ’Ә_X` over `U`, and an
inverse to `u` is given by Cramer's rule.

**(5.5.5)** Let `в„°`, `в„ұ` be locally free `рқ’Ә_X`-Modules (of finite rank), and let `u : в„° вҶ’ в„ұ` be a homomorphism. For
there to exist an open `U вҲӢ x` such that `u|U` is _injective_ and `в„ұ|U` is the _direct sum of_ `u(в„°)|U` _and a locally
free sub-`(рқ’Ә_X|U)`-Module_, it is necessary and sufficient that `u_x : в„°_x вҶ’ в„ұ_x` induce an _injective_ `рқ‘ҳ(x)`-linear
map `в„°_x / рқ”Ә_x в„°_x вҶ’ в„ұ_x / рқ”Ә_x в„ұ_x`. The condition is _necessary_: `в„ұ_x` is then the direct sum of the free
`рқ’Ә_x`-modules `u_x(в„°_x)` and `рқ’ў_x`, so `в„ұ_x / рқ”Ә_x в„ұ_x` is the direct sum of `u_x(в„°_x) / рқ”Ә_x u_x(в„°_x)` and
`рқ’ў_x / рқ”Ә_x рқ’ў_x`. The condition is _sufficient_: reduce to `в„° = рқ’Ә_X^m`; let `s_1, вҖҰ, s_m` be the images by `u` of the
canonical sections `e_i` of `рқ’Ә_X^m` (the `e_i` are the sections with `(e_i)_y` equal to the `i`-th element of the
canonical basis of `рқ’Ә_y^m` for `y вҲҲ X` вҖ” _canonical sections_ of `рқ’Ә_X^m`). By hypothesis `s_1(x), вҖҰ, s_m(x)` are
linearly independent, so if `в„ұ` has rank `n`, there are `n вҲ’ m` sections `s_{m+1}, вҖҰ, s_n` of `в„ұ` over some neighborhood
`V` of `x` with `s_i(x)` (`1 вүӨ i вүӨ n`) a basis of `в„ұ_x / рқ”Ә_x в„ұ_x`. By (5.5.4) there is an open `U вҠӮ V` with the `s_i(y)`
(`1 вүӨ i вүӨ n`) a basis of `в„ұ_y / рқ”Ә_y в„ұ_y` for every `y вҲҲ U`. By (5.5.4), there is an isomorphism `в„ұ|U вҘІ рқ’Ә_X^n|U` sending
`s_i|U` (`1 вүӨ i вүӨ m`) to `e_i|U`, finishing the proof.
